# Cherry Training Systems

Sweet cherry's natural vigor and apical dominance demand active training — without it, the tree forms one tall whip with sparse, narrow-angled scaffolds. PNW 667 (Long, Lang, Musacchi, Whiting 2015) catalogs eight systems; this article summarizes the four most relevant to home orchards.

## Modified Central Leader (default home-orchard pick)

A single dominant leader trunk with 4-6 well-spaced scaffold branches at wide angles, plus tertiary fruiting wood.

**Establishment**:
- At planting: head nursery whip to 30-36" (knee-high to thigh-high).
- Year 1 summer: select 4-6 scaffolds spaced 6-12" apart vertically and ~90° apart in the cardinal directions. Use limb spreaders or toothpicks to set wide angles (50-70° from trunk).
- Year 2-3: head the leader annually to force lateral scaffolds at the next tier.
- Year 4+: stop heading the leader; let it cap. Maintain.

**Mature pruning**:
- Annual late-July post-harvest pruning (per Cornell research).
- Remove water sprouts, downward-bent limbs, crossing branches.
- Renewal cuts on 5-7 year old fruiting wood, ~20% canopy/yr.

**Pros**: familiar, simple, well-suited to standard or semi-dwarf rootstock. Fits Kristin (15-18 ft mature) on standard rootstock.
**Cons**: requires a ladder once mature.

## Kym Green Bush (KGB)

Pedestrian (no-ladder) system — multiple temporary vertical fruiting leaders, all renewed via stub cuts to ~10 inches when they age out.

**Establishment**:
- At planting: head nursery tree at **18 inches** above ground.
- First dormant season: head all resulting leaders to 2-5 inches.
- Second growing season: repeat heading prior to summer solstice.
- Mature: top tree at 8 ft. Remove lateral branches. Renew leaders via stub cuts.

> "Head nursery tree 18 inches above ground at planting. First dormant season: head all leaders to 2-5 inches. Second growing season: repeat heading prior to summer solstice." — PNW 667

**Pros**: pedestrian (no ladder); simple to maintain; works on full-size or semi-vigorous rootstock.
**Cons**: not for non-spur cultivars (Regina, Attika). Requires aggressive heading establishment.
**Verdict for Kristin / BlackGold**: KGB works for both. Best if avoiding ladders is a priority.

## Spanish Bush

Multiple permanent vertical leaders (3-5), with lateral fruiting wood renewed annually. Hedge sides annually, top at 8 ft.

**Establishment**:
- Similar to KGB heading at planting.
- Select 3-5 permanent vertical leaders.
- Each leader develops lateral fruiting branches; laterals are renewed with stub cuts every 4-5 years.

**Pros**: low + spread out; pedestrian; fits non-spur cultivars.
**Cons**: more pruning labor than KGB.

## Open Center / Vase

Three to four main scaffolds radiating out from a low trunk; no central leader.

**Establishment**:
- Head whip to 24-30" at planting.
- Year 1: select 3-4 scaffolds at evenly-spaced compass points, wide angles.
- Remove the central leader.

**Pros**: maximum sunlight in canopy interior — good for wet/humid climates with disease pressure (cracks better, dries faster).
**Cons**: weaker structure than central leader; can split under heavy fruit load.

## What to pick

For the user's Kristin and BlackGold:

| Goal | Pick |
|------|------|
| Simplest, most familiar | **Modified Central Leader** |
| No ladder needed | **Kym Green Bush** |
| Maximum airflow / wet site | **Open Center** |
| Spread-out hedge style | **Spanish Bush** |

For most home orchards in zone 4-7 with medium humidity, **Modified Central Leader** is the recommended default. Switch to KGB if you want to keep the tree under 8 ft for ease of access and bird netting.

## What NOT to pick (commercial-only systems)

PNW 667 lists four more systems best ignored for home orchards:

- **Steep Leader** — 3-4 vertical leaders + horizontal scaffolds; pyramidal; complex training
- **Super Slender Axe (SSA)** — single leader, ultra-high density, dwarfing rootstock only; very labor-intensive renewal
- **Tall Spindle Axe (TSA)** — central leader with continuous spiral whorl; commercial high-density only
- **UFO (Upright Fruiting Offshoots)** — horizontal cordon trunk planted at 45°; commercial trellised plantings
- **Vogel Central Leader** — narrow upright form for vigorous rootstocks; useful but more demanding than Modified Central Leader
