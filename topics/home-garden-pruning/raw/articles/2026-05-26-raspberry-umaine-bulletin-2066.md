# UMaine Bulletin #2066: Raspberry and Blackberry Pruning

## Key findings

### Authorship
- Bulletin #2066, by **David T. Handley**, UMaine Vegetable and Small Fruit Specialist.
- Last updated 2023.

### Summer-bearing raspberries
- Selection criterion: "Only the most vigorous canes, those with the greatest height and basal diameter, should be left in the row."
- Density target: **4 to 5 canes per foot of row** after thinning.
- Timing: Old fruiting canes "completely remove[d] in late winter or early spring before buds break" (note: differs from many sources that recommend post-harvest removal — UMaine accepts winter removal as adequate).

### Fall-bearing (everbearing) raspberries
Two recognized management options:

1. **Double-crop method:** "Prune like summer varieties to produce both summer and fall harvests."
2. **Fall-crop only (mow-down):** "Simply mow all the canes down early each spring" and thin new growth "to about six inches apart."

The mow-down method translates roughly to ~2 canes per linear foot for fall-only — slightly thinner than the 4–5/ft used for double-crop.

### Black raspberries (and blackberries)
- Post-harvest: "remove all canes that fruited following the harvest."
- Density target (per hill): "**five to seven of the sturdiest** per hill."
- Lateral pruning in winter: "cut back to **12 buds (usually about 12 inches in length)**."

### General principle
- "Pruning is a vital part of maintaining a healthy raspberry planting" — emphasized for disease control and yield improvement.

## Notable quotes / specifics

> "Simply mow all the canes down early each spring" and thin new growth "to about six inches apart."

## Source notes

- **Cold-climate northeast US authority.** Aligns closely with UMN on cane density (4–5/ft for summer-bearing reds) but provides a slightly higher upper bound on hill density for blacks (5–7 vs UMN's 4–5).
- The "6 inches apart" specification for mow-down regrowth is unusually concrete — equivalent to ~2 canes/ft, which is sparser than double-crop density.
- Lateral-shortening guidance "12 buds / ~12 inches" is on the shorter end versus UMN's 12–18".
- Useful for triangulating the consensus 4–7 canes/ft range for floricane-fruiting red raspberries.
- David Handley is a long-tenured extension specialist; high credibility.
