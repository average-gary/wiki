# Growing Blueberries in the Home Garden (NC State Extension)

## Key findings

- **At planting (year 1):** Remove approximately **two-thirds of top growth on bare-root plants** and **half on potted plants**, "leaving only 1-3 of the most vigorous upright shoots." Strip all flower buds to prevent year-1 fruiting. This is the most aggressive at-planting cutback in the source set (UMD removes 1/3-1/2; UGA removes 1/3-1/2; NC State pushes 2/3 on bare-root).
- **Year 2:** Almost no pruning. Just remove all flower buds + any weak, damaged, or diseased growth.
- **Year 3:** Allow some flower buds on vigorous shoots — first real but small crop.
- **Year 4:** Bushes reach 4-5 ft. Can handle a crop, but flower buds should still be thinned to prevent overloading the still-young canes.
- **Mature bush winter pruning** focuses on:
  - Removing old, weak, or diseased canes.
  - Cutting back tall shoots to encourage lower branching and control height.
  - **"Thin fruiting shoots to reduce the number of flower buds by about 50%"** — explicit crop-load thinning rule, distinctive vs. other sources.
- **Timing:** Late winter is ideal, "particularly for mountain plantings" (cold-area waiting rule).
- **Rabbiteye summer pruning:** "Rabbiteye varieties may benefit from additional summer pruning in **late July** to manage vigorous growth." NC State is one of the few sources naming an explicit summer-pruning month for rabbiteye.

## Notable quotes / specifics

> Remove approximately two-thirds of top growth on bare-root plants and half on potted plants, "leaving only 1-3 of the most vigorous upright shoots."

> "Little pruning will be required the second year except removing all flower buds and any weak, damaged or diseased growth."

> "Thin fruiting shoots to reduce the number of flower buds by about 50%."

> "Rabbiteye varieties may benefit from additional summer pruning in late July to manage vigorous growth."

## Source notes

- The 50% flower-bud thinning rule for mature bushes is a distinctive, actionable target — translates the abstract "remove twiggy growth" advice into a concrete crop-load number.
- The 2/3 cutback at planting on bare-root is at the aggressive end of the spectrum. Reasoning: bare-root plants have lost most of their root mass; severe top reduction rebalances root:shoot ratio.
- Late-July rabbiteye summer pruning is the most specific summer-pruning timing in the source set. It targets vigorous water-sprout-style growth that would otherwise continue extending and overcrowd the canopy by next spring.
- NC State sits between Clemson and UMD geographically and covers both highbush and rabbiteye, making it a useful "transition zone" reference.
- No specific cane-number target given for mature bushes (unlike UMN's 4-6, UMD's 12-18, Clemson's 7); compiler should pull cane targets from those other sources.
