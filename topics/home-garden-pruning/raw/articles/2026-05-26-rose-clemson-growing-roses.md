# Growing Roses (Clemson HGIC 1172) — Shrub Rose & Knock Out section

## Key findings

- **Category placement:** modern shrub roses (the group containing Knock Out) are described as "vigorous and hardy" landscape plants requiring significantly less maintenance than hybrid teas or floribundas. This is the conceptual basis for treating Knock Outs more like a flowering shrub than like a fussy hybrid rose at pruning time.
- **Knock Out specifically:** explicitly named as "a popular modern shrub rose." Cultural recommendations therefore inherit from the shrub-rose category, not the hybrid-tea protocol.
- **Pre-winter cane shortening (NOT structural pruning):** "To prevent wind damage on large bushes, cut the canes back to 3 feet" before cold weather arrives. This is a wind-protection cut only — not the late-winter structural prune. Useful for tall second-year Knock Outs in exposed sites; the real pruning still happens at bud swell.
- **Disease-resistance context:** "Many have good disease and pest resistance" — but resistance is not immunity. Black spot and powdery mildew still occur; airflow management at pruning time matters even on Knock Outs.
- **Site requirements that affect pruning load:** at least 6–8 hours direct sun, good air circulation, well-drained soil, deep infrequent watering. Shaded or poorly-spaced Knock Outs grow leggy and force more remedial pruning.
- **Spacing implication for RRD:** the airflow recommendation aligns with the rose-rosette protocol of not letting canes touch between bushes — the eriophyid mite walks between contacting canes.

## Notable quotes / specifics

## Source notes

Clemson HGIC growing-roses factsheet — frames the conceptual category for landscape shrub roses and points downstream to the Pruning Roses (HGIC 1173) factsheet for technique. This source's main contribution to the pruning wiki is the **3-foot pre-winter wind cut** (a cut that's easy to confuse with structural pruning but serves a different purpose — preventing rocking and root damage in winter wind, not shaping the bush) and the explicit positioning of Knock Out within the low-maintenance shrub-rose category. It does not provide hard-pruning numbers or one-third-rule details — those come from the pruning factsheets.
