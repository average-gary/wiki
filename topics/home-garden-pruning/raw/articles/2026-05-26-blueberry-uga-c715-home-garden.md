# Home Garden Blueberries (UGA Extension Circular C715)

## Key findings

- **At planting (year 1):** "Remove low, twiggy growth entirely and tip remaining shoots to remove all the flower buds." Roughly **one-third to one-half** of the plant should be removed, and any flowers/fruit that escaped pruning should be plucked through the first year. UGA is in the aggressive-establishment camp.
- **Rabbiteye establishment:** Minimal pruning until the bush reaches **4-6 ft tall**. Don't begin renewal cycle until that threshold.
- **Rabbiteye renewal pruning (mature):** Remove "one to three of the largest canes each winter at 0 to 24 in. from ground level **or a total of about 20 percent of the canopy**." Renewal cycle ≈ 5 years — i.e. the entire bush gets turned over every five years.
- **Rabbiteye height cap:** "Excessively tall canes can be pruned back to 6 ft each winter" so fruit stays within reach.
- **Southern highbush establishment:** Begin cane-renewal program around **4-5 ft tall** (slightly earlier than rabbiteye).
- **Southern highbush renewal:** Remove oldest, weakest, or diseased canes entirely; then prune approximately two remaining canes annually either "to strong laterals or to within 1 ft of the ground." Same heuristic Clemson uses.
- **Southern highbush mature:** Prioritize removing low spreading branches and "excessively twiggy growth with too many flower buds." Twiggy growth at branch tips concentrates flower buds → too many small berries on weak wood; thinning improves berry size.
- **Cuts at 0-24 inches above ground:** Note this is *not* a flush ground-level cut. Leaving 0-24" of stub allows a vigorous replacement cane to break from latent buds on the stub. This is a significant nuance vs. the "remove at ground level" rule from northern sources.

## Source notes

- UGA C715 is the cleanest *quantitative* rabbiteye protocol in the set: 20% canopy / 1-3 largest canes / year / ~5-year cycle.
- The "cut at 0-24 inches" detail is materially different from "ground level" cuts in northern sources. Both work; stubs invigorate replacement growth on rabbiteye specifically.
- Height cap (6 ft) is a southeastern home-garden practical concern that doesn't show up in northern sources where bushes don't get that tall.
- Twiggy-growth rationale (too many flower buds → small berries) is the clearest articulation of why removing branch-tip clutter improves quality.
- No summer-pruning specifics; UGA treats it as a winter task. (Clemson is the outlier that allows post-harvest rabbiteye pruning.)
- Best paired with Clemson HGIC for southeast-region rabbiteye/southern-highbush work.
