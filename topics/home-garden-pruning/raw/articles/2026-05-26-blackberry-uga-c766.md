# Home Garden Blackberries — UGA Extension Circular C766

## Key findings
- Blackberries are biennial-caned: primocanes grow vegetatively year 1; floricanes flower, fruit, and senesce year 2. Fruit-bud initiation occurs in late summer to early fall on primocanes — meaning summer tipping decisions made then carry into next year's crop.
- Erect cultivars (e.g., Apache, Natchez, Ouachita): summer-cut new canes to 40–42 inches in early summer to encourage lateral shoot formation and improve hedge-row stability. This is taller than the 30–36 in target some sources cite — UGA's guidance is for the warmer Southeast where canes flush vigorously.
- Erect dormant pruning: after removing dead floricanes, reduce remaining laterals by one-third to one-half of their length. This is a length-ratio rather than a fixed inch target; lateral vigor varies with cultivar and site.
- Semi-erect (Triple Crown) and trailing (Chester) types require a trellis. For trailing, tip canes 6 inches above the top wire to force branching. Lateral shoots can be shortened to 10–20 inches if needed for a manageable canopy.
- Spent floricane removal: post-harvest pruning out of fruited canes is emphasized as central to maintaining productivity and disease management. Anthracnose, cane blight, and orange rust all overwinter in spent canes if left in place.
- Cultivar callouts: Apache (erect, large-fruited, thornless), Natchez (early erect, thornless), Ouachita (mid-season erect, thornless), Chester (late trailing, thornless).

## Notable quotes / specifics
> "Cut...to a height of 40 to 42 inches in early summer to encourage lateral shoot development."

> Dormant pruning: "Reduce these [laterals] by one-third to one-half of the length."

> Trailing types: "Tip[ped]...6 inches above the top wire to encourage branching."

> Lateral shoots on trailing types may be "shortened to lengths of 10 to 20 inches if necessary."

## Source notes
University of Georgia Cooperative Extension, Circular C766 — published through the College of Agricultural and Environmental Sciences and accessible via the CAES Field Report platform. UGA is a strong source for southeastern bramble culture; their cultivar list overlaps heavily with the University of Arkansas breeding program, which originated many of the leading thornless erect cultivars (Navaho, Apache, Ouachita, Natchez, Prime-Ark series). Key value of this source: clean separation of pruning approach by growth habit (erect vs semi-erect vs trailing), and the explicit 40–42 in tipping height target for erect cultivars (which is on the upper end of the extension consensus range of 30–48 in).
