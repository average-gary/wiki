# Northeast Small Fruit Management Guide: Pruning Brambles (UMass)

## Key findings

### Multi-state extension authority
- Hosted by UMass Amherst as part of the **2024–2025 Northeast Small Fruit Management Guide**.
- Co-authored by extension specialists across the Northeast region (NY, MA, ME, NH, VT, CT, RI), making it one of the most peer-reviewed extension references for the Northeast.

### Summer-bearing brambles (general)
- Timing: "Remove second year canes and thin out weak, spindly first year canes anytime after harvest through winter." Notably permissive on timing — anywhere from immediate post-harvest through dormancy works.
- **Red raspberry density:** "no more than **4 to 6 canes per linear foot of row**" — broader range than UMN's 4–5.
- **Black/purple raspberry & blackberry density:** "**4 to 8 canes per crown**" (per-hill metric instead of linear-foot, reflecting these brambles' clumping growth habit).
- **Row width:** narrowed to "**2 feet or less**."

### Fall-bearing (everbearing) brambles
Two recognized options:

1. **Mow-down (preferred for clean fall crop):** "completely mowed down each year in late fall to early spring before growth starts."
2. **Double-crop:** "remove weak, spindly first year canes after harvest" and "remove the tops of the canes where fruit was borne" — consistent with the spring tipping back to last fruited node described elsewhere.

### Implicit framework
- The guide separates red raspberries (canes/linear foot) from blacks, purples, and blackberries (canes/crown), reflecting different growth habits: reds spread via root suckers and form a continuous hedgerow; blacks/purples form discrete clumps from a single crown.
- Disease management in companion tables (Table 45 = summer-bearing pest mgmt; Table 46 = fall-bearing primocane-fruiting) reinforces that primocane-fruiting management is treated as a distinct system, not a variant.

## Notable quotes / specifics

> "Remove second year canes and thin out weak, spindly first year canes anytime after harvest through winter."

> "no more than 4 to 6 canes per linear foot of row" (red raspberries)

> "4 to 8 canes per crown" (blackberries, black and purple raspberries)

> Row width: "2 feet or less"

> Fall-bearing: "completely mowed down each year in late fall to early spring before growth starts"

## Source notes

- **Strongest source for the linear-foot vs. per-crown distinction.** This per-growth-habit framing isn't always explicit in single-state guides.
- Provides an upper bound on cane density (6/ft for reds, 8/crown for blacks/purples) higher than UMN (4–5/ft) — useful for capturing the realistic range across northern climates and management intensities.
- Permissive timing window ("after harvest through winter") supports gardener flexibility — useful counterpoint to sources that mandate immediate post-harvest removal.
- The 2-ft row-width spec is more conservative than UMN's "not wider than 12 inches" for new cane spread within rows but less conservative than CSU's "2 feet wide at ground level" — implies CSU is talking about the entire hedgerow base while UMass and UMN are distinguishing core row from spread.
- Part of the regional NE Small Fruit Guide updated annually for commercial and serious home growers.
