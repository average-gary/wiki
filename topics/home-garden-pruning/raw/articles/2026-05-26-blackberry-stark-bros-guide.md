# Pruning Blackberry Plants — Stark Bro's Growing Guide

## Key findings
- Two-year cane biology: berry bushes bear only on 2-year-old canes (floricanes). After fruiting, those canes are pruned to the ground to make room for the next generation of primocanes that will fruit the following year. This is the core mental model that drives every other pruning decision.
- Spring pruning for trailing-habit cultivars (which most thornless cultivars effectively are when run on a trellis): cut each main cane to 3–4 feet in length; trim side branches (laterals) to about 12 inches, keeping five or six buds per branch. The 5–6 buds-per-lateral target is consistent with the 12-bud target some extensions use; Stark's number is roughly half-length, suggesting a higher-quality / lower-yield trade than UMaine.
- Fall (post-dormancy) pruning: cut lateral branches back to 16–18 inches. These laterals will bear fruit the following summer. The slightly longer fall length (vs ~12 in spring) likely reflects a Stark-recommended approach of leaving laterals long going into winter and tightening in spring.
- Post-harvest sanitation: once 2-year-old canes have finished fruiting, remove them entirely. This both opens room for vigorous primocane growth and removes disease-harboring tissue.
- Overall objective stated: "maintain plant structure, prevent tangling, and ensure their ability to bear" — i.e., pruning is a structural exercise as much as a yield exercise. For thornless varieties this also means picking and trellis access, which deteriorates fast if canes tangle.

## Notable quotes / specifics
> "Most berry bushes bear only once on 2-year-old canes. After the canes have produced fruit, you should prune them back to the ground."

> Spring: "Prune each main cane to 3–4 feet in length. Then trim side branches to approximately 12 inches, maintaining five or six buds per branch."

> Fall: "After the plants become dormant in fall, cut back the lateral branches to 16–18 inches. These laterals will bear fruit the following summer."

## Source notes
Stark Bro's Nurseries & Orchards is a long-established (1816) commercial fruit-tree and small-fruit nursery; their growing guides are not peer-reviewed extension content but are widely cited and align with extension consensus on cane biology, summer tipping, and post-harvest cane removal. Quality is rated 3 (vs 4–5 for extension sources) because the guide does not differentiate erect from semi-erect from primocane-fruiting cultivars, and the 16–18 in fall lateral length sits longer than most extension guides (10–14 in). Useful as a check on extension-only sources and for the specific 5–6 buds-per-lateral cue, which is a practical home-grower-friendly count rather than an inch measurement.
