# Kristin and BlackGold Sweet Cherry Cultivar Profiles

**Sources:**
- Stark Bro's Nursery — Kristin Sweet Cherry product page (`starkbros.com/products/fruit-trees/cherry-trees/kristin-sweet-cherry`)
- Cummins Nursery — BlackGold® Cherry on Mahaleb product page (`cumminsnursery.com/buy-trees/product-detail.php?type=tree&id=14757`)

## Key findings — Kristin (NY-bred sweet cherry)

- **Mature size**: approximately 15-18 ft tall × 15-18 ft wide on standard rootstock. Recommended spacing 15-18 ft.
- **USDA zones**: 4-8. Cold-hardy; "tested in Norway and across Montana" for cold-climate reliability.
- **Vigor**: moderate, standard-sized tree. Time to first crop: 4-7 years.
- **Habit**: standard sweet cherry habit (upright, vigorous, apically dominant — typical *P. avium*). Limb spreaders and tree stakes recommended during establishment, suggesting trees benefit from early branch-angle training.
- **Harvest**: July; "large, glossy-skinned purplish-black cherries... firm and meaty."
- **Pollination**: NOT self-fertile. Requires a compatible (different group) sweet cherry within 100 ft. Kristin is in the same incompatibility group as Bing, Napoleon, Lambert, Ulster, Somerset, and Emperor Francis (per Andersen 2004) — so a non-group cultivar is required (BlackGold works as universal pollenizer).
- **Pruning notes**: not detailed by the nursery beyond support tools at planting.

## Key findings — BlackGold (Cornell/Geneva 2000 release)

- **Origin**: Cornell University cross of Stark Gold × Stella, introduced 1996, released ~2000. Bred specifically for late bloom to escape spring frost.
- **Tree habit**: half-standard size on Mahaleb rootstock (~60% of standard). Less vigorous than Kristin on standard rootstock.
- **USDA zones**: 4-8. **Exceptional cold hardiness** — "a BlackGold® planted in the Twin Cities by Purvis Nursery has taken -30°F with no tip damage."
- **Self-fertile**; "can be planted alone in a home garden." Also functions as "a universal pollenizer for any mid or late-blooming sweet cherry" — so a BlackGold near a Kristin solves Kristin's pollination problem.
- **Harvest**: early-to-mid season, ~June 27 in NY (≈7 days after Bing).
- **Disease resistance**: "considerable resistance to bacterial canker"; "crack tolerance is only moderate."
- **Productivity**: "reliable, high-yielding"; bears fruit within four years.

## Source notes

Both Stark Bro's and Cummins Nursery are reputable U.S. fruit tree nurseries; Cummins (Geneva, NY) has direct access to Cornell breeding stock and tends to carry technically accurate descriptions traceable to the breeder. These are nursery-catalog sources (quality 3) rather than peer-reviewed cultivar releases, but their habit/hardiness/disease claims are consistent with the formal Andersen 2004 review and the published Kristin and BlackGold release notices from NYSAES. **Practical implication for the home grower**: Kristin is the larger, more vigorous, hardier-but-not-self-fertile cultivar that anchors the planting; BlackGold is the smaller, self-fertile, late-blooming, canker-resistant partner that doubles as pollenizer. Because BlackGold has documented bacterial canker resistance and Kristin does not have a strong canker-resistance rating, **pruning timing matters more for Kristin** — late-July post-harvest pruning per Carroll et al. is the conservative protocol.
