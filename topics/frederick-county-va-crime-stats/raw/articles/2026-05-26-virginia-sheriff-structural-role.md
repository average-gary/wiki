# Virginia Sheriff — Structural Role and Frederick County Context

## Constitutional foundation

**Va. Const. Art. VII §4** — *"There shall be elected by the qualified voters of each county and city a treasurer, a sheriff, an attorney for the Commonwealth, a clerk…"*

Key facts:
- Sheriff is **constitutionally mandated** in every Virginia county AND every Virginia independent city.
- **Term: 4 years**, taking office January 1 after a November election.
- Sheriff is **NOT appointed by Board of Supervisors** and is NOT subordinate to it.
- Five constitutional officers per locality: Sheriff, Commonwealth's Attorney, Clerk of Circuit Court, Treasurer, Commissioner of Revenue. **All separately elected.**

## Statutory duties (Code of Virginia)

Title 15.2 (counties), Title 19.2 (criminal procedure), Title 53.1 (corrections):

- **§15.2-1722**: records-keeping mandate for sheriffs and chiefs of police — the legal hook for VSP IBR/NIBRS reporting
- **§15.2-1719**: sheriff custody of unclaimed property
- **§15.2-1721**: disposal of unclaimed firearms
- **§15.2-1730.1**: authority and immunity of sheriffs and deputies
- **§15.2-1609** area: Compensation Board funding

**Four-pillar duty list**: patrol-where-no-PD / jail / court security / civil process.

## Compensation Board funding model

Sheriffs in Virginia are **partially state-funded** via the Compensation Board, with localities free to supplement. So FCSO's budget is split between state appropriation and Frederick County supplement. This affects the politics of any "defund/expand" framing.

## Frederick County, Virginia — jurisdictional facts

- **Population (2020 Census)**: 91,419
- **Population (2025 estimate)**: 99,955
- **Area**: 416 sq mi
- **Northernmost county in Virginia**
- **Two incorporated towns**: Stephens City (largest) and Middletown
- **Winchester is the county seat but is an INDEPENDENT CITY** — politically independent of Frederick County
- **Current Sheriff**: Lenny Millholland (Independent), in office since January 1, 2016
- **Current Commonwealth's Attorney**: Ross Spicer (R)

## Why "Frederick County" ≠ "Winchester" jurisdictionally

Virginia has **38 independent cities** (more than every other state combined; almost all U.S. independent cities are in VA). The 1871 post-Reconstruction Virginia Constitution established the structure.

> *"Virginia's thirty-eight independent cities are not politically part of a county, even though geographically they may be completely surrounded by one."*

Functional implication: an independent city can be the **county seat** of a county it is NOT part of — exactly the Winchester / FrCo arrangement. **Crime statistics for Winchester are reported separately from FrCo**, even though Winchester is the county's namesake city.

## Three primary law-enforcement agencies in the FrCo geographic area

| Agency | Jurisdiction | ORI |
|---|---|---|
| **Frederick County Sheriff's Office (FCSO)** | Unincorporated FrCo + concurrent in towns | VA0340000 |
| **Stephens City Police Department** | Inside Stephens City town limits (concurrent with FCSO) | TBD |
| **Winchester Police Department** | City of Winchester (independent city, NOT in FrCo) | VA1095000 |
| Virginia State Police | I-81/I-66 corridors, VSP Area 22 | VA0290000 |

Towns in Virginia (Stephens City, Middletown) do NOT strip the sheriff of authority the way independent cities do. Sheriff retains **concurrent jurisdiction** in towns.

## Magistrate system

VA magistrates are state employees under the **Office of the Executive Secretary of the Supreme Court of Virginia**, NOT under the sheriff. Code of Virginia Title 19.2 §19.2-33 et seq.

## Court system

- **26th Judicial Circuit** covers Frederick + Winchester + Clarke + Page + Shenandoah + Warren counties.
- Joint Judicial Center, 5 N. Kent St., Winchester.
- Eight circuit judges (Chief: Hon. Kevin C. Black).
- Frederick and Winchester **share a circuit court** — federal-vs-state filing decisions for drug cases route through one institution.
- Circuit / General District / J&DR judges are **appointed by the General Assembly** (NOT elected).
