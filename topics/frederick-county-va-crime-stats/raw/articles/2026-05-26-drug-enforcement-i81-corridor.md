# Drug Enforcement + I-81 Corridor

## Northwest Virginia Regional Drug & Gang Task Force (NWVRDGTF)

**Confirmed entity.** Multi-jurisdictional task force covering:

- Frederick County (FCSO)
- Clarke County
- Page County
- Shenandoah County
- Warren County
- City of Winchester (Winchester PD)
- Town of Front Royal PD
- Town of Luray PD
- Town of Strasburg PD
- Virginia State Police

Coordinates with Northern Shenandoah Valley Substance Use Coalition for naloxone distribution.

## NWVRDGTF 2024 — ~$10.9M in drug seizures

Per regional reporting (Royal Examiner, Winchester Gazette, VSP releases): **NWVRDGTF seized roughly $10.9 million in illicit drugs in 2024** — a ~5x jump from the 2022 report's ~$2 million figure.

This is the single most consequential regional drug-enforcement datapoint for the FrCo/Winchester area.

## Representative cases

### Five-person FrCo narcotics/firearms operation (March 2024)
Five arrests in Winchester after a five-month investigation. Combined narcotics + firearms charges. **Demonstrates the recurring "guns + drugs" combined-charge pattern** in FrCo enforcement.

### John E. Wilson Jr. — fentanyl/cocaine arrest (April 2024)
Wilson, 29, of Winchester, arrested by NWVRDGTF in Front Royal with 2 oz of fentanyl/cocaine mixture. Cross-jurisdictional pattern: Winchester resident arrested in Warren County by task force.

## Federal context — Western District of Virginia

FrCo is in the U.S. Attorney's Office Western District of Virginia (WDVA). Major federal drug cases originating in FrCo would route through the WDVA **Harrisonburg division** (which handles Winchester-originating federal cases).

WDVA press releases at `justice.gov/usao-wdva/pr` are the canonical source — but were 403'd from this fetch environment. **Flagged for follow-up.**

## I-81 corridor context

FrCo straddles I-81, a major north-south drug corridor. VSP "Operation DISS-rupt" (Sept 2024) is a known I-81 interdiction operation — but specific FrCo numbers were not surfaced in this round. Most concrete I-81 stats came from Maryland State Police (different I-81 segment, north of FrCo).

## Drug-court / Recovery Court status — 26th Judicial Circuit

Per Virginia's Office of the Executive Secretary, Supreme Court of Virginia (https://www.vacourts.gov/courtadmin/aoc/djs/programs/sds/home.html):

Three statewide specialty docket types:
- Behavioral Health Docket
- **Recovery Courts (drug court)**
- Veterans Treatment Docket

Established under Rule 1:25 of the Supreme Court of Virginia.

**The 26th Judicial Circuit (Frederick + Winchester + Clarke + Page + Shenandoah + Warren) is not visibly listed as operating a circuit-court Recovery Court** on the index page. Definitive answer is in the OES annual evaluation reports (2019-2025 PDFs). Many of FrCo's peer southwest-VA jurisdictions DO operate recovery courts.

**If confirmed absent**, this is a notable gap given the regional opioid load — peer jurisdictions to the south (Roanoke, southwest VA) have drug courts.

## Drug-overdose data → enforcement linkage

Cross-reference [[2026-05-26-demographics-context|demographic context]]:
- VA statewide 2024 overdose deaths: **-37% YoY**
- Winchester crime 2024: **-8 to -13% YoY** across aggregators
- VSP statewide drug arrests 2024: **-7.2% YoY** (but +41.7% age 65+)

The supply-side fentanyl collapse in 2024 is a confounder for any "FrCo crime down due to enforcement" narrative — disentangling supply shock from policing strategy requires controlling for both.
