# HIDTA Designation — Frederick County, VA

**Verdict**: Frederick County, Virginia is a designated **Washington/Baltimore HIDTA (WB-HIDTA)** county — NOT Appalachia HIDTA.

Confirmed via the National HIDTA Assistance Center's published roster: https://www.nhac.org/hidta-counties-by-state/

## Designation history

- **WB-HIDTA established**: 1994
- **FrCo VA inclusion year**: not separately published per-county on the NHAC index. Likely part of the post-2000 westward expansion of WB-HIDTA into the Shenandoah Valley I-81 corridor (same expansion arc that brought in Warren County).
- ONDCP annual reports + WB-HIDTA's own annual report typically list per-county entry years; not extracted in this round.

## Critical pattern

HIDTA designation in northern VA tracks the **I-81 / I-66 / I-495 trafficking corridors**, NOT contiguous geography:

- Frederick (I-81 + Rt 7 to Loudoun): designated
- Warren (I-66 junction at Front Royal): designated
- **Rural Shenandoah-side counties** (Clarke, Shenandoah, Page): **NOT designated**, despite being immediately adjacent to FrCo and Warren

**FrCo sits at the westernmost edge of WB-HIDTA's Virginia footprint along I-81.**

## Full Virginia WB-HIDTA roster

## Appalachia HIDTA (separate footprint — for contrast)

Virginia Appalachia HIDTA counties: Carroll, Dickenson, Galax, Grayson, Lee, Pulaski, Scott, Tazewell, Wise, Wythe — entirely southwestern, **NO overlap** with northern Shenandoah Valley.

## What HIDTA designation provides operationally

HIDTA is an **ONDCP-administered grant program**, not an enforcement agency. Designation provides:

1. **Federal grant funding** to local agencies for drug-enforcement initiatives, equipment, overtime, analyst positions
2. **DEA-led task force participation** — designated jurisdictions can co-locate officers in HIDTA task forces with DEA, FBI, ATF, HSI, state/local partners under federal Title 21 cross-designation, giving local deputies/officers federal arrest authority for drug cases
3. **Intelligence sharing** through HIDTA Investigative Support Centers (ISCs) — case deconfliction, shared informant tracking, link analysis
4. **Asset-forfeiture sharing** under federal equitable-sharing rules (relevant in Virginia, where state forfeiture rules are more restrictive than federal)

For FrCo / Winchester specifically: designation makes FCSO and WPD eligible to detail officers into WB-HIDTA initiatives and to receive HIDTA-funded resources for drug interdiction along I-81.

## NWVRDGTF and HIDTA — relationship

The **Northwest Virginia Regional Drug & Gang Task Force** (NWVRDGTF, see [[2026-05-26-drug-enforcement-i81-corridor|drug enforcement article]]) is technically a **VSP-coordinated regional task force**, distinct from a HIDTA initiative. But via WB-HIDTA membership, FrCo/Winchester likely have:

- **HIDTA-funded slots/positions** in NWVRDGTF, OR
- **HIDTA AIMS grants** flowing through NSVSUC (the [[2026-05-26-26th-circuit-recovery-court|Recovery Court operator]]) to coordinate enforcement-treatment pipelines
