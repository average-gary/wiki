# FCSO 287(g) Status + Spanberger ED-1

Verdict on two claims that surfaced in earlier rounds with shaky sourcing.

## Claim 1: FCSO has a 287(g) MOU — UNCONFIRMED

**Status**: not verifiable from primary sources in this round.

- **ICE 287(g) participating-agencies list** (`ice.gov/identify-and-arrest/287g`): returned 403 to WebFetch.
- **ILRC 287(g) tracker**: data layer didn't render via WebFetch.
- **FCSO own site** (`fcva.us`, `fcsova.org`): 403'd.

**Indirect evidence FCSO does have an MOU**:
- Bing News aggregation of recent VA coverage: "ICE arrests in Virginia surged to over 7,000 in 2025, driven by 287(g) agreements" with "approximately 22 local sheriffs' offices maintain them" post-Spanberger.
- FrCo is in the Shenandoah Valley belt where 287(g) adoption was concentrated under Youngkin.
- Royal Examiner reporting: FCSO transferred 6 individuals to ICE custody.

**Counter-caveat**: **6 ICE handoffs is consistent with EITHER**:
- (a) a formal 287(g) jail-enforcement MOU, OR
- (b) plain detainer cooperation under VA Code §19.2-83.2 / federal 8 U.S.C. §1357(d)

The two are **legally distinct**, and Royal Examiner reporting does not name the legal vehicle.

**Likely model if MOU exists**: Warrant Service Officer (WSO) — the lowest-touch model, which Youngkin's executive order specifically encouraged because it requires only 8 hours of training. Jail Enforcement Model (JEM) is less common in mid-size VA counties.

**Definitive resolution**: file a FOIA request to FCSO for the executed MOA.

## Claim 2: Spanberger Feb 2026 termination — PARTIALLY VERIFIED, important nuance

There are **two distinct Spanberger actions**, not one:

### 2A. Day-One rescission (Jan 17, 2026)

Spanberger took office Jan 17, 2026 and rescinded Youngkin's executive order that had directed VA local/state LE to enter 287(g) MOAs.

Source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abigail_Spanberger, footnote 135.

**Did NOT force existing MOA holders to exit** — purely prospective.

### 2B. Executive Directive ED-1 (Feb 4, 2026)

**"Directing the Commonwealth's Law Enforcement Agencies to Terminate Any and All Section 287(g) Agreements"**

Listed on `governor.virginia.gov/executive-actions/`. The PDF is hosted at the governor's media library (URL appeared to have a formatting issue and direct PDF returned 404; title and date confirmed from index page).

### Critical scope nuance

ED-1 binds **the Commonwealth's executive-branch LE agencies**:
- Virginia State Police
- Department of Corrections
- Capitol Police
- DCJS-controlled bodies

**ED-1 does NOT bind sheriffs.** Sheriffs are constitutional officers under **Va. Const. Art. VII §4** — independently elected, accountable to voters, NOT subordinate to the governor.

Multiple post-ED-1 articles confirm sheriffs remain free to maintain MOAs:
- TANTV/MSN reports ~22 sheriff MOAs persisted
- AOL/Hill story: Trump border czar Tom Homan warning Spanberger about jail access — only makes sense if local sheriffs (who control jails) still hold MOAs

**Implication for FCSO**: ED-1 does NOT terminate FCSO's MOA if FCSO has one. Termination would require Sheriff Millholland's voluntary withdrawal or Frederick County Board of Supervisors denying jail-cost reimbursement.

## Earlier wiki framing (now corrected)

Earlier rounds noted "Spanberger reportedly terminated VA participation in Feb 2026." That framing was **misleading**:
- The termination is real (ED-1, Feb 4, 2026)
- But it binds executive-branch agencies only
- Sheriffs' MOAs (if any) survive ED-1
