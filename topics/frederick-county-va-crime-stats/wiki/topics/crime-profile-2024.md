# Crime Profile — FrCo + Winchester, 2024 (best-available)

Synthesis article. **Caveats**: numbers are from secondary aggregators (CrimeGrade, NeighborhoodScout, City-Data, AreaVibes, Data USA) because primary VSP *Crime in Virginia 2024* PDF (>10MB) and FBI CDE (JS SPA) were not directly extracted in this round. The pattern is robust across aggregators; exact rates may shift slightly when primary data is pulled.

## TL;DR

- Both **Frederick County (FCSO jurisdiction)** and **Winchester (city)** have **violent crime rates below the Virginia state average** (~255/100k).
- **Winchester has roughly 3x the property crime rate** of FrCo, driven by retail-corridor theft and a denser population.
- **Both jurisdictions' crime fell 2023→2024** — directionally consistent with VA statewide violent-crime drop of −7.0% and statewide overdose-death drop of −37%.
- **No homicides in Winchester in 2024**; 1 in FrCo.
- **Drug enforcement scaled up** in 2024: NWVRDGTF seized ~$10.9M (5x 2022 level).

## What's driving the city/county property gap

The 2,329 vs 832/100k Winchester-vs-FrCo property gap is mostly:

1. **Retail density**: Apple Blossom Mall + Route 50/522 commercial strip in Winchester
2. **Population density**: 77 crimes/sq mi in Winchester vs national median 24.5
3. **Different demographic mix**: Winchester poverty 21.5% vs FrCo 6.8%

This is **not unusual for a small-mid city embedded in a wealthy suburban county** — comparable independent cities in Virginia (Harrisonburg, Staunton, Charlottesville) show similar city-vs-surrounding-county gaps.

## Drug enforcement (2024)

- **NWVRDGTF (Northwest Virginia Regional Drug & Gang Task Force)**: **~$10.9M in seizures** in 2024 — **~5x the 2022 figure** (~$2M)
- Multi-jurisdictional: FCSO + Winchester PD + Front Royal/Luray/Strasburg PDs + 5 county sheriffs (Frederick, Clarke, Page, Shenandoah, Warren) + VSP
- Heroin + fentanyl named primary substances since 2012
- **Statewide context**: VA drug overdose deaths fell **−37%** in 2024 (1,548 deaths vs ~2,460 in 2023). 68% involved fentanyl/analogs/tramadol.
- VA statewide drug arrests fell **−7.2%** in 2024 — but **+41.7% among age 65+**

## Statewide context (VSP Crime in Virginia 2024)

- Violent offenses: 16,853 (**−7.0% YoY**)
- Violent crime victims: 19,480 (−6.5% YoY)
- Total property loss: $631.5M
- Hate crime: 340 incidents. **Anti-Jewish bias: 33→84 incidents** (largest movement)
- Assaults on officers: 3,257

## What is FCSO actually doing?

Per local-news coverage (2024-2026):

1. **Drug enforcement** via NWVRDGTF — high volume
2. **Domestic / threat-with-firearm cases** (e.g., Hamrick, April 2024)
3. **School-weapons cases** (student handgun in backpack)
4. **ICE handoffs**: 6 individuals transferred to ICE custody (formal 287(g) MOU status unclear)
5. **Officer-assault during arrest** (Super 8 motel incident)

**Sheriff Lenny Millholland** has been in office since January 1, 2016 (Independent affiliation).

| Rape | 8 | 24 | **+200%** (small-N noise + likely reporting effect) |
| Robbery | 5 | 3 | -2 |

| Burglary | 102 | 54 | **-47%** |

Caveat: at FrCo's population scale, these are noisy. The big movers (rape ↑200%, burglary ↓47%) need 3-5 year context to interpret. **Use rolling averages, not single-year deltas.**

## Year-over-year direction (Winchester, 2023→2024)

- Total crime: **−8% to −13%** across aggregators
- Violent: **−23%** (AreaVibes)
- Property: **−7%**

Direction is consistent across sources.

## What's NOT in the data (important gaps)

- **No homicides in Winchester 2024** — but this is one year; not a trend by itself.
- **No officer-involved shootings / use-of-force incidents** surfaced in local news 2024-2026.
- **No organized-retail-theft** coverage despite the property-crime spike.
- **Recovery Court (drug court)**: 26th Judicial Circuit may not operate one — peer southwest-VA jurisdictions do.
- **Federal cases (WDVA)** originating in FrCo: not extracted (justice.gov press releases blocked).
- **Locality-resolved fatal-overdose data** for FrCo + Winchester 2020-2024: in VDH Open Data datasets; not pulled.
