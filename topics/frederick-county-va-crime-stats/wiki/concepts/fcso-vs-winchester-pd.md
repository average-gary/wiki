## Three load-bearing observations

1. **Both jurisdictions are below VA state violent-crime average** (~255/100k). FrCo and Winchester are not high-violence relative to the state.

2. **Winchester's property crime rate is dramatically higher than the surrounding county** — ~2,329/100k vs FrCo's ~832/100k. Driven primarily by retail-corridor theft (Apple Blossom Mall area). This is a city-vs-county effect, not a "Winchester is dangerous" effect — comparable cities of ~28k pop typically have similar property rates.

3. **Winchester crime fell ~8-13% YoY 2023→2024**, with violent crime down ~23% per AreaVibes. This is **directionally consistent with VA statewide violent-crime drop of −7.0% YoY** and statewide overdose-death drop of −37%. Causal attribution to local policing requires controlling for the supply-side fentanyl collapse.

## Caveats

- All per-100k figures from secondary aggregators. Primary VSP/FBI numbers not yet pulled (PDFs >10MB blocked; CDE is JS SPA). The pattern is robust across aggregators but exact rates may shift slightly when primary data is extracted.
- Small-N noise: 1 vs 0 murders is not statistically meaningful at these populations. Use 3-5 year averages.
- Winchester's property-crime per-100k figures vary widely between aggregators (City-Data shows 156.9 — likely a normalized index, not a raw rate; NeighborhoodScout's raw counts produce ~2,300/100k). **Use raw counts when reporting**, not aggregator "indexes."

## Intra-jurisdiction variance

**Within Frederick County** (CrimeGrade): NE areas ~1-in-246 risk vs. central ~1-in-771. Eastern county ~88 incidents/yr vs. ~7 in western. **Concentration along Winchester/Rt 7/I-81 corridor.**

**Within Winchester**: western Winchester safest (1-in-885); southeastern least safe (1-in-181).
