# NIBRS vs SRS — Why Pre/Post-2021 Crime Stats Don't Compare

In 2021, FBI mandated all U.S. agencies report via the **National Incident-Based Reporting System (NIBRS)** instead of the legacy **Summary Reporting System (SRS)**.

The transition broke trend lines in three structural ways.

## 1. Hierarchy Rule abolished

- **SRS**: only the most serious offense per incident was reported.
- **NIBRS**: ALL offenses per incident are reported.

If a robbery includes both robbery + assault + weapons offense, SRS counted 1 offense (robbery). NIBRS counts 3.

**Mechanically inflates apparent crime counts** even for fully-compliant agencies that didn't change their actual policing.

## 2. Rape definition expanded (~2013)

- **Old SRS**: forcible carnal knowledge of a female
- **New NIBRS**: gender-neutral, expanded definition (includes oral/anal/object penetration; both sexes)

Counts step up at the change point. A "rape rate increase" near the transition is partially definitional.

## 3. New Crimes Against Society category

NIBRS added a third category — **Crimes Against Society** (drug, gambling, prostitution offenses) — that didn't exist in SRS top-line totals.

## Coverage drop

- **Pre-2021 UCR**: ~95% of US population covered by participating agencies
- **NIBRS-only first year (2021)**: roughly **half** the population covered (~52%)
- **Q4 2023**: ~82% recovered after NYPD/LAPD onboarded
- 2022 still had only ~83% of agencies submitting data

Brennan Center quote: *"Reliable government data on crime trends in 2021 may never be available."*

## What this means for FrCo / Winchester

- Any chart spanning 2020-2022 has a **discontinuity at 2021** that is not real-world signal.
- FCSO and Winchester PD reporting status during the transition window — confirm both reported every year 2018-2024. (The VSP UCR landing page publishes reporting status; flagged for follow-up.)
- For long-run trends, treat 2021 as an unreliable point. Use 5-year rolling averages where possible, but expect a structural break around 2021.

## Virginia is fully NIBRS

Virginia transitioned cleanly. Crime in Virginia 2022/2023/2024 (VSP) all use NIBRS Group A / Group B framework. Apples-to-apples for the 2022→2023→2024 trend window — but NOT comparable to pre-2021 SRS-era reporting.
