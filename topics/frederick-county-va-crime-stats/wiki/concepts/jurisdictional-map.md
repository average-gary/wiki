# Jurisdictional Map

## The core fact

**Frederick County and Winchester are two separate jurisdictions, even though Winchester is geographically embedded in Frederick County and is its county seat.** Crime statistics for the two report separately to FBI UCR/NIBRS and to VSP.

## Why: the Virginia independent-city structure

Virginia has **38 independent cities** (more than every other state combined). The 1871 post-Reconstruction Constitution established the structure:

> *"Virginia's thirty-eight independent cities are not politically part of a county, even though geographically they may be completely surrounded by one."*

Winchester is one of these 38. It can be the **county seat of a county it is not part of** — exactly the FrCo/Winchester arrangement.

## The four-agency map

| Agency | Jurisdiction | ORI | Pop served |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Frederick County Sheriff's Office (FCSO)** | Unincorporated FrCo + concurrent in towns | VA0340000 | ~96k |
| **Stephens City Police Department** | Inside Stephens City town limits (concurrent w/ FCSO) | TBD | ~2k |
| **Winchester Police Department** | City of Winchester (independent city, NOT in FrCo) | VA1095000 | ~28k |
| **Virginia State Police** | I-81/I-66 corridors, VSP Area 22 | VA0290000 | (statewide, FrCo coverage in patrol area) |

Towns in Virginia (Stephens City, Middletown) do NOT strip the sheriff of authority the way independent cities do. Sheriff retains **concurrent jurisdiction** in towns. Crime stats from inside Stephens City may appear under either Stephens City PD or FCSO depending on responding agency.

## Why this matters for crime statistics

If a source says "Frederick County crime rate is X":
- **If the source includes Winchester**: it's mixing two demographically distinct jurisdictions (Winchester poverty 21.5%, FrCo poverty 6.8%). Read carefully.
- **If the source is FCSO-only (ORI VA0340000)**: applies to unincorporated FrCo + concurrent town jurisdiction.
- **If the source is Winchester PD-only (ORI VA1095000)**: applies to the independent city only.

A naive reader will conflate them. The data does not.

## Constitutional officers (FrCo, separately elected)

Per Va. Const. Art. VII §4:

- **Sheriff** — Lenny Millholland (Independent), in office since Jan 1, 2016
- Commonwealth's Attorney — Ross Spicer (R)
- Clerk of Circuit Court
- Treasurer
- Commissioner of Revenue

Each is independently elected. **The sheriff is NOT subordinate to the Board of Supervisors.**

## Court structure

- **26th Judicial Circuit** — covers FrCo + Winchester + Clarke + Page + Shenandoah + Warren
- **Joint Judicial Center**, 5 N. Kent St., Winchester
- 8 circuit judges (Chief: Hon. Kevin C. Black)
- **Magistrates** are state employees under the Office of the Executive Secretary, Supreme Court of Virginia — NOT under the sheriff
