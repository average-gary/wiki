# Second's Bark — Bitcoin Magazine (Apr 2026)

**The only mainstream news article that explicitly names mining-pool payouts as an Ark use case.** Across Bitcoin Optech, conference talks, every pool operator's announcements, and the entire 2024–2026 Ark coverage corpus, this single phrase is the public footprint.

## Confirmed facts

- **Team**: Steven Roose (CEO), Erik De Smedt (CTO), both ex-Blockstream. Team of 11.
- **Funding**: **$5.1M from a private investor** (separate from Ark Labs' Tether-led round).
- **Mainnet launch**: "soon" (April 2026); currently signet-only.
- **Use cases enumerated**: "Employer payroll processing with instant finality" and **"Mining pool payout distribution at higher frequencies."**

## Quotes

- Engineer "Grubles": "with a Bark wallet, you can receive some bitcoin and begin doing Lightning payments literally in seconds."
- Acknowledged tradeoff: **"Wallets need to come online at least once a month."**

## Why this matters

The "Wallets need to come online at least once a month" admission is the **structural mismatch** with mining-pool payouts to small/intermittent miners. A Bitaxe that wakes once a quarter to mine for a day will lose its VTXOs to expiration sweep unless the pool runs delegated-refresh (hArk-style watchtower) — which itself reintroduces trust assumptions.

The mining-payout pitch is therefore a Second.tech marketing thesis, not a deployment, partnership, or third-party endorsement. Worth recording as such.
