# TIDES vs FPPS Empirical Experiment (heatpunks 2025)

Rare empirical head-to-head: same hashrate split between OCEAN (TIDES) and Braiins (FPPS) over months, sat-counted by the operator.

## Top-line numbers

- **Braiins FPPS**: 13,905 sats earned over test window
- **OCEAN TIDES**: 13,332 sats earned over the same window
- **Delta**: Braiins +~3.3%

## Caveats

- OCEAN suffered an **85+ hour gap between blocks** early on — single luck event dominated short-window result.
- Variance demonstrably converges only after the 8×D share window fills (mid-July inflection).
- Other participant tracking via Luxor's Hashprice index saw OCEAN outperform 5–15% in different 2025 windows. **Methodology massively shifts conclusions.**

## Operator's verdict

> *"If you're mining in the short term, FPPS is seemingly more profitable. If you're running long term…I expect to see OCEAN be more profitable."*

## Why it matters

- One of the **only public empirical comparisons** of TIDES vs FPPS by an actual miner with hardware.
- Confirms Rosenfeld/Chatzigiannis variance theory at small scale: TIDES variance dominates short-run; convergence is long.
- Counter-data underlines that FPPS's "predictability" advantage is real for short-horizon miners — but the long-horizon argument for non-custodial PPLNS-style schemes (TIDES, SLICE, eHash) is preserved if you're willing to wait.

## Open questions surfaced

- Where does the convergence horizon actually sit for typical home miners? (1 month? 3 months? 1 year?)
- Does DATUM fee discount (1%) recover OCEAN's gap to Braiins fully?
- Does SLICE/DMND look the same as TIDES at small scale, or does JD-binding change variance properties?
