# FiberPool (Sakurai & Shudo 2025)

Recent (2025) preprint formally analyzing decentralized pool reward properties. Closest academic frame for evaluating p2poolv2's PPLNS variant.

## Four formal properties analyzed

FiberPool's Proportional payment scheme is verified for:

1. **Mining fairness** — reward proportional to hashrate contribution
2. **Budget balance** — total payout = total reward (no over/under-issuance)
3. **Reward stability** — bounded variance over time
4. **Incentive compatibility** — no profitable deviation from honest mining

These are **the exact axes any decentralized PPLNS variant — including p2poolv2 — must satisfy**.

## Architecture: storage chain + child chain

Decomposes into:
- **Smart contract layer** for share verification
- **Storage chain** as the share ledger
- **Child chain** for reward fees

The storage-chain-as-share-ledger pattern is the structural cousin of p2poolv2's share-chain.

## Comparison vs. P2Pool and SmartPool

FiberPool explicitly identifies:
- **P2Pool**: "scalability and security issues"
- **SmartPool**: lacks budget balance + imposes fees

p2poolv2 attempts to fix P2Pool's scalability via uncles + atomic swaps, while preserving P2Pool's no-fee model. Whether p2poolv2 satisfies all 4 FiberPool properties is **an open question** — no formal proof exists for p2poolv2's PPLNS variant.

## Why ingest

Provides a 2025 baseline for evaluating decentralized PPLNS variants on formal properties; lets the wiki cite "what fairness/budget-balance means" instead of hand-waving.

## Negative finding

**No academic paper directly names p2poolv2 or 256 Foundation.** No peer-reviewed work formally verifies share-chain payout semantics with uncles. The TLA+ spec at `p2poolv2/spec/ShareChain.tla` covers share generation/validation/longest-chain but **NOT payout math** — a real research gap.
