# Automation Economics — HMLV, Cobot ROI, Labor

**Mach Medical single-piece flow (Modern Machine Shop):**
- Cell: **Okuma 5-axis + Universal Robots cobot + CMM**, orchestrated by **Flexxbotics** for single-piece flow.
- **"Flex lot sizing"** handles **batches as small as one part** — the answer to high-mix ortho. Strategy: machine partially-finished implants in batches, finish as individual orders land.
- **Lead time 20 weeks → 3 weeks**; **scrap <1%**; parts held to **<25% of the allowed 1 mm tolerance**. In-process ultrasonic cleaning + closed-loop CMM offset + 6-slot nonconforming buffer. Current **unattended runtime 36 hours**, expandable (lights-out trajectory).

**Cobot ROI (Toolcraft UR5e; MedPro Devices):**
- Toolcraft UR5e: output **255 → 370 parts/week (~45%)**, **production costs −23%**, **ROI in 12 months**, 30-µm repeatability, force/torque CNC fixture loading; enabled a third shift the firm "couldn't provide the workforce" for.
- MedPro Devices (hip stems, spinal cages, knee, Ti + PEEK): two cobots tending CNC → **+29% CNC uptime in 60 days, zero quality deviations**, ISO 7 cleanroom, automated cycle logging for FDA/ISO traceability.

**Labor model:** automation = **"force multiplier," not replacement** — senior machinists reassigned from load/unload to overseeing multiple cells; directly addresses the skilled-machinist shortage. MDDI survey: ~half of medical device firms plan AI/automation amid labor crisis + retiring skilled workers.

**Key economic insight:** ortho's low-volume/high-variety profile (vs automotive) makes **hard automation uneconomic**; the winning pattern is **flexible robotic cells + cobot tending + single-piece flow**, where automation improves PQ repeatability (high Cpk) and born-digital traceability simultaneously.
