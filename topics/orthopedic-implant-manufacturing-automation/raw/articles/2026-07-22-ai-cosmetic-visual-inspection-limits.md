# AI Cosmetic Visual Inspection — Help & Limits

**Why cosmetic inspection resists automation (DWFritz + IVS):**
- Defects (scratches, dents, chips, micro-cracks — e.g., "a scratch on a femoral knee implant") are **random, varied, often microscopic** on curved/textured/reflective surfaces that defeat conventional rule-based vision; traditional vision programming took "months of coding."
- Human inspectors suffer **visual fatigue and saccadic oversight** (inconsistency).
- **Deep learning** learns from example images instead of hand-coded rules: claims **up to 90% higher defect-detection rate vs humans**, ~50% productivity gain, 100% coverage.

**The validation catch (IVS — the honest limit):**
- DL often outputs only **pass/fail with no quantitative metrics** — a "black box" you must trust "blindly," creating **FDA/GAMP validation challenges** vs traditional vision (measurements, grey levels, feature counts are easier to validate).
- **Two-tier strategy**: borderline results routed to human operators — full autonomy for cosmetic judgment is not yet trusted.

**Convergence (KITOV.ai):** hybrid classical-3D-vision + DL detects scratches/dents/discoloration/corrosion/micro-cracks **down to 50 µm**; robot inspects all six sides in one cycle via **AI-optimized CAD-derived paths**; two-stage detect-then-measure (AI flags → bright/dark-field vision to exact location); integrates **CMM-Manager as GD&T engine** (ISO/ASME Y14.5). Unifies cosmetic + dimensional + functional in one robotic cell; targets medical device manufacturing.
