# Swiss-Type Machining of Bone Screws

- **~95% of bone screws are made on Swiss-type (sliding-headstock) lathes.** The rigid guide bushing sits millimeters from the cutting zone so unsupported length L≈0; deflection scales **δ ∝ L³**, so Swiss "mathematically eliminates" deflection on the extreme L/D of screws — the physical reason Swiss dominates conventional lathes. (Corroborated as ~95% of bone screws, dental implants, spinal fixation.)
- Three alloys → challenges: **Ti-6Al-4V ELI** (high heat, permanent screws), **316LVM** (work-hardening, plates/instruments), **CoCrMo** (high tool wear, joint replacements).
- Tolerances: **single-digit microns (±0.005 mm)**; surface finish **≤0.4 µm via thread whirling**; **sphericity within 0.003 mm** for pedicle-screw heads.
- Key automated operations: **thread whirling** (eccentric carbide-insert ring completes a thread in one pass — high-throughput OD threading), **Torx drive-interface profiling** with live tooling, **Y-axis milling of self-tapping flutes**.
- Bar-fed continuous unattended production; integrated deburring key (avoids manual post-process deburring).
