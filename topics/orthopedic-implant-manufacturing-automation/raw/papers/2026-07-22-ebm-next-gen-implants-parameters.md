# Next-Gen Orthopaedic Implants by Additive Manufacturing (EBM)

Peer-reviewed, dense on process parameters. PMC3432366.

**Materials & properties:**
- **Ti-6Al-4V ELI** — 6.04% Al, 4.05% V; powder ~30 µm; melts ~1630 °C; solid modulus **E = 110 GPa**.
- **Co-29Cr-6Mo / ASTM F75** — powder ~40 µm; melts ~1430 °C; solid **E = 210 GPa**.

**EBM process parameters:**
- Layer thickness **50–100 µm**; preheat passes >10⁴ mm/s reaching **600–800 °C** (hot vacuum build → low residual stress, near-isotropic); melt scan in vacuum from 3D-CAD; build envelope up to ~0.2 m³.

**Porous lattice = the killer app (stress-shielding match):**
- Stiffness scales as **E = E₀·(ρ/ρ₀)ⁿ**, n ≈ 2.0–2.3. A relative-density-0.5 knee component yields **~19 GPa** — near cortical bone, and **impossible to achieve in solid metal**. This is the physics reason AM is chosen over machining/casting for titanium.

**Microstructure & post-processing:**
- As-built Ti-6Al-4V: acicular α (vertical) and α′-martensite in thin struts (Vickers 4.5–5.2 GPa). Co-29Cr-6Mo: columnar Cr₂₃C₆ carbides.
- **HIP** (anneal 1200 °C/4 h, homogenize 1220 °C/4 h) dissolves carbides; for Co-29Cr-6Mo elongation improves ~10× (3.6% → 32%).
- **Articulating surfaces still need machining/polishing after AM.**
- ~1,000 Ti-6Al-4V EBM acetabular shells implanted (CE approved, Adler/Ala Ortho 2007) — porous-AM clinical maturity.

**Automation takeaway:** the value of AM is the geometry (porous lattices, patient-specific) machining can't make; the cost is a mandatory HIP + heat-treat + machining post-processing tail.
