# Morphometry of Commercial 3D-Printed Ti Acetabular Cups

Peer-reviewed, open access. PMC9639285. Measured **12 final-production titanium acetabular cups from 6 manufacturers** — real commercial parts, not test coupons.

**Measured geometry:**
- Strut thickness **0.28–0.65 mm**; porous-layer depth **0.57–11.51 mm**; median pore size **0.74–1.87 mm**; **porosity 34–85%**.
- **5 of 6 manufacturers used TPMS** (triply periodic minimal surface) engineered lattices; **one used a stochastic lattice** — documents the two competing lattice-design philosophies.

**Osseointegration design rationale:**
- Trabecular bone is 50–90% porous, pores 50 µm–1 mm; bone ingrowth maxes ~2.5 mm depth (design basis for porous-layer depth).

**Automation/quality flag:**
- **Residual titanium powder trapped in pores** is an unresolved post-processing (depowdering) concern for porous implants — a clinically significant automation gap (unreleased powder can cause inflammation, impede vascularization). Corroborated by CT-scanning sources that detect trapped/unfused powder as lighter-gray voxels.
