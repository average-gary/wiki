# Crosslinked / Vitamin-E UHMWPE Bearings

Peer-reviewed. PMC2716092. The polyethylene liner/insert story.

**Base material & forming:**
- **GUR1050 UHMWPE** resin (Ticona), consolidated as isostatically molded/ram-extruded bar stock.
- Liners/inserts are **machined from consolidated stock** — UHMWPE is one implant material still fundamentally machined, not molded-to-net.

**Crosslinking (wear resistance):**
- **Gamma irradiation 85 kGy** (hip-sim/tensile) and **100 kGy** (rim-impingement). Radiation cleaves C-H/C-C bonds → free radicals that recombine into crosslinks in the amorphous phase.

**The oxidation problem & stabilization:**
- Free radicals trapped in the **crystalline phase** cause long-term oxidative embrittlement → a stabilization step is mandatory.
- **Remelting vs annealing tradeoff:** post-irradiation melting eliminates free radicals but "reduces mechanical properties and fatigue strength" via crystallinity loss. Vitamin E avoids this penalty.
- **Vitamin E by diffusion** (post-irradiation, since pre-blending lowers crosslinking efficiency): doped at **120 °C**, homogenized under argon; times scale with thickness (4.9 mm liner = 2 h dope + 24 h homogenize).
- Final: packaged in argon with O₂ scavenger, gamma-sterilized.

**Automation takeaway:** the machining is automatable, but the process is gated by **long thermal-diffusion batch steps** (hours per batch) and inert-atmosphere handling. Standard: **ASTM F648 / ISO 5834**.
