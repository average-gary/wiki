# BIOLOX delta Ceramic Femoral Heads — Material Rationale

OEM technical PDF (DePuy). Hard numbers on the ceramic route.

**Composition (ZPTA — zirconia-toughened, platelet-reinforced alumina):**
- **~74% alumina, ~25% zirconia**, plus chromium oxide (restores hardness lost to zirconia addition) and strontium oxide (forms SrAl₁₂O₁₉ platelets during sintering that dissipate crack energy).

**Manufacturing levers = grain size, sintered density, porosity:**
- Alumina matrix grain size **<1.5 µm**; zirconia particles **0.2–0.6 µm**; improved via HIPing + tempering. Grain-size reduction directly raises strength.

**Properties:**
- **Flexural strength 1150 MPa** (vs CoCr 700, stainless 800, BIOLOX forte alumina 550).
- **Fracture toughness 8.5 MPa·m^0.5** (vs forte 4.3).
- **Burst strength 101 kN** (vs early alumina 50, forte 55; FDA guidance 46 kN).

**Transformation toughening ("airbag"):** a crack hitting a tetragonal zirconia particle triggers tetragonal→monoclinic transformation with **~3–4% volume increase** that closes the microcrack.

**Why the sinter-then-grind route (automation implication):**
- Ceramics are brittle (low fracture toughness) — cutting tools would fracture them. So they are **near-net sintered, then diamond-ground / lapped / polished**, never conventionally machined.
- "Scratches and microcracks result from polishing and finishing" — finishing defects are themselves **crack initiators**, which is why finishing must be tightly controlled. Standard: **ISO 6474-2 / ASTM F2393**.
