# Porous Tantalum — CVD vs PM vs AM

Peer-reviewed (Frontiers Bioeng. Biotech. 2023). Definitive route comparison.

**CVD = mainstream commercial route (= Zimmer Trabecular Metal):**
- Pure Ta reacts with Cl₂ → gaseous TaCl₅; H₂ reduces it to deposit Ta onto a **carbon skeleton**.
- Result: **porosity 75–85%, pore size ~400–600 µm, Ta coating 40–60 µm thick, E = 1–30 GPa, compressive strength 10–200 MPa** — tuned to trabecular bone.

**Powder metallurgy** (space-holder NaCl/rice flour; compact 350–450 MPa; sinter 1300–2000 °C in vacuum):
- Pore 100–400 µm but many **closed pores** limit interconnectivity — inferior to CVD.

**Additive (SLM/EBM):**
- Enables patient-specific porosity/geometry; example SLM: porosity 79.7%, E = 1.22 GPa, compressive strength 3.61 MPa.

**Why tantalum constrains automation:**
- Refractory, **melting point ~2980 °C**, reacts with C and O at high temperature → severely limits conventional melt/machine routes. Combined with cost/scarcity, that is why **CVD-on-carbon (not machining)** dominates. AM is the emerging alternative.
