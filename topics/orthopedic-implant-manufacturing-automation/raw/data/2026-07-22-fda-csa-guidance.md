# FDA Computer Software Assurance (CSA)

Two specialist regulatory-consultancy analyses of the final guidance.

- Title: **"Computer Software Assurance for Production and Quality System Software."** Shift from documentation-heavy **CSV** to a **risk-based CSA** model. Principles: **"Integration. Modernization. Proportionality."**
- Endorses a spectrum of assurance activities: **scripted testing, exploratory/ad-hoc testing, experience-based testing, vendor testing, continuous monitoring.** Key line: **"Use the right level of rigor for the risk, not a one-size-fits-all validation package"**; **"No one is asking for binders of screenshots."**
- Four-step risk framework: (1) identify **intended use**; (2) determine **risk** of software failure to process/product (+ cybersecurity, data integrity, operational continuity); (3) select proportionate **assurance activities**; (4) establish the **record**. Recognizes "software creates risk even when it's working exactly as designed."

**Why this is the pivotal enabler of maximal automation:**
- CSA **lowers the validation cost** of adopting automation software. Under old CSV, validating a new MES/vision/AI-inspection tool could cost more than the tool, discouraging automation.
- CSA lets manufacturers **leverage vendor testing** and reserve heavy scripted validation only for **high-risk, product-quality-affecting** functions — directly widening how far you can economically automate.
- Transition: guidance still cites current QSReg; FDA will update once **QMSR** is effective.
