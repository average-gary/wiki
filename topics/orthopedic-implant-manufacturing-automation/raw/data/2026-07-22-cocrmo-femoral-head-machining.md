# CoCrMo Femoral Head Manufacturing (Croom Medical)

- CoCrMo femoral heads start as an **investment casting exceeding 50 HRC**, poor thermal conductivity — "among the most difficult to machine"; tools deteriorate rapidly, thermal management critical.
- **Route: CoCrMo casting → 5-axis mill-turn CNC → robotic polishing → CMM inspection.** 5-axis cuts the sphere and bores the internal Morse taper (neck-length options) in one setup.
- Spherical surface must hit **sphericity within ±0.004 mm** to avoid accelerated joint wear.
- **Hand-polishing bottleneck** is the explicit automation target: skilled-technician polishing gave inconsistent part-to-part results; Croom replaced it with **robotic buffing arms, abrasive flow machining (AFM), and automated electropolishing** for repeatable mirror finishes.
- Validation under **ISO 13485 + FDA QSR**, IQ/OQ/PQ requiring ≥3 full batches; CMM verifies OD, spherical form, taper.

Cast **F75** (femoral heads/condyles via investment casting) vs wrought **F1537**.
