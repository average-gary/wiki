# Automated Implant Finishing — the hand-polish displacement

The counterintuitive core finding: the "notoriously manual" final polish is being displaced by (1) force-controlled robotic belt/buff cells and (2) automated mass/drag/stream finishing + dry electropolishing.

**DLyte dry electropolishing (ActOn):** CoCr femoral knees on **DLyte PRO500**, **24 knee implants per cycle**, **Ra 0.2 µm → below 0.05 µm** (>80% reduction), **60 min/24 pieces**, cost driven to "€2 for 5 years at 40,000 pcs/yr" (>60% production-cost reduction). No edge rounding (preserves geometry). Explicitly replaces "robotised belt polishing or abrasive polishing" and prior manual belt/ceramic/plastic/nutshell stages.

**Rösler drag finishing (R6/1000 SF):** workpieces on rotary spindles on a carousel dragged through stationary media — **up to 40× higher intensity vs vibratory**. Named case: **Smith+Nephew Penang** runs two cells of three R6/1000 SF units each on **CoCr knee implants** (~50% higher productivity via optimized holders). Three-step media: ceramic (remove grinding lines) → wet plastic (pre-polish) → dry corn-cob/nutshell + compound. 1,080 mm bowl, six spindles, **18 parts simultaneously**; auto centrifuge + monitoring of ≤14 params. Achievable **Ra 0.8 µin (0.02 µm)** on knee femorals/acetabular cups (Rösler ortho part-5); automated drag finisher with robotic load/unload available.

**OTEC stream finishing:** workpiece clamped, lowered into rotating media container; **Ra down to 0.01 µm**, even in smallest chip flutes; **PULSE FINISHING** reaches Rpk 0.1 µm in <1 min single stage. SF-Automation variant with integrated loading for takt-time lines; well-suited to **AM part finishing** & internal geometries where line-of-sight polishing fails.

**PushCorp active force control (WHY robotic polishing works now):** an active-force-compliance device with a sliding carriage maintains constant contact force independent of the robot arm (compensates only end-effector mass) → beats robot-arm-only force control on contours. Consistent force **extends abrasive life 200–300%**. Servo spindles: 100% duty cycle, constant RPM/torque → uniform finish. (Corroborated by Acme Mfg "programmable compliance in all abrasive belt heads" and AV&R.)
