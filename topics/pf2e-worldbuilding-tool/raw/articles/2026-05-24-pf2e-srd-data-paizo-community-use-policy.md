# Paizo Community Use Policy — Key Extracts

**Last updated**: 2024-08-22 (no 2025/2026 updates as of fetch).

## What Fan Creators MAY Use

- Materials in the Community Use Package + official product cover images
- Product descriptions from packaging or paizo.com listings
- Content from Paizo Blog and Web Fiction (with exceptions)
- Artwork and maps during Twitch/YouTube streams of adventures
- Descriptive references to trademarks, character names, locations, plots, dialogue
- Personal-use printed assets

## What Fan Creators May NOT Use

- Planet Stories publications, Pathfinder Comics excerpts
- Blog photographs (third-party photographer rights)
- Unpublished artwork or maps
- "Adult", offensive, or content unsuitable for minors
- Modified logos, icons, or product covers (grayscale OK)
- Anything paywalled or behind email/survey opt-ins

## Required Attribution

> "[Project name] uses trademarks and/or copyrights owned by Paizo Inc., used under Paizo's Community Use Policy (paizo.com/licenses/communityuse). We are expressly prohibited from charging you to use or access this content."

Plus reproduce original author/artist credits and maintain visible contact information.

## Commercial Use Restrictions

- **"Your project must be free"** — paywalls forbidden entirely.
- **Allowed**: Patreon donations, ad revenue from YouTube/Twitch, identified sponsor support.
- **Not allowed**: Charging directly for access.

## Implications for a PF2e Worldbuilding/LLM Tool

Community Use is the lever that lets a tool reference **Golarion-specific** content — deity names (Sarenrae, Iomedae, Asmodeus), nations (Cheliax, Absalom), iconic NPCs, plot references — that ORC alone cannot cover because they are Reserved Material. The tradeoff is hard: **the tool cannot be paywalled**. A worldbuilding tool that wants to ship Golarion as the example setting must remain free; a SaaS subscription model would need to either (a) keep Paizo IP behind a free tier, (b) move to **Pathfinder Infinite** for setting content, or (c) ship only generic ORC mechanics and let users plug in their own setting names.
