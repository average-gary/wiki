# Three adjacent tools

## Campfire Writing
- 18 modules: word processor, encyclopedia, maps, calendar, "Systems" (flowcharts), character sheets, relationship webs, moodboards, timelines, research, share/co-write.
- Web + desktop + free mobile writing app.
- Pricing not on landing — known to use a "buy modules" model (one-time per module, plus an optional sub).
- **No AI features mentioned.**
- **Audience: novelists, not GMs.** Worldbuilding modules are useful but RPG-blind (no statblocks, no encounter math).

## Plottr
- Desktop-first (Win/Mac); cloud Pro tier adds web/mobile.
- 40+ outline templates (Hero's Journey, Romance Beat, Mystery Formula, etc.).
- Series Bible for multi-book continuity.
- **Explicitly anti-AI**: "When most writing tools are scrambling as fast as they can to add AI, Plottr is committed to rejecting AI features."
- Worldbuilding tools (magic systems, cultures, locations, languages) exist but are secondary to plot timeline.
- Lifetime pricing available.
- **Audience: plotters/pantsers in the novel-writing space.**

## Inkarnate (mapmaker, not wiki)
| Tier | Price | Assets | Export | Maps |
|------|-------|--------|--------|------|
| Hobby | Free | 1k+ | 2K | 3 |
| Creator | $7.99/mo | 30k+ | 8K | 100, personal use |
| Studio | $14.99/mo | 30k+ | 16K | unlimited, commercial license |

- 23,400+ HD fantasy assets.
- Cloneable community maps via Explore page.
- **No AI features.**
- Direct competitors: Wonderdraft (one-time desktop), Dungeondraft (battlemaps, one-time), Worldographer/Hexographer (hex+political maps).

## Cross-cutting observations
- **The "no AI" stance is a deliberate market position** (Plottr, Inkarnate, Campfire all silent or hostile) — there is room for an explicitly LLM-native tool to win the AI-curious segment.
- **Mapmakers and wikis are separate products today.** Inkarnate maps don't auto-link into any wiki; LegendKeeper has nested maps but no asset library to rival Inkarnate. A PF2e tool could embed/import Inkarnate exports + Wonderdraft `.dd2vtt` files and unify them with PF2e statblocks/locations.
- Pricing benchmarks: $4–$10/mo is the sweet spot users tolerate for hobby creative tools. Lifetime/one-time pricing (Plottr, Foundry) is increasingly the differentiator vs. SaaS fatigue.

## Relevance
Confirms two market gaps: (a) no integrated wiki + map + statblock + LLM product exists; (b) lifetime / BYO-key pricing is wide-open competitive terrain in 2026.
