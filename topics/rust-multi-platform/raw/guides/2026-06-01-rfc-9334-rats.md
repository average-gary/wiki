# RFC 9334 — RATS Architecture (Informational)

Defines the role taxonomy any "device identity attestation" system has to make decisions about.

## Four roles

- **Attester** — the device producing Evidence
- **Verifier** — applies appraisal policy to Evidence, produces Attestation Result
- **Relying Party** — consumes Attestation Result to make trust decisions
- **Endorser** — typically the manufacturer; vouches for attestation key provisioning

## Two topologies

- **Passport Model** — Attester collects Attestation Result, then presents to RPs (cacheable, offline-friendly)
- **Background-Check Model** — RP forwards Evidence to Verifier on demand (real-time, requires Verifier connectivity)

For edge fleets: **Passport Model** is the right default — receipts travel with the device and don't require Verifier round-trip per RPC.

## Three freshness mechanisms

| Mechanism | Needs | State | Edge fit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Timestamps** | synchronized trusted clock | low | bad (TEE rarely has trusted clock) |
| **Nonces** | per-request challenge from verifier | high (per-request) | bad for offline / async |
| **Epoch IDs** | shared epoch beacon | medium | good — reusable across entities |

## Long-lived attestation keys

"Attestation key provisioning is the process that occurs in the factory or elsewhere to establish signing key material on the device and the validation key material off the device." Direct alignment with the **Mender preauthorization** + **Balena fleet provisioning key** patterns: identity is injected at manufacture, not generated at runtime.

## Layered isolation rule

"Target Environment should not be able to tamper with the Attesting Environment that measures it." If you can't enforce this with hardware (TPM/TrustZone/SE), the attestation is honor-system.

## Privacy caveat

Evidence "reveal[s] a great deal of information about the internal state of a device as well as potentially any users." Consider whether what you sign is what you wanted to disclose.

## Why it matters

Defines the role taxonomy and freshness primitives any "single-slot fleet identity" design has to make decisions about — passport vs background-check directly maps to whether receipts travel with the device or are fetched per RPC. Critical reading.
