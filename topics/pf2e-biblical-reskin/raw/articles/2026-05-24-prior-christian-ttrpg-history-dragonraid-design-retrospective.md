## Design intent

- "The core idea of DragonRaid is to use a fairly classical fantasy roleplaying game setup to create situations where Christian learning is possible."
- Stated player outcomes: "resist sin, counter deceptive arguments, memorize Scripture, and build moral and spiritual character."
- Designed explicitly as a **Protestant Christian alternative to D&D** during the Satanic Panic.

## Reviewer ambivalence (the productive tension)

- "As a Christian teaching tool, DragonRaid conveys the lessons of the Gospel while, as a roleplaying game it contains several interesting and relatively innovative design features."
- Two distinct value lenses (catechetical vs. mechanical) — a critic can praise one and dismiss the other independently.

## Satanic Panic context

- Even as an *alternative* to D&D, "the creators received a lot of blowback for even trying such a thing during the Satanic Panic." Christian audiences distrusted any fantasy-RPG framing regardless of content.

## Relevance

Confirms the core lesson for the PF2e biblical reskin: the **target audience itself was hostile** in the 1980s. The 2026 audience is friendlier, but the trap of "preachy mechanics dressed as gameplay" still defines the failure mode.
