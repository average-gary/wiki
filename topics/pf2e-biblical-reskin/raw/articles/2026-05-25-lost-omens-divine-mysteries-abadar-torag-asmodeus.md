# Abadar (deity-277) — Divine Mysteries pg. 34

- **Edicts** (3): "bring civilization to the frontiers, earn wealth through hard work and trade, follow the rule of law"
- **Anathema** (3): "engage in banditry or piracy, steal, undermine a law-abiding court"
- **Sanctification**: **can choose holy or unholy** (rare flexibility — only true neutrals get this)
- **Divine Font**: **Harm or Heal** (chosen at character creation; tracks sanctification)
- **Domains** (primary, 4 + alternate, 4): Cities, Earth, Travel, Wealth / Creation, Duty, Metal, Toil
- **Favored Weapon**: Crossbow
- **Religious symbol**: golden key; **sacred animal**: monkey

# Torag (deity-281) — Divine Mysteries

- **Epithet**: **Father of Creation** (legacy was "The Forge Father" — Remaster shifted to a more cosmological/creator-deity title)
- **Edicts** (4): "be honorable and forthright, keep your word, respect the forge, serve your people"
- **Anathema** (3): "tell lies or cheat someone, intentionally create inferior works, **show continued mercy to the enemies of your people when such enemies prove they are undeserving**"
  - Remaster expanded the legacy "show mercy to the enemies of your people" — adds nuance ("continued", "when... undeserving"). Less crusader, more conditional.
- **Sanctification**: can choose holy
- **Religious symbol**: iron hammer; **sacred animal**: badger

# Asmodeus (deity-275) — Divine Mysteries

- **Edicts** (3): "negotiate contracts to your best advantage, rule tyrannically and torture weaker beings, show subservience to your betters"
- **Anathema** (3): "break a contract, **share power with the weak** [Remaster], insult Asmodeus by showing mercy to your enemies"
  - Legacy was "free a slave"; Remaster updates to "share power with the weak" — abstracts away from the specific archaic act, broadens to a cosmic disposition.
- **Sanctification**: **must choose unholy** (mandatory)
- **Divine Font**: Harm
- **Religious symbol**: red pentagram; **sacred animal**: serpent

## Pattern observations across the sanctification spectrum

| Sanctification value | Examples | Mechanic |
|----------------------|----------|----------|
| `must choose unholy` | Asmodeus, Lamashtu, Rovagug, Zon-Kuthon | Mandatory unholy; Harm font |
| `can choose unholy` | (some lesser/morally complex) | Optional |
| `can choose holy or unholy` | Abadar, Gorum (Remaster) | Flexible — neutral major deity |
| `can choose holy` | Iomedae, Sarenrae, Erastil, Torag, Shelyn | Optional — "good" alignment lineage |
| `none` | **Pharasma**, Gozreh, Nethys (some) | No champion path; field is empty |

## Anathema tone-shift pattern

The Remaster systematically softened or generalized legacy anathema where the legacy phrasing was archaic, specific to slavery/feudalism, or excessively absolute:

- "rob a tomb" → "take from the dead in bad faith" (Pharasma)
- "free a slave" → "share power with the weak" (Asmodeus)
- "show mercy to enemies" → "show **continued** mercy when **undeserving**" (Torag)

This pattern matters for a YHWH entry: **anathema should not be drafted as Iron-Age literal prohibitions** ("do not eat shellfish") but as **the underlying ethical violation**, framed in language that survives across moral frameworks.
