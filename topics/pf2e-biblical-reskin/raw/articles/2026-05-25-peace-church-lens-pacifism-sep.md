# SEP Pacifism — key findings

- **Absolute vs. contingent pacifism**: absolute rejects *all* violence unconditionally (problematic edge case: defending against rights violations); contingent/conditional rejects particular wars on prudential or just-war grounds. Anabaptist and Quaker traditions sit on the *absolute* end; just-war Christianity (Augustine → Aquinas → Niebuhr) sits on the contingent end. Both claim Christian warrant — this is *the* internal Christian dispute on violence.
- **Hauerwas's framing**: pacifism is "a theological position" intertwined with eschatological faith — i.e., Christians can renounce violence because they believe history is ultimately God's, not theirs to secure by force. This is structurally the opposite of "realist" theologies that treat the world as needing to be held together by Christian use of coercive power.
- **Yoder on Constantinianism**: just-war theory is itself a *post-Constantinian* compromise; the ante-Nicene church was overwhelmingly pacifist. Recovering pre-Constantinian ethics is the Anabaptist project.
- **Pacifism is not passivism**: SEP stresses *active* nonviolent resistance — protest, civil disobedience, community building, reconciliation. Gandhi, MLK, the velvet revolutions, Lithuania 1990–91 are catalogued as historical successes. This is the design lever: pacifist play is not "do nothing," it's "intervene without violence."
- **Standard objections** (and standard responses) — useful as the *design pushback list*:
  - Cowardice / free-rider (you benefit from soldiers' protection) → pacifists willingly accept suffering; contribute via nonviolent service.
  - "Clean hands" unworldliness → transcendent values supersede temporal pragmatism.
  - Ineffectiveness against Nazis / determined aggressors → historically nonviolent campaigns *do* succeed; means-ends unity prevents moral compromise.
  - Betrayal / treason → transcendent allegiance (Tolstoy, Hauerwas) supersedes nation-state claims.
- **Other religious traditions converge**: Hindu / Jain / Buddhist *ahimsa*, Islamic pacifist strands. A peace-church reskin can borrow design language from a much wider field than just Christian sources.
