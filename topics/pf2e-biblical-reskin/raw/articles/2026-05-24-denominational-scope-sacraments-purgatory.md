# Sacraments, Purgatory, Marian Theology — Comparative

## Sacraments

| Tradition | Number | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Catholic | 7 | Baptism, Confirmation, Eucharist, Penance, Matrimony, Holy Orders, Anointing of the Sick |
| Orthodox | 7 (loosely) | "Anything the Church does as Church is sacramental" — number not strictly enforced |
| Anglican | 2 + 5 lesser | Baptism + Eucharist as "Sacraments of the Gospel"; the other five "commonly called sacraments" |
| Lutheran | 2 or 3 | Baptism + Eucharist; Confession sometimes included |
| Reformed | 2 | Baptism + Lord's Supper; explicitly anti-transubstantiation |
| Evangelical/Pentecostal | "Ordinances" 2 | Believer participation framing rather than sacramental |

## Purgatory

- **Catholic**: condition (not place) of purification for those who die in grace but imperfectly purified. Living can assist via prayer, masses, indulgences.
- **Orthodox**: rejects the term "purgatory" but affirms intermediate state and prayer for the dead; some hold Aerial Toll Houses.
- **Anglican**: 39 Articles XXII calls purgatory "a fond thing, vainly invented."
- **Lutheran**: rejects purgatory; affirms intermediate state (Hades = Paradise + Gehenna).
- **Reformed/Evangelical**: rejects entirely.

## Marian dogmas (Catholic)
1. Theotokos / Mother of God — Council of Ephesus 431 (**shared with Orthodox**)
2. Perpetual Virginity — Constantinople II 553 (**shared with Orthodox, classic Anglican, Luther**)
3. Immaculate Conception — Pius IX 1854 (**Catholic only**; Orthodox reject)
4. Assumption — Pius XII 1950 (**Catholic only**; Orthodox have parallel "Dormition")

*Hyperdulia* (Mary) > *dulia* (other saints) > but neither is *latria* (worship, God only).

## Design implications
- **Sacraments as mechanics**: PF2e ritual system maps perfectly. Catholic/Orthodox = seven ritual types; Reformed = two; this directly constrains class options.
- **Purgatory as plane**: Catholic version → abstract transitional plane. Orthodox toll-houses → adventureable. Protestant → nonexistent (collapse to heaven/hell binary).
- **Saint intercession**: Catholic/Orthodox unlock named-saint Champion causes (St Michael, St George, St Mary of Egypt, etc.). Protestant cuts this entirely — Champions must be cause-based not patron-based.
- **Mary**: Catholic-Orthodox setting can have a "Theotokos" archetype (Queen of Heaven angelic intercessor). Protestant setting cannot without breaking the lens.
