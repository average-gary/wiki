# Pathfinder Second Edition Remaster Project announcement

**Source:** Paizo community blog, April 2023.

## What the announcement says

The post frames the Remaster as:

- A consolidation of four years of errata and balance updates into four new hardcover rulebooks (Player Core, GM Core, Monster Core, Player Core 2) released Nov 2023 – July 2024.
- A **transition off the OGL** to Paizo's new ORC license.
- Surgical excision of "alignment and a small number of nostalgic creatures, spells, and magic items exclusive to the OGL."
- Strong reassurance of **backward compatibility**: the core rules system is "unchanged"; existing PF2e content remains usable.

## What the announcement does NOT say

- No design rationale for *why* alignment specifically was chosen for removal beyond the OGL-flight framing.
- No mention of sanctification, holy/unholy, spirit damage, or any of the replacement mechanics.
- No theological, cosmological, or setting-ideology motivation.
- No mention of monotheism, pluralism, privatio boni, Augustinian metaphysics, or any worldbuilding consideration.

## Significance for the gap-closing inquiry

This is the **negative-space confirmation** that we needed: the founding public document of the Remaster project itself frames alignment removal as **licensing-driven** with **no theological or worldbuilding motivation**.

Combined with [[2026-05-25-sanctification-designer-commentary-bonner-champion-preview]] (which gives only mechanical flexibility as rationale) and [[2026-05-25-sanctification-designer-commentary-loza-divine-mysteries-interview]] (which calls sanctification an "egregious" backward-compatibility break, not a theological feature), the public record is consistent:

> **The sanctification framework was chosen because Paizo needed *something* to replace alignment-locked tenets after stripping the OGL "alignment" mechanic, and "holy/unholy" was the most generic divine-vs-anti-divine vocabulary available.** The theological richness Christian readers may notice (privation, sanctification-as-deity-conformity, spirit-as-non-mental soul-damage) is not the designers' stated intent; it is emergent.
