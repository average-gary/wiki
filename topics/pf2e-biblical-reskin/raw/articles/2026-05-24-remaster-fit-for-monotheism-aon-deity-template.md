# Deity stat block — primary source notes

## Per-deity fields

- **Edicts** (encouraged behavior — open-ended prose)
- **Anathema** (forbidden behavior — open-ended prose)
- **Sanctification** (holy / unholy / none / "can choose between …")
- **Divine Font** (healing / harmful / "can choose")
- **Domains** (4-8 from the domain list, e.g. creation, family, knowledge)
- **Favored Weapon**
- **Divine Skill** (one skill)
- **Divine Ability** (Wis or Cha typically — used by Raised by Belief background)
- **Cleric Spells** (deity-specific spell list, often 3-5 spells)
- **Pantheon** (optional grouping — purely flavor)
- **Deity Category** (e.g. Inner Sea, Eldritch Apotheoses, etc.)

## Devotee benefits (longer entries)

- Most major deities also list "benefits available to the most ardent devotees" — Champion devotion spells, divine intercessions (minor and moderate boons + curses).

## Monotheism implications

- The stat block is fully data-driven; nothing forces multiple deities.
- A monotheistic reskin needs ONE deity entry: name = YHWH/God/the Lord, edicts (e.g. "love God; love neighbor; tell the truth; uphold justice; care for the poor"), anathema (e.g. "worship idols; murder; bear false witness; oppress the foreigner/widow/orphan"), sanctification = holy, divine font = healing, domains = creation, family, healing, knowledge, sun, truth, favored weapon = whatever fits (mace? sling?).
- Pantheon field unused or set to "none."
- The only structural friction: classes that say "choose a deity" still imply choice exists — homebrew note: in a strict monotheism the choice is "the Lord, or apostasy/demonic patron."
