- **Setting**: Real-world Holy Land geography in a historical-fantasy frame; Crusades-era.

- **License**: Unknown; appears to be hobbyist-share, no explicit license posture.

- **Pathfinder-relatedness**: D20-family, so structurally portable to PF1 with mid-effort conversion, but **not a PF2 product**. PF2's three-action / proficiency / Remaster chassis is far enough from D20 3.x that this would be more 'inspiration' than 'conversion source' for a PF2 reskin.

## Significance

- Confirms that **at the D20 level, Christian/historical-Crusades content does exist** as personal-site projects. The niche is therefore not actually unprecedented; what is unprecedented is a **PF2e** Christian product.
- Useful as a **reference for setting tone** — what does a D20 Holy Land sourcebook actually contain? — even if not directly portable.
- Reinforces the pattern surfaced everywhere else: Christian RPG content tends to live as **personal-site / one-GM artifacts**, not as packaged commercial products with active communities. The exception (per [[prior-christian-rpg-lessons]]) is Testament (Green Ronin, 2003) which is the canonical positive case.
- Suggests an **opportunity**: the absence of a PF2e Crusader / Holy Land product is more notable when paired with the existence of D20 / 5e ones (Crusader Earth; *The Adventurer's Guide to the Bible* for 5E). PF2e specifically has a hole.
