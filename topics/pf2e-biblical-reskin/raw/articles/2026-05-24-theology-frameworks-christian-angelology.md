# Christian Angelology

## The Pseudo-Dionysian Hierarchy (6th c., systematized by Aquinas)

**First Sphere (closest to God, contemplate Him directly):**
- **Seraphim** — "burning ones," six-winged (Isaiah 6); lead heavenly worship.
- **Cherubim** — multi-winged, four-faced (Ezekiel 1, 10); guardians of holiness; on the Ark.
- **Thrones / Ophanim** — wheels of Ezekiel; seats of divine judgment.

**Second Sphere (cosmic governance):**
- **Dominions / Lordships** — regulate lower angels; preside over celestial order.
- **Virtues** — work miracles; govern natural elements.
- **Powers / Authorities** — restrain demonic forces; war angels.

**Third Sphere (engaged with creation):**
- **Principalities / Rulers** — guardians of nations, cities, institutions.
- **Archangels** — chief messengers; named individuals.
- **Angels** — guardian angels of individuals; common messengers.

## Named Archangels
- **Catholic Church (officially recognized): Michael, Gabriel, Raphael only.** The Holy See's 2001 *Directory on Popular Piety* discourages naming other angels in liturgy.
- **Eastern Orthodox**: liturgically venerates seven — Michael, Gabriel, Raphael, **Uriel, Selaphiel, Jegudiel, Barachiel** (sometimes Jeremiel as eighth).
- **Protestant**: typically only the three biblically named (Michael in Daniel/Jude/Revelation; Gabriel in Daniel/Luke; Raphael only in deuterocanonical Tobit, so some Protestants exclude him).
- **Uriel** — appears in 2 Esdras (4 Ezra) and 1 Enoch; Catholic recognition is limited; Orthodox accepts.

## The Watchers
- Pre-Dionysian angelic class from 1 Enoch's *Book of the Watchers* (3rd-2nd c. BCE).
- Two camps: faithful Watchers (the "holy ones who watch") and fallen Watchers (Shemyaza, Azazel et al. — descended on Mt. Hermon, taught humans forbidden arts, fathered Nephilim).
- Influenced Jude 6, 2 Peter 2:4 ("angels who sinned"), and most second-temple Jewish demonology.
- Not in the Pseudo-Dionysian hierarchy — exists as a parallel tradition.

## Cherubim — Biblical Form
- Ezekiel 1, 10: four faces (man, lion, ox, eagle), four wings, wheels-within-wheels.
- Genesis 3:24: guard Eden's gate with flaming sword.
- Exodus 25:18-22: gold cherubim on the Ark's mercy seat.
- **Note**: Biblical cherubim look nothing like Renaissance putti (chubby babies). They are awe-inspiring composite creatures, closer to Mesopotamian *lamassu*.

## Seraphim — Biblical Form
- Isaiah 6:2-3: six wings (two cover face, two cover feet, two for flight).
- Cry "Holy, holy, holy" — the *Trisagion*.
- Only Isaiah names them; later tradition places them at the apex.

## Denominational Divergence Summary
- **Catholic**: full Pseudo-Dionysian hierarchy in popular theology, three-named archangels in liturgy, non-binding doctrinal status.
- **Eastern Orthodox**: same nine ranks but with seven-archangel veneration.
- **Protestant**: minimal hierarchical interest; named archangels limited to canonical scripture; Pseudo-Dionysius treated as historical curiosity, not authority.
