# Lewis's Two-Tier Magic

## Deep Magic
- Established law/order embedded in Narnia at creation.
- The White Witch invokes it legitimately to claim Edmund — "all traitors belong to her."
- Functions like natural law / contractual obligation. Even the Emperor across the sea respects it.

## Deeper Magic
- Older than Deep Magic — "from before the Dawn of Time."
- Activated when an innocent willingly dies in a traitor's place: the stone table cracks, death works backwards.
- Aslan's resurrection isn't a violation of the rules but the operation of a *deeper* rule the Witch never knew.

## Witchcraft vs. Divine Power (per Lewis)
- **Witchcraft** = rule-exploitation, domination, the Deplorable Word, petrification, weather-coercion. The Witch's power amplifies her ego "at a cost" of misery.
- **Divine power** = sacrifice, restoration, resurrection. Aslan submits *to* the law and is vindicated *through* a deeper law.
- The Witch literally cannot comprehend self-sacrifice — her metaphysic is purely transactional.

## In *That Hideous Strength*
- Merlin's magic is "lawful" only when he is possessed by the Oyéresu (planetary angels) — a vessel for divine command.
- N.I.C.E. magic = scientism + demonic Macrobes. Lewis's verdict: rejecting God opens you to demons regardless of intellectual sophistication.
- "True authority comes from alignment with God's creation, not from technological mastery or willful autonomy."

## Implication for a Christian Reskin
Lewis hands us a clean two-tier system: *good supernatural power = obedience to Logos / vessel for higher will*; *evil sorcery = self-willed domination via exploit*. The same spell list can be reskinned by **the source of authority**, not the mechanical effect.
