# Magic and Merlin
Merlin awakens after centuries of sleep. His magic is "older" than Christianity but is *not* condemned: it is **conduit-magic** — power flowing through a properly aligned vessel. He becomes "possessed by the Oyéresu" (the planetary angels of the Space Trilogy), and only then unleashes his full power against the N.I.C.E.

## Key Theological Move
- Magic is never autonomous. It is always *participation*.
- The question is never "is magic permitted?" but "by whose authority and through whose voice?"
- The Pendragon (a divinely appointed leader) commissions Merlin; without that obedience-chain, Merlin's craft would be evil.

## The N.I.C.E. as Anti-Church
- Wears the surface of *scientism* but is in fact a magical cult.
- Members like Wither, Frost, and Filostrato knowingly serve the Macrobes (demons).
- "The disciplined cruelty of some ideological oligarchy" is Lewis's portrait of organized evil — bureaucratic, technocratic, demonic.
- **Lewis's argument**: scientific naturalism *opens* people to demonic possession because it denies any framework for resisting it.

## The Eldila / Oyéresu
- Planetary angels in the Space Trilogy, appearing also in *That Hideous Strength*.
- Provide the "lawful" supernatural channel.
- Merlin-possessed-by-Oyéresu is the Lewis paradigm of "good magic": the human is a hollowed vessel for a higher will.

## Implication for a Christian Reskin
- Lewis offers a more permissive frame than the all-magic-is-demonic Augustinian default.
- Magic-as-sub-creation is recoverable *if* the caster is in obedience.
- "Pre-Christian" magic (Merlin, Numenorean smithcraft) can be retained in a deep-history setting where the prophets are still arriving — perfect for an OT/Patriarchal-era PF2e reskin.
- Cleanest mechanical move: re-flavor Sorcerer "bloodlines" as *anointings* (Davidic, Aaronic, Nazirite) and Witch "patrons" as either angelic Watchers or fallen Watchers (Enochic literature) — the player chooses.
