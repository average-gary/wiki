# Quaker Peace Testimony — key findings

- **1660 declaration to Charles II** (George Fox + 11 companions): "All bloody principles and practices we do utterly deny, with all outward wars, and strife, and fightings..." — a corporate, public, and unconditional renunciation of warfare in any kingdom, earthly or spiritual.
- **Inner Light doctrine**: divine illumination indwells *every* person. This is the theological foundation for both pacifism (you cannot harm a bearer of the Inner Light) and for Quaker non-hierarchical ecclesiology (no priest is needed to mediate access to God).
- **Silent waiting worship**: Quaker meetings sit in silence; participants speak only when "moved by the Inner Light." This is a *third* worship register distinct from (a) Catholic/Orthodox sacramental ritual and (b) Pentecostal charismatic-emotional expression. For a TTRPG: no sacramental mechanics, no charism mechanics — instead, *contemplative listening* mechanics.
- **Conscientious objection in practice**: historic refusal of military service; tax resistance against military funding; alternative service via the American Friends Service Committee (Nobel Peace Prize, 1947). Quakers built institutional infrastructure for nonviolent witness rather than just holding it as private conviction.
- **Active, not passive**: pacifism here is "active nonviolence" — protest, civil disobedience, peace-building, reconciliation work. Distinct from quietist withdrawal. This is the angle that *enables* adventure-shaped play (mission, witness, intervention) rather than just cutting combat.
