# Player Core 2 Preview: The Champion, Remastered

**Author:** Logan Bonner (Pathfinder 2e Design Lead)
**Published:** June 12, 2024 on the Paizo community blog.

## Key designer claims (paraphrased / quoted from extracted blog text)

- The original PF2 champion was built on alignment: each alignment had a tied cause with rigid tenets — "a strict set of hierarchical rules."
- The remaster replaces this with **edicts and anathema "tied to different character choices to guide your roleplaying."** Example given for the Justice cause: edicts include "follow the law, respect legitimate authorities or leadership"; anathema include "take advantage of another, cheat."
- Stated design goal: **"more emphasis on edicts rather than an unbendable code"** to allow **"more well-rounded, nuanced characters"** with **"a better balance over 'should nots' instead of all 'must nots.'"**
- **Sanctification is *optional*** and tied to deity. "Each sanctification gives you another edict and anathema, and adds the holy or unholy trait to all your Strikes."
- Cause distribution: justice, liberation, obedience (any sanctification); desecration, iniquity (unholy only); redemption, grandeur (holy only).

## What the post does NOT say

- No theological framing. No mention of monotheism, privation, vitalism, or any specific religious tradition as inspiration.
- No explanation of why **"holy/unholy"** were chosen as the binary labels (vs. e.g. "consecrated/desecrated", "luminous/shadowed", "axiomatic/entropic", "celestial/fiendish"). The labels are presented as self-evident.
- No explanation of why **"spirit"** damage exists or what theological/metaphysical concept it represents.
- The post talks about "design" exclusively in terms of *flexibility* and *roleplaying nuance*, never in terms of cosmology or worldbuilding intent.

## Significance for the gap-closing inquiry

This is the single most authoritative public statement on sanctification design from the PF2e design lead. It confirms:

1. The *stated* design problem was **rigidity of alignment-locked tenets**, not pluralism, not OGL-flight, not theological updating.
2. The *stated* design solution was **edict/anathema flexibility per character**, with sanctification as an opt-in flavor layer.
3. **No theological justification for the holy/unholy/spirit/vitality/void taxonomy is given.** The taxonomy is treated as self-evident vocabulary.

The Augustinian-feeling vitality-vs-void privation echo, and the unusually clean monotheistic fit of the framework, appear to be **incidental** — emergent properties of designers reaching for the most general moral-good/moral-evil terminology available once alignment was off the table.
