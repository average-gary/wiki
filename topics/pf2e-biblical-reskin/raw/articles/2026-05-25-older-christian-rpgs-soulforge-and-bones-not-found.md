## Interpretation

The Soulforge name in the original gap note is heavily collided with non-Christian gaming products dating back to at least the early 2000s, which makes any small Christian indie project under that name effectively unsearchable. If a "Soulforge / Devotional Roleplaying" project ever existed, plausible scenarios:

1. A self-published church-bulletin-or-zine project with no web presence.
2. A vapor project announced on a long-defunct forum (Christian Gamers Guild listserv era, late 1990s) that never shipped.
3. Misremembering of a different title (e.g., the prior gap note conflated *Rapture: The Second Coming* with "Book of Days," so similar conflations are plausible here too).

"dem Bones" as a Christian parody of the Bones engine likewise produced zero hits. The Bones system is itself relatively obscure (Tales of Gargentihr, etc.); a Christian parody of it would be a niche-of-a-niche and is consistent with not surfacing.

## Recommendation

Treat these as **unverified, likely never released** rather than continuing to dig. The cost-benefit on further searches is poor: another round of WebSearch will turn up the same Steam Soulforge and the same nursery-rhyme "dem bones" results. The only realistic path forward is a primary source (Christian Gamers Guild archives, RPG.net Pre-2005 Wayback snapshots once that tool is reachable from the harness) — not standard web search.
