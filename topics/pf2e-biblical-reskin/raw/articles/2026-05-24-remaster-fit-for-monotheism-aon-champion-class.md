# Champion (Remaster) — primary source notes

## Sanctification

- Champion gains **holy**, **unholy**, or **no sanctification** based on chosen deity.
- Holy strikes carry the holy trait; unholy strikes carry the unholy trait. None: neither.
- Gaining the opposing trait removes the original until the **atone ritual** is completed.

## Causes (each adds to edicts/anathema)

- Cause is now decoupled from a specific alignment grid. It layers onto sanctification, not replaces it.
- Cause grants the **champion's reaction** (the iconic protective action), upgrading at level 9 (relentless reaction) and 11 (exalted reaction).

## Deity coupling

- One deity is selected. Champion gets training in deity skill, deity favored weapon, **Champion's Aura** (15-ft emanation marking devotee).
- Deity drives sanctification (holy/unholy/none), edicts, anathema, divine font.

## Anathema enforcement

- "Acts fundamentally opposed to your deity's ideals are anathema to your faith."
- Violation → loss of holy/unholy trait, focus pool, and blessing — restored only via atone ritual.

## Blessing of the Devoted (lvl 3)

- Blessed Armament / Blessed Shield / Blessed Swiftness — all theology-neutral.

## Monotheism implications

- Sanctification is binary-plus-null, not four-axis: a single deity can cleanly be holy and the system Just Works.
- Cause is no longer alignment-locked, so a single-god setting can offer all causes under one God so long as edicts/anathema are reframed (e.g., paladin/redeemer/liberator become covenantal vocations).
- Unholy champions still need an in-fiction source — in monotheism, fallen-angel/demonic patrons or apostate covenant-breakers fill that slot.
