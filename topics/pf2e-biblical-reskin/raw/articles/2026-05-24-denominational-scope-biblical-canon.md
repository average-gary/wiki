# Biblical Canon Differences

## Catholic (46 OT + 27 NT)
Council of Trent (1546) approved deuterocanon: Tobit, Judith, Wisdom, Sirach, Baruch, Letter of Jeremiah, 1-2 Maccabees, plus additions to Esther/Daniel. Prayer of Manasseh, 3 Esdras, 4 Esdras appear in Sixto-Clementine appendix as "apocryphal."

## Eastern Orthodox
Septuagint-based. Adds 1-3 Maccabees (vs Catholic 1-2), Psalm 151, Prayer of Manasseh integrated into 2 Paralipomenon, 1-2 Esdras canonical. Synod of Jerusalem (1672) formally established canon. Some include 4 Maccabees as appendix.

## Protestant (66 books)
Luther moved deuterocanonical books to separate "Apocrypha" section, "useful and good to read" but not authoritative for doctrine. Reformed/Baptist traditions typically omit entirely.

## Anglican/Lutheran (39 OT + apocrypha)
Apocrypha read in lectionaries "for example of life" without establishing doctrine. Article VI of 39 Articles. Middle ground.

## Oriental Orthodox / Ethiopian Tewahedo
Broadest canon: includes 1-3 Ethiopic Maccabees (Meqabyan — different texts from Greek Maccabees), Book of Enoch, Jubilees, Pseudo-Josephus.
