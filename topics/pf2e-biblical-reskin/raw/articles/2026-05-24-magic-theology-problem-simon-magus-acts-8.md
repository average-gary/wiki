# The Acts 8:9-24 Narrative

- Simon is a Samaritan sorcerer who "bewitched the people" with magic.
- Converts under Philip's preaching; baptized.
- Sees Peter and John confer the Holy Spirit by laying on of hands.
- Offers money for the same power.
- Peter: "Thy money perish with thee, because thou hast thought that the gift of God may be purchased with money."

## The Foundational Distinction

| | Magic (Simon) | Miracle (Apostles) |
|---|---|---|
| Mode | Technique, performance, awe | Petition, laying on of hands |
| Economy | Transactional (purchase) | Gift (gratis data) |
| Source | Personal/occult power | Holy Spirit |
| Purpose | Self-aggrandizement | Building the Church |
| Result | Bewitchment | Edification |

## Apocryphal Amplification
Later Acts of Peter has Simon levitate to prove his power; Peter's prayer brings him crashing down. The literary point: any *appearance* of magical power, when set against true apostolic authority, collapses. Useful for adventure design — "the witch's spell fails when faced with prayer" can be a mechanic.

## Implication for a Christian Reskin
- "Simony" is the verbal trace of this boundary. Any class that *trades* for power (bloodline, patron, pact, eldritch contract) is on the Simon side.
- Any class that *receives* power (sent prophet, anointed king, gifted apostle) is on the apostolic side.
- The mechanical distinction need not change PF2e's spell slots — but the *fluff* of acquisition is everything: study/contract/bargain vs. anointing/calling/sending.
