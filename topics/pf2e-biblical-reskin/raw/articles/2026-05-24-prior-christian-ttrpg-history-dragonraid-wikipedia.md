## Designer & lineage

- Dick Wulf, 1984, published by Adventures for Christ.
- Setting names: Dastan / EdenAgain (allegorical fantasy world).
- 2018: Wulf transferred the franchise to Christian author James R. Hannibal, who relaunched as **Lightraider Academy**.

## Mechanics: "Word Runes" / battle verses

- Replaces traditional magic. Players must **recite Bible verses from memory** to produce in-game effects.
- Stu Horvath: "an extremely early example of the dialogue-as-mechanic ideas" later seen in narrative-focused games.
- **Adventure Learning System**: players roll d10, NPCs roll d8 — a built-in player advantage so the **Adventure Master** (GM) can reliably deliver scripted moral lessons.

## Reception

- Caught in a vise: secular gamers rejected the fundamentalist framing; conservative Christian groups condemned it alongside D&D during the Satanic Panic.
- Horvath's blunt summary: **"DragonRaid was a failure"** in the mainstream RPG market.

## Relevance

The canonical cautionary tale: when mechanics literally require the player to perform piety (recite scripture to cast a spell), you alienate gamers who came to play and Christians who feel the sacred is being trivialized.
