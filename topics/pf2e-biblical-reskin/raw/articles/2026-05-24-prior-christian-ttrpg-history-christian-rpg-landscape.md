## Known titles in the lineage

- **DragonRaid** (1984, US) — earliest, most documented.
- **In Nomine Satanis / Magna Veritas** (1989, France).
- **Angeli** (1993, France).
- **Inferno** (1993, Italy).
- **Kuninkaiden Aika** (1993, Finland).
- **Holy Lands RPG** — "historical fantasy and alternative Christian role-playing."
- **Testament** (2003, Green Ronin, US) — d20 Bronze Age Israel.
- **Adventurer's Guide to the Bible** (modern, Red Panda, 5E-compatible).
- **Lightraider Academy** (2018+, James R. Hannibal) — DragonRaid revival.

## The two-category split

A repeated framing in the Christian-RPG discourse: games divide into

1. **Indoctrination tools** — designed primarily to teach doctrine; mechanics enforce piety. (DragonRaid is the archetype.)
2. **Christian-themed settings** — Christian doctrine/mythology supplies setting; mechanics remain neutral. (Testament, Adventurer's Guide are archetypes.)

Category 1 consistently fails commercially. Category 2 finds niche success.

## Active communities (as of 2026)

- **Saga Society** — "a group of Christians creating Christian culture through tabletop gaming."
- **r/RPGs_for_Christians** — Reddit subreddit dedicated to the question.
- **Lightraider** community around the DragonRaid revival.

## Relevance

Maps the field. The PF2e biblical reskin should self-identify clearly with **Category 2** to inherit the success patterns and avoid the canonical failure modes of Category 1.
