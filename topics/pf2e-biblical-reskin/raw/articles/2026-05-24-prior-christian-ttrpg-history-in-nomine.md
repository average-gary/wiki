## Profile

- Publisher: **Steve Jackson Games** (US adaptation of French RPG **In Nomine Satanis / Magna Veritas**).
- Frame: angel/demon roleplaying using Judeo-Christian cosmology — **not exclusively Christian** in orientation.
- Archangels are aligned with multiple real-world faiths: Khalid (Islam), Dominic and Laurence (Catholicism), etc.

## Design philosophy: declined moral monopoly

- "Takes itself rather seriously" but presents **"shades of gray"** where "good and bad outcomes are a matter of perspective."
- Campaigns can be sympathetic to Heaven, or portray Hell's forces as freedom fighters.
- The setting is theological; **moral judgment is left to players**.

## Mechanics — thematically resonant without being preachy

- **d666** (three six-sided dice) is the core resolution mechanic.
- **1-1-1** = Holy Trinity → divine intervention favoring Heaven.
- **6-6-6** = Number of the Beast → infernal intervention favoring Hell.
- Theology drives the *flavor* of mechanics rather than gating mechanics behind real-world piety.

## Reception

- Rick Swan praised the **"reverent treatment of a sensitive subject"** and noted the creators "tackled a volatile premise head on."
- Andy Butcher criticized **"poor organisation and a lack of background information"** — a recurring pitfall when a setting carries heavy theological baggage but the book doesn't onboard the reader.

## Relevance

Two transferable lessons:
1. **Thematic mechanics** (d666 mapping to Trinity/Beast) can carry meaning without requiring player piety — a model for biblical-flavored crit/fumble tables, divine intervention, etc.
2. Theological games **must over-invest in onboarding**. In Nomine got dinged for assuming readers brought the cosmology. A biblical PF2e reskin needs strong setting orientation up front.
