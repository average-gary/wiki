## Product profile

- Author: **Scott Bennie**.
- Publisher: **Green Ronin Publishing**, part of the **Mythic Vistas** historical line for d20/D&D 3rd edition.
- Year: **2003**, 240 pages.
- Sequel/supplement: **Testament: The Hittites** (2005). Bennie also worked on **Eternal Rome** (2007) in the same line.

## Design philosophy: neutrality, not preaching

- A 2007 reviewer: "Mr. Bennie clearly attempts to remain as neutral as possible when depicting the various cultures included in Testament."
- Deliberately **does not** cast any faction (Israelites, Egyptians, Canaanites, Philistines) as heroes or villains.
- Plays **historically straight**: Bronze Age Israel as a setting, not as theology or allegory.

## What it does NOT do (the "Testament solve")

- No Bible-verse-as-spell mechanic.
- No "demonstrate Christian virtue" XP system.
- No required player piety.
- The d20 chassis stays intact; the setting supplies flavor, factions, gear, and the historical-religious atmosphere.

## Reception

- A reader who had "read the Old Testament entirely" found "the cultural representations to be reasonably presented."
- Generally regarded as the most credible Bible-adjacent RPG product because it respects both the source material and the medium.

## Relevance — the "Testament solve" the PF2e biblical reskin should learn from

Treat the Bible as **historical/mythic setting material**, not as a mechanical layer that polices player behavior. Keep the system's existing gameplay loop. Let theology emerge through narrative and player choice, not through dice rules.
