# Aerial Toll Houses

## Doctrine
After death, the soul is escorted by angels through an aerial realm. At each of (typically) twenty toll houses, demons accuse the soul of a specific sin category — falsehood, slander, unmercifulness, sodomy, theft, etc. Each station demands the soul produce evidence of compensatory good deeds or repentance. Failure means the demons drag the soul to hell.

## Theological status
**Theologoumenon, not dogma.** Not all Orthodox accept it. Detractors include David Bentley Hart (calls it Gnostic and heretical) and Stephen J. Shoemaker (notes it was obscure for the first millennium). Defenders include Seraphim Rose. Number 20 is not fixed.

## Primary source
*Life of Basil the Younger* (10th century) — Theodora's vision is the locus classicus.

## Design implications
- Ready-made dungeon: each toll house is a thematic encounter zone with a specific sin, a specific demon-prosecutor, and a defending angel-witness.
- Maps cleanly to PF2e's existing planar-travel framework: think "spiritual purgatorial layer" as a literal traversable plane.
- Contested-doctrine status is itself useful: a worldbuilder can include or omit and stay within the Orthodox lens.
- Catholic purgatory is more abstract (a *condition* of purification, not a place of demonic prosecution) and translates less directly to game terms.
