# Cessationism vs Continuationism

## Cessationist position
Miraculous charisms were temporary — they authenticated apostles and the closing of the canon. With the canon complete, the gifts ceased. **Held by**: Reformed, Presbyterian, much of Anglicanism, Calvinist Baptists, dispensationalists.

## Continuationist position
Charisms remain active throughout church history. The Bible contains no explicit text mandating their cessation. **Held by**: Catholic Church, Eastern Orthodox, Methodism, Holiness, Moravians, all Pentecostal/Charismatic. **Lutherans**: middle position.

## Charisms at issue
Glossolalia (tongues), prophecy, faith healing, miraculous intervention, discernment of spirits.

## Design implications — the magic-theology problem
This is **the crux** for the reskin. PF2e Divine tradition assumes ongoing miracle-working. Therefore:
- **Continuationist lens** (Catholic/Orthodox/Pentecostal): trivially compatible. Cleric/Champion/Oracle = active charismatic.
- **Cessationist lens** (Reformed): hostile to the premise. The reskin must either (a) set the campaign in the apostolic age before cessation, (b) treat magic as non-charismatic (natural arcane only?), or (c) abandon strict cessationism.
- **Pentecostal lens** is the *most* generous: every Cleric is a healing-revival evangelist, every Oracle a prophet, every Sorcerer a tongues-speaker. Frank Peretti's *This Present Darkness* is the cultural template.

This single doctrine is the strongest filter on which lens a PF2e reskin can comfortably adopt.
