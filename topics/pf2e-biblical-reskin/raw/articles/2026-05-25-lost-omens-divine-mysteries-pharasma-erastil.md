# Pharasma (deity-290) — Divine Mysteries pg. 86

- **Epithet**: The Lady of Graves
- **Edicts** (3): "strive to understand ancient prophecies, destroy undead, lay bodies to rest"
- **Anathema** (3): "create undead, desecrate a corpse, take from the dead in bad faith"
  - Remaster softened from legacy "rob a tomb" → "take from the dead in bad faith" (intent matters, not act)
- **Area of Concern**: birth, death, fate, prophecy, and time
- **Divine Attribute**: Constitution or Wisdom
- **Divine Font**: Heal
- **Sanctification**: **none** (cannot choose holy or unholy — strictly neutral)
- **Divine Skill**: Medicine
- **Favored Weapon**: Dagger
- **Domains** (primary, 4): Death, Fate, Healing, Knowledge
- **Alternate Domains** (3): Soul, Time, Vigil
- **Cleric Spells** (3): 1st Mindlink, 3rd Ghostly Weapon, 4th Vision of Death
- **Religious symbol**: spiraling comet; **sacred animal**: whippoorwill
- **Narrative blurb**: ~190 words, cosmological (her throne on the Spire at the center of the planes, her role as final arbiter, the River of Souls, prophecy framing). **Notable: longest narrative of the four sampled.**

# Erastil (deity-282) — Divine Mysteries pg. 58

- **Epithet**: Old Deadeye
- **Edicts** (4): "care for your home and family, fulfill your duties, keep the peace, protect the community"
- **Anathema** (4): "abandon your home in its time of need, choose yourself over your community, tarnish your reputation, tell lies"
- **Area of Concern**: family, farming, hunting, and trade
- **Divine Attribute**: Constitution or Wisdom
- **Divine Font**: Heal
- **Sanctification**: can choose holy
- **Divine Skill**: Survival
- **Favored Weapon**: Longbow
- **Domains** (primary, 4): Earth, Family, Nature, Wealth
- **Alternate Domains** (1): Duty
- **Cleric Spells** (3): 1st Sure Strike, 3rd Wall of Thorns, 5th Nature's Pathway
- **Religious symbol**: bow and arrow; **sacred animal**: stag; **sacred colors**: brown, green
- **Narrative blurb**: ~140 words, **anti-crusader** framing — distinguishes Erastil from "many other good deities" who send followers out to fight evil; Erastil instead protects pastoral life.

## Pattern observations specific to these two

- **Pharasma's sanctification = none** is a structural precedent: a deity can be major, central, holy-adjacent, and yet have *no* mechanical "holy/unholy" tag. **This is directly relevant to a YHWH entry that wants to sidestep the holy/unholy duality** — Pharasma proves Paizo will leave the field empty.
- **Anathema as character integrity**: Erastil's "tarnish your reputation, tell lies" is identical in shape to a Decalogue-style prohibition. The Remaster framing of anathema as virtue-violation rather than purely metaphysical taboo is theologically friendly.
- **Edict count**: 3 (Pharasma, Iomedae) or 4 (Sarenrae, Erastil) is the universal range. Never more than 4 for the major deities sampled. Never fewer than 3.
