# Specific Prohibitions

## Deuteronomy 18:10-11
Forbids: child-sacrifice, divination (qosem qesamim), soothsaying (me'onen), augury (menahesh), sorcery (mekhashshep), spell-casting (hover hever), consulting familiar spirits ('ob), wizards (yiddeoni), necromancy (doresh el-hammetim).

## Exodus 22:18
"You shall not tolerate a sorceress" — *mekhashshepha*. Translation contested (some scholars argue "poisoner").

## Leviticus 19:26 / 20:6 / 20:27
Forbids divination and soothsaying; mandates death for those with familiar spirits.

## Key Observation
The prohibitions name **specific practices and contractual relationships with specific spirits**, not "anyone who works wonders." Moses' staff-to-snake, Elijah's fire, Elisha's bear, the prophets' visions — none fall under these terms. The line is drawn at:
1. Pact with non-YHWH spirit/dead.
2. Manipulating outcome by occult technique rather than petitioning YHWH.
3. Divination outside lawful means (Urim/Thummim, prophet).

## Implication for a Christian Reskin
This is the strongest argument for a **charism + miracle** reskin: the Bible doesn't ban supernatural effect, it bans (a) the *source* (foreign spirits / dead) and (b) the *mode* (occult technique vs. obedient petition). A PF2e Cleric/Champion/Oracle reskinned as "prophet" is fully kosher; a PF2e Witch with a "patron" is precisely what Deut 18 forbids.
