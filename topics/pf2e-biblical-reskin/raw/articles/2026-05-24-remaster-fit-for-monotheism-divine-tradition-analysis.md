# Divine tradition — Remaster status and monotheism friction

## What the Remaster did NOT change

- The four-tradition split (arcane / divine / occult / primal) is **structurally identical** pre- and post-Remaster.
- Class-to-tradition mappings unchanged: cleric/champion = divine, druid = primal, bard/witch = occult, wizard = arcane, sorcerer = bloodline-dependent.

## Tradition flavor (synthesized from Player Core + GM Core)

- **Arcane** — academic study, manipulation of the fabric of reality
- **Divine** — granted by deities, healing/sanctification heavy, holy/unholy interaction
- **Occult** — the mind, the soul, hidden truths, mental effects
- **Primal** — the natural world, life cycles, elements, druidry

## Monotheism friction

- In a Christian worldview, **nature is God's creation**, so primal magic should also be divine in origin. The PF2e split treats them as parallel sources, which subtly implies a polytheism (different powers grant different magics).
- Likewise, occult ("the soul, hidden truths") could equally be God-given in a monotheistic frame.
- **Mechanically**, however, the four-tradition split is purely a spell-list-and-stat-block organizing principle; the in-fiction *source* is purely flavor. You can rule that "all four traditions ultimately derive from God, but mortals access them through different disciplines" without changing a single rule.

## Recommended reskin

- Divine = clergy/sacramental magic (priests, paladins/champions, divine sacraments)
- Primal = saints/anchorites/desert fathers — magic of God's creation, accessed via communion with the natural order
- Occult = mystics/contemplatives — magic of the soul and inner life
- Arcane = scholastic magic — magic understood through reason, accessible to learned natural philosophers (problematic in some Christian traditions; mark as morally neutral or suspect depending on setting)
- This keeps all spell lists intact, requires zero rule changes, and gives each tradition a saint-type archetype.

## Holy/unholy interaction with non-divine traditions

- Holy/unholy traits are NOT exclusive to divine spells in the Remaster — primal, occult, and arcane spells can also carry the holy or unholy trait when appropriate (e.g. *holy light* primal cantrip).
- This means a monotheistic reskin's "all traditions are God-given" framing has rules support: any caster can sanctify their magic.
