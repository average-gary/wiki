# Monk + Bard — easy reskins

## Monk (ID=60, Wis-key)
- "Qi spells" (POST-REMASTER NAME — was "ki" pre-Remaster) are focus spells; player chooses **divine OR occult** designation. **For biblical setting: pick divine.** This is critical — the rule already authorizes the priestly designation.
- Stances; Mystic Strikes (level 3 unarmed magic); Master / Perfected Form scaling.
- Class explicitly notes philosophical framing of "honing your body into a flawless instrument" — fits desert-father / stylite asceticism perfectly.

**Reskin verdict**: pure reflavor. Reframe as Desert Father (Anthony, Pachomius), stylite (Symeon), Cenobite. Stance names retag as prayer-postures (orans, prostration, kneeling-vigil). "Qi" stays mechanically but is renarrated as "spiritual discipline" or "Pneuma" (Greek: breath/spirit). Mountain Stance = Pillar of Symeon. Ironblood Stance = Anchorite endurance.

## Bard (ID=32, Cha-key, occult prepared)
- Muses (Enigma / Maestro / Polymath / Warrior).
- Compositions: cantrips (Courageous Anthem) + focus spells (Counter Performance).
- Inspire Courage's mechanical effect: status bonus to attack/damage/fear-saves.

**Reskin verdict**: pure reflavor + ONE tradition retag. Bard is **occult tradition** by RAW — for a biblical setting, retag to **divine tradition** to avoid the "occult" connotation problem. Mechanically the Bard's spell list is mostly enchantment/illusion which becomes "stirring of the spirit" / "the word fitly spoken" (Proverbs 25:11).

**Direct mappings**:
- Compositions = Psalms / hymns.
- Courageous Anthem = "The LORD is my strength and song" (Ex 15:2; Ps 28:7) — David before Goliath.
- Counter Performance = exorcising-Saul's-evil-spirit (1 Sam 16:23, David's harp).
- Maestro Muse = Levite-musician (1 Chron 25 — sons of Asaph, Heman, Jeduthun "set apart for the service of song").
- Warrior Muse = Deborah's Song (Judges 5).
- Enigma Muse = wisdom-literature poet (Solomon, Asaph's "I will open my mouth in a parable").
- Polymath Muse = court chronicler / scribe-cantor.

The Levite-musician role (1 Chronicles 25, 2 Chronicles 5, the Sons of Korah) is a **better historical anchor** than "court bard" and is explicitly liturgical — hits the brief's "Psalmist" instinct dead-center.
