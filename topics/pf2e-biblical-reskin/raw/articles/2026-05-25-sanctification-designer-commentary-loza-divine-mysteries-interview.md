# Luis Loza interview (BadLuckGamer, 16 May 2024) — Reddit summary by u/EzeDoesIt

**Source:** Reddit post by EzeDoesIt summarizing Loza's livestreamed interview on BadLuckGamer YouTube ([video link](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1UZI2bCzlJs)). Loza is Paizo's Creative Director of the Rules and Lore Team.

## Designer-attributed statements relevant to the gap

### On the magnitude of the sanctification change (paraphrased from interview)

> "He feels like they've done a good job overall with keeping the game compatible with the previous content, **sanctification and wizard schools being the most egregious changes**. They're gonna address the most glaring things that need adjustment, but he asks to not expect them to go back and adjust every single thing to the Remaster."

This is the **most senior Paizo lore figure** publicly characterizing sanctification — and **his frame is "egregious backward-compatibility break to manage," not "theological breakthrough."**

### On Divine Mysteries' conception

> "**Divine Mysteries was actually conceived BEFORE the OGL fiasco!** They always wanted to do a sequel to Lost Omens: Gods & Magic, and came up with Yvali to delve into what makes a god and what makes them tick."

Important nuance: the deepest theological/divine product of the Remaster era was *not* a response to the alignment removal. It was an in-flight project that got retrofitted to the new mechanics.

### On the reverse-engineering effort

> "Luis made sure to grab every god stat block he could find and update them for the Remaster. All of the domains across all the books and AP backmatter have been collected and updated as well."

This is *retrofit* language. The designers are *applying* the new sanctification taxonomy to existing deities, not *deriving* new deities from a new theological vision.

## What is conspicuously absent

- No discussion of *why* "holy/unholy" was chosen as the alignment replacement.
- No discussion of *why* sanctification was tied to deity rather than to character choice or cosmic axis.
- No theological, monotheistic, privation-theory, or Augustinian framing.
- The interview is focused entirely on *which gods die*, *which pantheons get expanded*, and *how to handle backward-compat* — not on the metaphysics of holy/unholy.

## Significance for the gap-closing inquiry

Loza's "egregious changes" framing is the smoking gun for the negative finding. A designer who saw sanctification as a deliberate *theological upgrade* would frame it positively ("we're proud of the new spiritual framework"). A designer who sees it as *necessary surgery to escape alignment-locked OGL terminology* would frame it as he does: an unfortunate but necessary backward-compatibility break.

Combined with Bonner's mechanically-framed Champion preview ([[2026-05-25-sanctification-designer-commentary-bonner-champion-preview]]), this confirms:

> **Sanctification is a mechanical-flexibility solution to a licensing-driven problem. Its theological resonance is not designer-intentional.**

This *strengthens* the wiki's working thesis that the Remaster is *accidentally* monotheism-friendly — Paizo did not design it to be, but the de-aligned, deity-anchored, holy/unholy-binary structure happens to fit a Christian-Biblical reskin much better than alignment-grid PF2e did.
