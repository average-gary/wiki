# Pathfinder Remaster — Wikipedia summary

## Timeline

- April 2023: Paizo announces the Remaster Project after the OGL controversy.
- Nov 2023: *Player Core* and *GM Core* ship.
- Mar 2024: *Monster Core* ships.
- Aug 2024: *Player Core 2* ships.
- 2024: *Lost Omens: Divine Mysteries* (deity remaster) ships.

## License shift

- OGL → ORC (Open RPG Creative License) due to Wizards of the Coast's OGL controversy.
- Forced removal of D&D-3.5-inherited elements: **alignment, eight schools of magic, specific spells (Magic Missile, etc.), and magic items (Bag of Holding, etc.)**.

## Backward compatibility

- Pre-Remaster supplements remain functional with minor mental translation; the rules engine is the same.
- License-provenance per record matters for redistribution: pre-Remaster = OGL, post-Remaster = ORC.

## Monotheism implications

- The OGL-driven removals are theologically convenient *by accident*: alignment was already the biggest mechanical obstacle to monotheism, and Paizo had to remove it for licensing reasons.
- Schools removal (except Illusion) reframes magic as trait-based — no built-in "necromancer subclass" pushing back on a monotheistic worldview.
- The Remaster is structurally more compatible with monotheism than legacy PF2e was, even though Golarion's lore remains polytheistic.
