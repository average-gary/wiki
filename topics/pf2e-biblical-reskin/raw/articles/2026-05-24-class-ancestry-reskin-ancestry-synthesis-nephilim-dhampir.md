# Ancestry reskin synthesis (PF2e Remaster)

> **Sourcing note**: AoN ancestry-detail pages and PathfinderWiki were blocked (403). What's here draws from the published Player Core 2 framework, the AoN Ancestries listing page (which confirms the 8 Core ancestries: Dwarf, Elf, Gnome, Goblin, Halfling, Human, Leshy, Orc), and the AoN Sorcerer page which confirms the Angelic bloodline as canon "descended from celestials" (divine tradition). Versatile heritages (Nephilim, Dhampir, Aiuvarin, Dromaar) are Player Core 2 / Howl of the Wild material.

## Tier 1: Major reskin wins (ship enthusiastically)

### Nephilim (versatile heritage) — THE single biggest ancestry win
PF2e Remaster merged Aasimar / Tiefling / Aphorite / Ganzi / Duskwalker into one **Nephilim** versatile heritage with sub-options for celestial-descended, fiend-descended, etc. The setting tax to use this is literally zero: Genesis 6:1-4 ("the sons of God came in unto the daughters of men, and they bare children to them, the same became mighty men which were of old, men of renown") gives a CANONICAL biblical Nephilim. Sub-heritages (angelkin / grimspawn / lawbringer / musetouched / pitborn / peri-blooded etc.) become:
- **angelkin** = descendant of bene-elohim (the unfallen "sons of God")
- **grimspawn / pitborn** = descendant of the fallen Watchers (Enoch tradition)
- **idyllkin / lawbringer** = elohim of justice line
- **musetouched** = elohim-of-inspiration line
- **peri-blooded** = Persian peri-influenced redeemable line

The redemption arc is built into the heritage: Nephilim of fallen-line CAN turn toward the light. Strongly recommended as the setting's "exotic but playable" ancestry.

### Dhampir (versatile heritage) — Cain-cursed framing
Vampire-blooded; undying-but-fallen. Direct Genesis 4 reference: Cain marked, cursed to wander, but not destroyed. Dhampir mechanics (slow healing, undying resilience, but social othering) port directly. **Strong reskin fit.**

## Tier 2: Reflavor with light setting work

### Human (ID=64)
Trivial. Just specify ethnic backgrounds (Hebrew, Egyptian, Babylonian, Roman, Canaanite, etc.).

### Leshy (ID=65, Core)
Sentient plant ancestry. Reskin as **Edenic remnant** — uncorrupted nature given voice (talking trees of LOTR/Narnia). Pairs natively with the Druid-as-Edenic-keeper reskin. Or: humbler reskin as miracle-grown saints (Aaron's rod budded, Numbers 17). Keeps mechanics, retags lore.

### Geniekin / Naari / Sulis / Talos / Undine (Player Core 2 versatile heritage cluster)
Elemental-descended humans. Reskin as **"the elements obey the Word"** lineages — descendants of mortals touched by miracle (Daniel's three friends in the furnace; Moses' staff). The brief's framing is right: this is a gameable hook. Mechanics (resistance to element type, elemental innate spell) survive.

### Aiuvarin (half-elf) / Dromaar (half-orc)
Versatile heritages applied to a base ancestry. If Elf and Orc ship, these ship. Lore retag is whatever fits the parent ancestries.

## Tier 3: Reflavor with significant theological work

### Dwarf, Elf, Halfling, Gnome (all Core)
Pre-Babel diverse humanity (Genesis 11) is the cleanest justification — Lewisian "old peoples" framework. Each becomes a distinct post-Babel cultural lineage with the morphological distinctions retained as God-permitted human diversity. Alternative: cut all four for a strict-historical Hebrew setting.

### Orc (ID=66, Core post-Remaster)
Brief is right: Cain-line / curse-bearing peoples / "the nations" outside Eden. Now a Core ancestry post-Remaster (no longer monstrous), so framing them as redeemable post-Curse humans is mechanically supported. **Strong fit if the setting embraces a Cain/Esau/Edomite framework.**

### Goblin (ID=62, Core)
Same problem and same solution as Orc, but smaller and less heroic. Could be reskinned as Philistine-coastal-people / Amalekite-style. Or cut.

### Kobold, Catfolk, Tengu, Ratfolk, Lizardfolk, Tripkee, Kholo
Decision: setting tone. Two paths:
- **Pre-Babel diverse humanity / "peoples of the earth"** (cosmopolitan setting). All survive as cultural variants.
- **Cut as too-creature-like** (strict historical setting).

## Tier 4: Cut or villain-only

### Beastkin
Partial-bestial transformation. Theologically fraught (image-of-God concerns). Recommend cut, OR reskin narrowly as a curse-state for villains (Nebuchadnezzar in Daniel 4 grew animal-like). Not a player option.

### Animist-class-coupled ancestries
Any ancestry whose mechanics presume animist-spirit cosmology should be cut alongside the Animist class.

## Fetchling (ID=30, Shadow Plane–touched)
Sheol-touched / Saul's Witch-of-Endor descendants — the brief's instinct is theologically precise. But Fetchling's "shadow plane" is the realm of the dead in Pathfinder lore, which lines up with Sheol. Use as a **rare, uneasy** ancestry — playable but socially marked. Not first-tier ship, but legitimate.
