# Tolkien's Two Categories of Magic

## Mageia vs. Goeteia
- **Mageia** = ordinary, mechanical magic ("speaking the proper words at a magic door, such as the Doors of Durin, to make it open"). External-mechanism causality.
- **Goeteia** = influence over perception and will. Elves used goeteia "for artistic beauty without effort and without deception"; Sauron used it to "dominate the wills of his subjects."

## Tolkien's Moral Rule
1. Neither category is intrinsically good or evil.
2. The cardinal sin is **using either to dominate another free will** — this is what makes Sauron's magic evil and the Ring corrupting.
3. Power is "not possessed or attainable by Men as such" — it requires a particular nature or grace (Elves, Maiar, Wizards-as-incarnate-angels).
4. Numenorean smithcraft uses "spells" — there is genuine ambiguity at the borders between magic and high craft.

## Implication for a Christian Reskin
Tolkien gives us a license to keep magic-as-art (sub-creation, healing, song, smithcraft, hidden ways) but to mark **domination/coercion of the will** as the moral fault line. This maps cleanly onto charm, dominate, command-type spells as suspect, while abjurations, evocations of light, healing, divination-as-true-seeing remain morally neutral or good.
