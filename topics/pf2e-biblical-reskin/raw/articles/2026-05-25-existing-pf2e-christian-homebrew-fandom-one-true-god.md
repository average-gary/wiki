# Caleb's Pathfinder Campaign Wiki — "The One True God"

**URL**: https://pdd.fandom.com/wiki/The_One_True_God
**Live fetch**: 403 from this tool; details below sourced from Brave Search snippet aggregation and a 2019 r/Pathfinder_RPG cross-reference (apcl2r "God has domains").
**Discovered via**: Brave Search "The One True God pathfinder homebrew deity wiki"

## Stat block (per surfaced excerpts)

- **Name**: The One True God
- **Alignment**: Lawful Good (the 2019 Vampirella-one-shot variant on r/Pathfinder_RPG/apcl2r used Lawful Neutral)
- **Domains**: Community, Glory, Good, Healing, Law (some sources also list Protection, Sun)
- **Favored weapon**: Heavy Mace
- **Holy symbol**: Cross
- **Sacred text**: Holy Bible
- **Distinctive mechanic**: +2 sacred bonus on saving throws after scripture study (per Brave snippet of the wiki entry)
- **In-setting role** (per the cross-referenced 2019 thread): "the only significant faith that's not a straight up death cult," redemption focus

## Status & scope

- **Edition**: Pathfinder 1st Edition (the campaign wiki and the 2019 thread predate PF2 Remaster).
- **Scope**: Single deity stat block. Not a class reskin, not a full setting, not an ancestry, not a rules supplement. About 1/200th of what would be needed for a Christian PF2e setting.
- **License**: Fandom user-generated content, no explicit license posture beyond Fandom's defaults (CC-BY-SA on text).
- **Status**: Single private campaign wiki. No active community around the stat block. Not republished.
- **Author**: "Caleb" (the wiki host); username and identity not surfaced.

## Significance

- This is the **only artifact surfaced in the gap-closing search that actually delivers a Christian-coded deity in a Pathfinder framework**. Everything else surfaced was discussion, advice, or recommendations to use Sarenrae as a stand-in.
- It confirms the niche is technically possible to inhabit (someone did it; the rules accommodate it) but **almost entirely empty at the artifact layer** (one Fandom subwiki page, PF1, single deity, no broader product).
- The mechanic "+2 sacred bonus after scripture study" is a soft DragonRaid-style temptation — it makes a piety-adjacent activity a mechanical input. Worth flagging in [[prior-christian-rpg-lessons]] as a real-world example of the trap that the wiki's Distilled Rule #1 warns against.
