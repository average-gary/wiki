# Cleric (Remaster) — primary source notes

## Deity selection

- Choose a deity granting: trained skill, favored weapon proficiency, deity-specific spells, **sanctification** (holy / unholy / none).
- No alignment requirement — cleric just must keep edicts and avoid anathema.

## Sanctification

- Holy or unholy trait on damage and effects, derived from deity, identical structurally to Champion.
- "If you gain the opposing trait in some way, you lose the previous trait until you complete an atone ritual."

## Divine Font

- 1st level: 4 extra spell slots/day (5 at lvl 5, 6 at lvl 15).
- **Healing Font** prepares only *heal*; **Harmful Font** prepares only *harm*. Deity dictates which (some allow choice).
- Heal deals vitality damage to undead and heals the living; harm reversed. Neither carries holy/unholy by default.

## Doctrine

- Cloistered Cleric (caster-focused) or Warpriest (martial). Pure mechanical pick — no theology.

## Edicts/anathema enforcement

- Repeated anathema violations → loss of divine font and cleric spellcasting until atonement.

## Tradition

- Casts on the **divine** spell tradition (Wis-based).

## Monotheism implications

- Cleric requires zero core-rule changes for monotheism: pick "the One God," set edicts/anathema, choose healing or harmful font (default healing for a YHWH-style God; harmful for apostate/demonic clergy if those exist).
- The biggest theological friction: **harmful font** is generic harm-spell access — fine in vacuum, but if the only deity is the Christian God, "harmful clerics" are conceptually awkward unless they're explicitly fallen/apostate or you cut harmful font from the canonical clergy.
