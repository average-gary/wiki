# Catholic Encyclopedia: Angels

## Nine Ranks (per Gregory the Great / Pseudo-Dionysius)

**First Hierarchy (closest to God):**
1. Seraphim — Isaiah 6, six wings, burning love
2. Cherubim — guardians (Eden gate, Ark of the Covenant)
3. Thrones — God's inner court, attendants of judgment

**Second Hierarchy (governance):**
4. Dominations (Dominions) — govern lower orders
5. Virtues — manifest divine power, miracles
6. Powers — execute divine will, restrain evil

**Third Hierarchy (closest to humanity):**
7. Principalities — oversee nations and territories
8. Archangels — bearers of significant messages
9. Angels — personal guardians, messengers

## Named Archangels (canonical)
- **Michael** ("Who is like God") — warrior, defeats Satan (Rev 12, Jude)
- **Gabriel** ("God is my strength") — annunciator (Daniel, Luke)
- **Raphael** ("God heals") — guide (Tobit, deuterocanonical)

Apocryphal/non-canonical: **Uriel** ("God is my light"), Jeremiel, Selaphiel, others — appear in 2 Esdras, 1 Enoch, Eastern Orthodox tradition.

## Sources of the Hierarchy
- **Pseudo-Dionysius**, *De Coelesti Hierarchia* (~5th-6th c.) — the foundational text.
- **Thomas Aquinas**, *Summa Theologiae* I, q.108 — systematized the three-triad structure.
- **Gregory the Great** — gave the most-cited ordering of the nine ranks.

## Doctrinal Status
"Though the doctrine it contains regarding the choirs of angels has been received in the Church with extraordinary unanimity, no proposition touching the angelic hierarchies is binding on our faith." Devotional and theological tradition, not dogma — which is exactly the latitude a worldbuilder needs.
