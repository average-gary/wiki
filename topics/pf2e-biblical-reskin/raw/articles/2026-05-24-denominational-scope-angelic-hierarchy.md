# Angelic Hierarchy

## The nine choirs (Pseudo-Dionysius, *De Coelesti Hierarchia*)

**First sphere** (closest to God):
- Seraphim — six-winged, sing the Trisagion
- Cherubim — multi-faced, guard the throne
- Thrones / Ophanim — wheels of fire from Ezekiel

**Second sphere** (cosmic governors):
- Dominions — order the celestial spheres
- Virtues — administer miracles, govern nature
- Powers — restrain evil, bind demons

**Third sphere** (engaged with creation):
- Principalities — guard nations and institutions
- Archangels — Michael, Gabriel, Raphael (named in canon); Uriel (deuterocanonical, 2 Esdras)
- Angels — guardian of individuals

## Tradition variations
- **Catholic**: full Pseudo-Dionysian hierarchy via Aquinas. Vatican 2001 *Directory* discourages naming angels beyond the three scriptural archangels.
- **Orthodox**: same hierarchy; richer iconographic tradition (each rank has fixed icon-conventions); seven archangels in some traditions (adds Uriel, Selaphiel, Jegudiel, Barachiel).
- **Protestant**: tends to flatten — angels and archangels with little speculative hierarchy. Reformed especially suspicious of extra-biblical angelology.
- **Pentecostal/spiritual-warfare**: revives Ephesians 6's "principalities and powers" as territorial demons; Frank-Peretti-style cosmology.

## Design implications
- **Catholic/Orthodox** lens: full nine-choir bestiary with mechanical differentiation by rank — Seraphim as solar-tier, Powers as battlefield-control, Principalities as nation-bound.
- **Protestant** lens: angels are messengers and warriors only; less ranked variety.
- **Pentecostal** lens: the demonic side gets the rich hierarchy (territorial spirits over cities, principalities over nations) — perfect for spiritual-warfare encounter design.
