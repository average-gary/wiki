# Nephilim (Britannica)

## Biblical references
- **Genesis 6:1-4** — "sons of God" take "daughters of men"; offspring are Nephilim, "men of renown," "mighty men of old."
- **Numbers 13:32-33** — Israelite scouts report giants in Canaan, "sons of Anak from the Nephilim."
- Possibly **Ezekiel 32:27** as oblique reference to fallen warriors.

## Three Competing Readings

### 1. Sethite / Cainite Intermarriage
- "Sons of God" = righteous Sethite line (Adam → Seth → Enosh, etc.).
- "Daughters of men" = corrupted Cainite line.
- Offspring "Nephilim" are the mixed bloodline that loses its righteousness.
- Their "great size" is metaphorical or literal but human.
- **Augustine** favored this; most Reformed/Calvinist readers follow.

### 2. Angelic-Human Hybrids
- "Sons of God" (*bene elohim*) = fallen angels / Watchers.
- They cohabit with human women; offspring are giant Nephilim.
- **1 Enoch's Book of the Watchers** (3rd-2nd c. BCE) is the locus classicus — names the ringleader Watchers (Shemyaza, Azazel) and details the descent on Mt. Hermon.
- This was the **second-temple Jewish consensus** and informs Jude 6, 2 Peter 2:4.
- Theological objection: spiritual beings can't reproduce biologically (Matthew 22:30 — angels don't marry).

### 3. Royal Hyperbole / "Men of Renown"
- "Sons of God" = ancient kings claiming divine descent (Mesopotamian-style).
- Nephilim = their elite warrior offspring; "great size" = warrior reputation.
- Modern critical-historical reading.

## Worldbuilding note
The angelic-hybrid reading (option 2) is the gameable one — it gives you giants, fallen angels, named demonic ringleaders, and a flood-as-divine-purge cosmology. The Sethite reading is theologically safer but mechanically empty for a fantasy ancestry.
