# 1 Enoch — Book of the Watchers (chapters 6-16)

R.H. Charles, *The Book of Enoch*, Oxford 1917 (public domain). Sourced verse-by-verse from Wikisource's chapter-paginated transcription of the Charles edition.

> Canonicality note: 1 Enoch is **non-canonical to Catholics, Orthodox, and Protestants**; **canonical to the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church and Eritrean Orthodox**. Quoted directly in Jude 14-15 ("Behold, the Lord cometh with ten thousands of his saints"). Alluded to in Jude 6 and 2 Pet 2:4 (angels who "kept not their first estate," reserved in chains). Heavily influential on Second Temple Judaism and early Christian demonology even where rejected from canon.

## Chapter 6 — The descent and the oath on Hermon

> 6:1 "And it came to pass when the children of men had multiplied that in those days were born unto them beautiful and comely daughters."
> 6:2 "And the angels, the children of the heaven, saw and lusted after them, and said to one another: 'Come, let us choose us wives from among the children of men and beget us children.'"
> 6:3 "And Semjâzâ, who was their leader, said unto them: 'I fear ye will not indeed agree to do this deed, and I alone shall have to pay the penalty of a great sin.'"
> 6:4 "And they all answered him and said: 'Let us all swear an oath, and all bind ourselves by mutual imprecations not to abandon this plan but to do this thing.'"
> 6:5 "Then sware they all together and bound themselves by mutual imprecations upon it."
> 6:6 "And they were in all two hundred; who descended in the days of Jared on the summit of Mount Hermon, and they called it Mount Hermon, because they had sworn and bound themselves by mutual imprecations upon it."
> 6:7 (List of the nineteen chiefs of tens): **Samîazâz** (= Semjâzâ), **Arâkîba**, **Râmêêl**, **Kôkabîêl**, **Tâmîêl**, **Râmîêl**, **Dânêl**, **Êzêqêêl**, **Barâqîjâl**, **Asâêl** (= Azâzêl in later chs.), **Armârôs**, **Batârêl**, **Anânêl**, **Zaqîêl**, **Samsâpêêl**, **Satarêl**, **Tûrêl**, **Jômjâêl**, **Sariêl**.
> 6:8 "These are their chiefs of tens."

## Chapter 7 — Wives, Nephilim, devouring

> 7:1 "And all the others together with them took unto themselves wives, and each chose for himself one, and they began to go in unto them and to defile themselves with them, and they taught them charms and enchantments, and the cutting of roots, and made them acquainted with plants."
> 7:2 "And they became pregnant, and they bare great giants, whose height was three thousand ells:"
> 7:3 "Who consumed all the acquisitions of men."
> 7:4 "And when men could no longer sustain them, the giants turned against them and devoured mankind."
> 7:5 "And they began to sin against birds, and beasts, and reptiles, and fish, and to devour one another's flesh, and drink the blood."
> 7:6 "Then the earth laid accusation against the lawless ones."

## Chapter 8 — The forbidden-arts catalogue (the *pharmakeia* genealogy)

> 8:1 "And Azâzêl taught men to make swords, and knives, and shields, and breastplates, and made known to them the metals 〈of the earth〉 and the art of working them, and bracelets, and ornaments, and the use of antimony, and the beautifying of the eyelids, and all kinds of costly stones, and all colouring tinctures."
> 8:2 "And there arose much godlessness, and they committed fornication, and they were led astray, and became corrupt in all their ways."
> 8:3 "**Semjâzâ** taught enchantments, and root-cuttings, **Armârôs** the resolving of enchantments, **Barâqîjâl** (taught) astrology, **Kôkabêl** the constellations, **Êzêqêêl** the knowledge of the clouds, 〈**Araqiêl** the signs of the earth, **Shamsiêl** the signs of the sun〉, and **Sariêl** the course of the moon."
> 8:4 "And as men perished, they cried, and their cry went up to heaven."

**This verse 3 is the load-bearing primary-text reference for any Lewisian-style "fallen Watchers taught the corrupted version of the arts" framing.**

## Chapter 9 — The four archangels intercede

Michael, Uriel, Raphael, Gabriel look down from heaven, see the bloodshed, and address the Most High. They name **Azâzêl** (taught all unrighteousness; revealed eternal heavenly secrets) and **Semjâzâ** (leader of those who lay with women). They report that the giants' violence has filled the earth with blood; the souls of the slain cry to heaven.

## Chapter 10 — The four-fold judgment

The Most High commissions four angels:

- **Uriel → Noah**: warn him of the coming flood; "Hide thyself!"
- **Raphael → Azâzêl**: bind him hand and foot, cast him into darkness in the desert of **Dudâêl**; cover him with rough and jagged rocks; he remains there until the day of the great judgment, when he is cast into the fire. Raphael also commanded to heal the corrupted earth.
- **Gabriel → "the bastards and the reprobates, the children of fornication"** (the Nephilim): provoke them to mutual destruction; they shall not have the long life their fathers hoped for.
- **Michael → Semjâzâ and his associates**: bind them for **seventy generations** under the hills of the earth, until the day of their judgment, when they shall be led off to the abyss of fire.

The chapter closes with restoration: the earth cleansed of all impurity, the righteous escape, all humanity becomes righteous and worships God.

## Chapter 11 — Eschatological blessing

> 11:1 "And in those days I will open the store chambers of blessing which are in the heaven, so as to send them down upon the earth over the work and labour of the children of men."
> 11:2 "And truth and peace shall be associated together throughout all the days of the world and throughout all the generations of men."

## Chapters 12-13 — Enoch's commission to the Watchers

Enoch, hidden among the Watchers and holy ones, is called "scribe of righteousness." He is sent to announce to **Azâzêl**: "thou shalt have no peace: a severe sentence has gone forth against thee to put thee in bonds." The fallen Watchers, upon hearing this, weep and ask Enoch to draw up a petition for forgiveness because they themselves cannot speak before the Lord for shame. Enoch writes the petition; falls asleep at the waters of Dan south of Hermon; receives a vision of chastisement.

## Chapter 14 — Enoch's throne-vision

Enoch is borne up through clouds, wind, and lightning. He sees a wall of crystal surrounded by tongues of fire; a great house of crystal with a floor of stars and lightning, with cherubim; beyond it a second house exceeding the first in glory; and a throne of crystal with wheels "as the shining sun" — the **Great Glory** seated on it, His raiment whiter than snow, brighter than the sun, surrounded by ten thousand times ten thousand attendants. Enoch is told the petition of the Watchers will not be granted.

## Chapter 15 — God's reproach

> 15:2 The Watchers "should intercede for men, and not men for [them]."
> 15:3-4 "Wherefore have ye left the high, holy, and eternal heaven, and lain with women, and defiled yourselves with the daughters of men ... and begotten giants?"
> 15:6-7 The Watchers were created **spiritual, immortal, eternal**; they had no need of wives because there is no procreation in heaven.
> 15:8-9 "And now, the giants, who are produced from the spirits and flesh, shall be called evil spirits upon the earth, and on the earth shall be their dwelling."
> 15:11-12 These spirits "afflict, oppress, destroy, attack, do battle, and work destruction on the earth ... they rise up against the children of men and against the women, because they have proceeded from them."

**This is the etiology of demons in the Enochic tradition**: demons = the disembodied spirits of dead Nephilim. Distinct from the Lucifer-fall account; the two are sometimes harmonized, sometimes left parallel.

## Chapter 16

> 16:1 The spirits of the giants, having gone forth from their slaughtered flesh, shall destroy without judgment until the day of the great consummation.
> 16:3 "You [Watchers] have been in heaven, but all the mysteries had not yet been revealed to you, and you knew worthless ones, and these in the hardness of your hearts you have made known to the women, and through these mysteries women and men work much evil on earth."
> 16:4 "Say to them therefore: 'You have no peace.'"

This list is **load-bearing** for the Lewisian-hybrid (#2) approach in `magic-theology-approaches.md`: the catalogue is precisely the forbidden version. The faithful-Watchers axis (the unnamed obedient remainder) authorizes a parallel licit version of each art via the same celestial categories — the same metallurgy and herbalism, sourced from holy not fallen Watchers.

## Cross-references

- Mt. Hermon as the descent-site is identified via 6:6 etymology (paronomasia: *Hermon* / *herem* "ban/oath").
- The 200 figure (6:6) is the basis for "two hundred fallen angels" wherever it appears in subsequent angelology.
- 70-generation binding (10:12) is the basis for "until the appointed time" in Jude 6, 2 Pet 2:4.
- Jude 14-15 quotes 1 Enoch 1:9 directly ("Behold, the Lord cometh with ten thousands of his holy ones to execute judgment...").
