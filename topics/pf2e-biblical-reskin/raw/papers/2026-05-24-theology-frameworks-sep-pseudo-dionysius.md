# Pseudo-Dionysius the Areopagite (SEP)

## Identity and Dating
- Late 5th / early 6th century CE author writing under the pseudonym of St. Dionysius the Areopagite (Acts 17, Paul's convert).
- Lorenzo Valla in 1457 first definitively challenged the attribution; modern scholarship dates the corpus 485-528 CE.
- Identity remains unknown; likely a Syrian Christian monk.

## The Corpus
1. **On the Divine Names** — affirmative theology
2. **On the Celestial Hierarchy** — angelic orders
3. **On the Ecclesiastical Hierarchy** — church liturgical order mirrors heaven
4. **Mystical Theology** — apophatic (negative) theology, union with God
5. Ten Letters

## The Celestial Hierarchy: Three Triads / Nine Ranks
- First triad: **Seraphim, Cherubim, Thrones** — direct contemplation of God
- Second triad: **Dominions, Powers, Authorities** (sometimes Dominations, Virtues, Powers) — cosmic governance
- Third triad: **Principalities, Archangels, Angels** — engagement with creation

The hierarchy is both ontological (degrees of being) and epistemological (degrees of divine knowledge transmitted downward).

## Neoplatonist Character
- Heavily indebted to **Proclus** (412-485 CE).
- Frames reality as a procession (proodos) from God and return (epistrophe) to God, mediated through hierarchy.
- This Christianized Neoplatonism is the philosophical scaffolding underlying medieval Christian cosmology.

## Influence
- Foundational for **Aquinas** (Summa Theologiae structurally borrows abiding-procession-return).
- Bonaventure, Eriugena, Meister Eckhart, the Rhineland mystics.
- The angelic hierarchy as nine choirs is essentially Pseudo-Dionysius's invention — pre-Dionysian Christian texts list angelic ranks but not in a fixed system of nine.

## Worldbuilding takeaway
The nine-rank scheme is a 5th-century synthesis, not Bible-literal. It's authoritative *traditional* but flexible — the source material a fantasy worldbuilder needs.
