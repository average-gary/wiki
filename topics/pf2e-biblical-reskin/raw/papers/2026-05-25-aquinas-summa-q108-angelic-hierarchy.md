# Aquinas, ST I, Q.108 — De ordinatione angelorum secundum hierarchias et ordines

## Article structure (8 articles)

1. Do all angels belong to one hierarchy?
2. In one hierarchy, is there only one order?
3. In one order, are there many angels?
4. Is the distinction of hierarchies and orders natural?
5. The names and properties of each order
6. The comparison of the orders to one another
7. Will the orders outlast the Day of Judgment?
8. Are men taken up into the angelic orders?

## Core distinction: three hierarchies by mode of receiving illumination

Per Aquinas (Art. 1, 6), the three hierarchies are distinguished by *how* angels receive divine enlightenment:

- **First hierarchy** — contemplates "types of things... proceeding from God as the first universal principle." (Relation directly to God.)
- **Second hierarchy** — contemplates how those types "depend on the universal created causes." (Universal governance.)
- **Third hierarchy** — applies the types "to particular things as depending on their causes." (Execution / particulars.)

## The nine orders (Art. 6)

| Hierarchy | Orders |
|---|---|
| First | Seraphim, Cherubim, Thrones |
| Second | Dominations, Virtues, Powers |
| Third | Principalities, Archangels, Angels |

## Rationale for threefold-of-three (Art. 2)

> "Every multitude has a beginning, a middle, and an end... a threefold order of men: the supreme... the last... and a place between these."

Each hierarchy thus internally repeats the pattern of (a) ordained principle, (b) universal mediator, (c) particular executor.

## Citations to Pseudo-Dionysius

Aquinas explicitly grounds his framework in Pseudo-Dionysius's *De Coelesti Hierarchia*:
- "Hierarchy is order, knowledge, and action." (*Coel. Hier.* iii)
- The lowest order, "Angels," are "as it were, placed in the vestibule of God." (*Coel. Hier.* vii)

## Gregory the Great variant

Aquinas notes Gregory the Great orders Principalities, Virtues, and Powers differently with respect to which orders execute particular acts. Aquinas reconciles both authorities rather than choosing.

## Load-bearing for game design

- The **threefold structure is metaphysical, not stylistic**: by mode of illumination, not by power tier. A reskin that maps angel-types to PF2e creature types should preserve *contemplation/governance/execution* as the axis, not raw CR.
- **Seraphim are not "the strongest" angels** in any martial sense — they are closest to God in contemplation. Pathfinder solars are NOT seraphim by power; they are *messengers* (the lowest order, by Aquinas's logic).
- **Archangels are second-from-bottom**, not top. Game-tradition (D&D, PF2e) inverts this. Reskin must decide: keep tradition or follow Aquinas.
