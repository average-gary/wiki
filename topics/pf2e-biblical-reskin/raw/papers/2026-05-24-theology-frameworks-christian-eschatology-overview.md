# Christian Eschatology — Survey

## Resurrection of the Dead
- OT seeds: Daniel 12:2, Job 19:25-27.
- NT centerpiece: 1 Cor 15. Paul: "sown a natural body, raised a spiritual body."
- **Two-resurrection** reading (Rev 20): righteous first, then general resurrection after the millennium — common in premillennial readings.
- **Catholic** specifies four properties of the glorified body: incorruptibility, spirituality, agility, glory (Aquinas, Summa Suppl. q.82-85).

## The Second Coming (Parousia)
- Visible, audible, worldwide return of Christ.
- Preceded by tribulation, false christs, signs and wonders, the appearance of the Antichrist (varies by tradition).

## The Millennium — Three Views

### Premillennialism
Christ returns *before* a literal 1000-year earthly kingdom.
- **Historic premillennialism** — early church (Justin, Irenaeus, Tertullian); Christ returns once, after tribulation.
- **Dispensational premillennialism** — 19th-c. invention (Darby, Scofield); adds Rapture, divides timing into pre-/mid-/post-tribulation Rapture.
- Dominant in American evangelicalism.

### Postmillennialism
Christ returns *after* a millennial age of Christian flourishing produced by the gospel's gradual triumph.
- 19th-c. American Protestant heyday; fed abolitionism and Social Gospel.
- Faded after WWI but retains adherents (theonomists, some Reformed).

### Amillennialism
The "thousand years" is symbolic — it's the present church age between the resurrection and Christ's return.
- **Augustine** (City of God, book 20) — the locus classicus.
- The dominant view of **Catholic, Orthodox, and most mainline Protestant** traditions.

## Final Judgment
- Universal Christian expectation.
- **Catholic**: distinguishes **particular judgment** (at death — heaven, purgatory, or hell) from **general judgment** (at end of history).
- **Orthodox**: avoids "purgatory" terminology but accepts an intermediate post-mortem state and prayers for the dead.
- **Protestant**: rejects purgatory; believers go directly to be with Christ ("to be absent from the body is to be with the Lord," 2 Cor 5:8).

## New Heavens and New Earth
- Revelation 21-22: New Jerusalem descends; God dwells with humans.
- "No more sea" (Rev 21:1) — the chaos-deep (Heb. *tehom*) is abolished.
- **Restoration vs annihilation** — the dominant strand (especially in N.T. Wright, Catholic eschatology, Eastern Orthodox theosis tradition) reads this as the **renewal of creation**, not its replacement. Some dispensationalist Protestants read 2 Peter 3:10-13 as cosmic annihilation followed by re-creation.

## Intermediate State Divergences
- **Catholic**: Purgatory for those in grace but imperfectly purified.
- **Orthodox**: post-mortem maturation, prayers for the dead, no formal purgatory doctrine.
- **Protestant**: generally no intermediate purification; immediate beatific vision or damnation. Some Adventists/Jehovah's Witnesses hold "soul sleep."
