# Pharmakos (G5333) — the practitioner

## Lexical gloss

- **Strong's**: "a poisoner, sorcerer, magician"
- **HELPS Word-studies**: "properly, a sorcerer; used of people using drugs and 'religious incantations'"
- **Thayer's**: pertaining to magical arts

## NT occurrences

**Revelation 21:8** — the second-death list:
> "the cowardly, the unbelieving, the abominable, murderers, the sexually immoral, **sorcerers** (pharmakois), idolaters, and all liars"

**Revelation 22:15** — the outside-the-city list:
> "Outside are the dogs and the **sorcerers** (pharmakoi) and the sexually immoral and the murderers and the idolaters, and everyone who loves and practices falsehood"

## Structural note

In both Revelation lists, pharmakos sits *adjacent to* eidololatres (idolater) and *separately from* murderer/immoral/liar. This is the same structural placement pharmakeia has in Gal 5:20. The grammar of the vice-list groups pharmakeia with **cult-offenses** (idolatry, falsehood) rather than with violence-offenses (murder) or substance-abuse offenses per se — though substance is presupposed in the technique.

## Implication for translation

Because the agent-noun (pharmakos) is built on the same root and embedded in the same cultic-vice cluster, "sorcerer" (one who works magic by drug-and-incantation) is the dominant NT sense. Pure "drug-user" or pure "poisoner" undertranslates the cultic dimension; pure "magician" undertranslates the technical-substance dimension.
