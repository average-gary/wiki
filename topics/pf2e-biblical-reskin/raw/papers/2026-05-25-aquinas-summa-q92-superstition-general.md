# Aquinas, ST II-II, Q.92 — De superstitione

## Article structure (2 articles)

1. Whether superstition is a vice contrary to religion.
2. Whether there are various species of superstition.

## Definition (Art. 1, Respondeo)

> "Superstition is a vice contrary to religion by excess, not that it offers more to the divine worship than true religion, but because it offers divine worship either to whom it ought not, or in a manner it ought not."

This is the load-bearing definition. Note: superstition is NOT defined as "false belief" or "magic" but as **misdirected or maldirected worship**.

## Species of superstition (Art. 2)

Aquinas identifies three species, which structure Q.93-96:

1. **Improper mode** — worshipping the true God "in an undue mode" (Q.93: undue worship of true God).
2. **Idolatry** — giving "divine honor to a creature" rather than God alone (Q.94).
3. **Divinatory / magical practices** — consulting demons via explicit or tacit compact (Q.95-96).

## Load-bearing for game design

- Aquinas's frame is **demonological-Augustinian**: any "magic" that works via signs/tokens lacking natural causal power must work via demonic compact (explicit or tacit). There is no neutral arcane.
- For a Reformed/Augustinian-leaning reskin, this rules out PF2e's category of "arcane" as morally neutral. Either:
  - (a) arcane caster must be reframed as demonic/forbidden, OR
  - (b) all PC spellcasting must be "divine" (trusting in God) or "natural" (operating via real causes God built into creation).
