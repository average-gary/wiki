# Strong's 5331 — pharmakeia (φαρμακεία)

## Strong's headword

> "medication ('pharmacy'), i.e. (by extension) magic (literally or figuratively) — sorcery, witchcraft"

Derived from **pharmakeus** (G5332, "sorcerer / dispenser of drugs"), itself from **pharmakon** (drug, potion, spell-substance).

## Thayer's senses (verbatim summary)

a. the use or the administering of drugs
b. poisoning
c. sorcery, magical arts, often found in connection with idolatry and fostered by it
d. figuratively, the deceptions and seductions of idolatry

## NT occurrences

- **Galatians 5:20** — among works of the flesh, between "idolatry" and "enmity / strife"
- **Revelation 9:21** — alongside murders, sexual immorality, thefts; textual variant `pharmakon` (Nestle-Aland) vs `pharmakeia` (Textus Receptus)
- **Revelation 18:23** — Babylon's "pharmakeia" deceived all nations
- Related noun **pharmakos** (G5333): Rev 21:8, 22:15 — the agent/practitioner

## KJV translation tally

- "sorcery" (2x), "witchcraft" (1x); pharmakos as "sorcerer" (2x)

## Key lexical observation

The drug-sense is **etymologically primary** but biblically the term is consistently embedded in lists of occult/idolatrous practice. Thayer's "(c)" is the locus of NT meaning — magical arts whose technique involves pharmaka but whose offense is the idolatry/rebellion they enact.
