# Denominational lens decision

The **meta-decision** for any Christian-Biblical PF2e reskin: which denominational tradition frames the canon. This is not optional — every choice (which archangels are named, what the deuterocanon contributes, whether the magic-theology problem allows continuationist charisms, what counts as a "saint" for Champion causes) flows from this choice. Pretending you've avoided the choice = defaulting to the worldbuilder's own assumed lens.

## The six lenses

| Lens | Canon | Angels/Demons | Magic-theology stance | Eschatology / planes | Saints / Champion causes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Catholic** | 73 books (deuterocanon incl. Tobit's Raphael) | Full 9-choir Pseudo-Dionysian; Michael/Gabriel/Raphael named | **Continuationist**; sacraments + sacramentals = ritual mechanics | Heaven, Hell, Purgatory (condition), Limbo | Vast: named patrons for every cause; relics as Thaumaturge implements |
| **Eastern Orthodox** | Septuagint + 1–3 Macc, Ps 151, Pr Manasseh | 9-choir + 7 archangels; rich demonology | **Continuationist**; theosis as PC arc; uncreated light | Heaven, Hell, **Aerial Toll Houses** (theologoumenon), Hades intermediate | Saints + icons as power-anchors; iconographer subclass |
| **Reformed/Calvinist** | 66 books | Flat — angel/archangel only; suspicious of speculation | **Cessationist** — hostile to ongoing Cleric magic | Heaven, Hell only | None — Champion causes must be abstract (Justice, Mercy) not patron-saints |
| **Evangelical/Pentecostal** | 66 books | Active spiritual-warfare cosmology; territorial spirits, principalities | **Strong continuationist** — best fit for charisms-as-magic | Heaven, Hell; rapture/millennium variants | Limited — Bible heroes only, no post-canon saints |
| **Anglican / Lutheran** | 39 + apocrypha (non-doctrinal) | 9-choir respected but not central | Lutheran continuationist-cautious; Anglican varies | Heaven, Hell; intermediate state without purgatory | Calendar of saints honored without invocation |
| **Lewisian / "mere"** | Defers (use 66 as common denominator) | Generic angel/demon; no fixed hierarchy | Brackets the question | Heaven, Hell; defers on intermediate states | Brackets — Champion causes only |

(Restorationist / Latter-Day Saint is explicitly out of scope — additional canon (Book of Mormon, D&C, Pearl of Great Price) represents a fundamentally different theological framework from the historic-creedal Christianity this reskin targets.)

## The load-bearing axis: cessationism vs continuationism

Per [[2026-05-24-denominational-scope-cessationism-continuationism]] — this is the **single doctrine that most narrows which denominational lens can host PF2e Divine magic without theological friction**.

- **Cessationist** (Reformed-leaning, some Baptist): the apostolic charisms (tongues, prophecy, healing as miraculous) ceased with the closing of the canon. Implication: ongoing Cleric magic is theologically awkward; canonical clergy should not be regularly performing miracles. Pushes the reskin toward [[magic-theology-approaches]] approach #3 (all-magic-demonic) or #4 (strict-historicist), where Cleric is rare/unique/biblical-figure-only.
- **Continuationist** (Catholic, Orthodox, Pentecostal, charismatic Anglican/Lutheran): charisms remain active. Implication: PF2e Divine tradition maps directly to charisms (1 Cor 12). Approach #1 or hybrid #1+#2 is natural fit.

If your audience is Reformed/cessationist, Cleric-as-routine-spellcaster is theologically loaded; you may need to either reframe Cleric as biblical-figure-only or commit to approach #3.

## The canon axis

Catholic and Orthodox accept the deuterocanon (Tobit, Wisdom, Sirach, Baruch, 1–2 Macc, additions); Protestant 66-book canon does not. Implication for lore:

- **Tobit** gives **Raphael** as named archangel and **Asmodeus** as named demon — both load-bearing for a PF2e reskin.
- **2 Maccabees** anchors **prayers for the dead** and **martyrs** — both gameable.
- **Wisdom of Solomon** gives **Lady Wisdom** as cosmic personification — gameable as quasi-deity / herald / empyreal lord.
- **1 Enoch** (non-canonical to most, canonical to Ethiopian Orthodox) gives the **Watchers** and **Nephilim** mythology — load-bearing for the angelology / demonology / ancestry mappings in [[biblical-cosmology-pf2e-mapping]].

A Protestant-frame setting can still **reference** Tobit's Raphael and 1 Enoch's Watchers as in-world apocrypha that PCs encounter — you just cite the source explicitly rather than naturalizing it as Author Truth.

## The angelic-hierarchy axis

Per [[2026-05-24-denominational-scope-angelic-hierarchy]]:

- **Catholic**: nine-rank Pseudo-Dionysian, 3 named archangels (Michael, Gabriel, Raphael).
- **Orthodox**: nine-rank Pseudo-Dionysian, **7 named archangels** (+ Uriel, Selaphiel, Jegudiel, Barachiel).
- **Protestant**: usually flat (angel / archangel); 2 named (Michael, Gabriel); Raphael contested due to Tobit; Uriel/Selaphiel/etc. typically not.
- **Lewisian**: defers on hierarchy; uses generic angel / archangel.

A reskin needs to pick a hierarchy. The Pseudo-Dionysian nine-rank scheme **is the richest fantasy material** and is already mostly aligned with PF2e's celestial creature ladder ([[biblical-cosmology-pf2e-mapping]]). Adopting it is a Catholic/Orthodox-leaning choice.

## The saint-and-Champion-cause axis

- **Catholic / Orthodox**: vast roster of named saints; Champion causes can attach to specific saints (St George the dragon-slayer, St Michael the warrior, St Raphael the healer-guide). Relics as Thaumaturge implements work natively.
- **Reformed**: explicit rejection of saint-veneration. Champion causes must be **abstract virtues** (Justice, Mercy, Liberation) attached to YHWH directly. Relics theologically loaded.
- **Pentecostal**: limited — Bible heroes only (Joshua, Daniel, Paul) as exemplars, not patrons.
- **Anglican / Lutheran**: **honored but not invoked** — saints appear in the calendar but Champion causes don't attach to them.

This is one of the biggest design-impact axes. **The Catholic/Orthodox lens unlocks a ready-made roster of patron saints**, each of which can serve as a Champion cause flavor; the Reformed lens forbids it.

## Strategy patterns

### 1. Pick-one-and-state-it (cleanest)

Pick a single denominational lens and write the setting bible inside it. **Cleanest, most coherent, narrowest audience.** Tolkien chose this (implicit Catholic). Most successful Christian fantasy authors do.

### 2. Multi-lens / "varies by region in setting"

Different in-world cultures hold different theological views. **Risks turning real-world disputes into fantasy factions** in a way that flattens both the lore and the actual disputes. Hard to do well; easy to produce caricature.

### 3. Ecumenical / mere-Christianity default (widest audience)

Aim at the consensus across traditions; flag denomination-specific options as setting variants. C.S. Lewis's *Mere Christianity* preface: a hallway, not a room; the room is one of the denominations. **Best for a wide-audience product; lowest denominational specificity; risks being theologically thin** unless paired with strong worldbuilding elsewhere.

### 4. Setting-as-meta-question

Make the in-world theology itself a campaign theme — the Reformation as plot, the East-West Schism as world-history backdrop, the Council of Nicaea as adventure path. **Higher creative ceiling**; requires more theological literacy from the GM. Highest reward when it works; highest failure mode when it doesn't.

## Decision aid: "If you want X, pick Y"

| If you want... | Pick |
|---|---|
| Widest commercial / table audience | **Lewisian / "mere Christianity"** (strategy #3). Risk: thin if not bolstered. |
| **Richest ready-made fantasy furniture** | **Catholic** (strategy #1). Tolkien's choice. Relics, saints, sacraments, purgatory, nine choirs, all "just work." |
| Most distinctive aesthetic, least crowded | **Eastern Orthodox** (strategy #1). Theosis, icons, hesychasm, Aerial Toll Houses — largely untapped in tabletop. |
| Cleanest spiritual-warfare action | **Pentecostal / Charismatic** (strategy #1). Peretti-style. Most natural Cleric/Champion fit. |
| Highest theological rigor, hardest design | **Reformed** (strategy #1). Cessationism forces creative engagement with [[magic-theology-approaches]]. |
| Compromise / broadest Western Christian | **Anglican / Lutheran** (strategy #1). Hooker's "scripture, tradition, reason" middle. |
| Theological depth as campaign content | **Setting-as-meta-question** (strategy #4) on top of any single lens. |

## Worked examples

### Catholic-Recommended Default (path of least design friction)

73-book canon. Full 9-choir bestiary. Seven sacraments as ritual subsystem. Mary as Queen of Heaven NPC. Named patron-saint Champion causes (St George dragon-slayer, St Michael warrior, St Raphael healer-guide). Thaumaturge implements = relics. Purgatory as a transitional plane. All charisms active (continuationist). Closest in tone to Tolkien's *Silmarillion* and to a medieval-Catholic vibe. **This is the highest-leverage default for a wide-audience product** — most fantasy material, most class-reskin wins, most named-NPC roster.

### Orthodox Alternative

Septuagint canon + Enoch as variant. 7 archangels. Theosis as level-20 capstone (PC literally radiates uncreated light). Icons as scrying/buff anchors. Iconographer Monk-archetype. **Aerial Toll Houses as a literal level-15+ post-mortem mega-dungeon.** Hesychast Jesus-Prayer as a continuous Focus-spell mechanic. **Most distinctive aesthetic** in the genre.

### Pentecostal Alternative

66-book canon. Flat angel/demon binary but rich *demonic* hierarchy (territorial spirits, strongholds). Spiritual warfare as the explicit campaign frame (Peretti's *This Present Darkness* as cultural touchstone). Cleric = anointed evangelist with healing/tongues/prophecy. No saints, no Mary, no purgatory. Focus on revival, deliverance, conversion as in-game beats. **Cleanest fit for [[magic-theology-approaches]] approach #1 (charism)**.

### Reformed Alternative (hardest)

66-book canon. Flat angelology. **Cessationist**: routine Cleric magic theologically awkward; default toward [[magic-theology-approaches]] approach #3 (all-magic-demonic) or #4 (strict-historicist). Champion causes are abstract virtues attached to YHWH directly. Covenant theology (Abraham/Moses/Davidic/New) as worldbuilding scaffold — unique strength. **Hardest design problem; deepest theological rigor.**

### Lewisian / "mere Christianity"

Defers on canon (use 66 as floor). Generic archangel imagery. Brackets cessationism. Brackets Marian theology. Champion causes as Beatitude-tenets, not patron-saints. **Widest audience, thinnest distinctive material.** Pair with [[magic-theology-approaches]] approach #2 (Lewisian/Tolkienian) for internal consistency.
