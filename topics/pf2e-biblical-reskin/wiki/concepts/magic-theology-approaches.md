# Magic-theology approaches

The single hardest design problem for a PF2e Biblical reskin: **how to handle "magic" given the Bible's explicit prohibitions on sorcery** (Deut 18:10–12; Lev 19:31; Gal 5:20; Rev 21:8 — `pharmakeia`/`mageia`), while PF2e's classes are magic-saturated.

## The sources of the prohibition

Per [[2026-05-24-magic-theology-problem-deuteronomy-18-prohibitions]], the OT/NT prohibitions name *specific practices*, not "supernatural effect" generically:

- **Deut 18:10–12** — diviner, soothsayer, augur, sorcerer, charmer, medium, necromancer.
- **Lev 19:31, 20:6, 20:27** — mediums and spiritists.
- **Gal 5:20** — `pharmakeia` (illicit ritual technique fusing drugs, incantation, and idolatrous intent; Thayer's four senses span drug-administration, poisoning, sorcery, and figurative idolatrous deception — see [[2026-05-25-pharmakeia-word-study-strongs-thayers]]) among works of the flesh.
- **Rev 9:21, 18:23, 21:8, 22:15** — `pharmakeia` / `pharmakoi` among the damned. The vice-list placement is **cultic, not pharmacological** — `pharmakeia` sits beside idolatry and falsehood, grouped with cult-offenses against the rightful God ([[2026-05-25-pharmakeia-word-study-pharmakos-revelation]]).
- **Acts 8:9–24** — Simon Magus (transactional purchase of power).
- **Acts 13:6–12** — Elymas / Bar-Jesus.

What's banned: **the source and the mode** (techne, transaction, pact with non-divine spirits) — not "supernatural effect." Prophet-style miracles never fall under these terms.

## Six approaches compared

| Approach | Theology | PF2e classes kept | PF2e classes cut | Table fun | Mechanical cleanness |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Charism model** (Catholic-charismatic) | Only Spirit-gifted miracles allowed (1 Cor 12) | Cleric, Champion, Oracle, partial Bard, partial Druid | Wizard, Witch, Sorcerer, Magus, Summoner, Psychic, Thaumaturge | Restrictive but principled | Excellent — 1 Cor 12 maps to spell schools |
| **2. Lewisian / Tolkienian** | Magic = sub-creation/conduit; question is *which authority* | All classes (reflavored by source) | None mechanically; Witch/Warlock become "vessels" with two valid sides (angelic vs fallen) | **Highest narrative flexibility** | Medium — relies on fluff discipline |
| **3. All-magic-demonic** (Reformed Augustinian) | Non-divine spellcasting = pact with fallen powers | Cleric, Champion only; others playable as *fallen* with tragic-arc framing | Effectively all but Divine, unless redemption arc | Dark, hard-mode; not for casual tables | Easy — binary split |
| **4. Strict-historicist** (Testament-style) | OT ban literally enforced in-world | Fighter, Ranger, Rogue, Monk, Investigator, Cleric (=priest), Champion (=judge), Oracle (=prophet) | Wizard, Witch, Sorcerer, Magus, Summoner, Psychic, Thaumaturge, Druid, Bard | Lowest crunch; martials shine | Cleanest — fewer subsystems |
| **5. Concession / fallen-world** | Magic is corrupted-good awaiting redemption | All classes; players choose tragic, redeemed, or oblivious framing | None | Most player freedom | Loose — requires GM curation |
| **6. Hard-canon translation tables** | Surface-level reskin only; spell-by-spell flavor swap | All classes; no class-level surgery | None | Familiar play, deep flavor | Labor-intensive but transparent. **Lookup table:** [[biblical-miracle-to-pf2e-spell-map]] (~50 rows, seeded from [[2026-05-25-spell-to-miracle-mapping-synthesis]]). |

## The four traditions × the four serious approaches

| PF2e tradition | #1 Charism | #2 Lewisian | #3 All-Demonic | #4 Strict-Historicist |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Divine** | Kept whole; reflavored as Spirit-charismata (1 Cor 12). Cleric=priest, Champion=judge, Oracle=prophet. | Kept; explicitly the Logos-channel. | **Kept; the only legitimate tradition.** | **Kept; the *only* casting tradition.** Cleric/Oracle/Champion only. |
| **Primal** | Partial keep (creation-stewardship, healing, plant-growth). Cuts pact-with-spirits flavor. Druid → "garden-keeper" / Edenic remnant. | Kept; nature-as-sub-creation under Logos. Druid = steward of Eden's remnant. | **Cut** as nature-spirit pact / animism. | **Cut** entirely. |
| **Arcane** | **Cut.** Wizardry = `pharmakeia` / Egyptian *hartumim*. | Kept *if* reframed as "high craft / Numenorean smithcraft / Solomonic wisdom" within obedience. | **Cut** as quintessentially demonic technique. | **Cut.** |
| **Occult** | **Cut.** Mediums, ghost-binding = Saul-at-Endor sin. | Kept *only* as "discernment of spirits" + apocalyptic/Enochic visions. Heavily restricted; many subclasses cut. | **Cut** as paradigmatically forbidden. | **Cut.** |

### The 1 Enoch 8 forbidden-arts table — primary-text anchor for the Lewisian hybrid

The recommended **hybrid Charism + Lewisian** default below stands or falls on whether the "faithful Watchers vs fallen Watchers" axis has primary-text grounding. It does. 1 Enoch 8 names which Watcher taught which forbidden art ([[2026-05-25-1-enoch-book-of-watchers-ch6-16]]); the licit parallel is the same celestial category (the unfallen remainder of the original 200 hosts) sub-creating under the Logos:

| Watcher | Forbidden art (1 En 8) | PF2e tradition | Licit parallel under Logos |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Azâzêl** | swords/knives/shields/breastplates; metallurgy; cosmetics; antimony; dyes | **Arcane / craft** | Numenorean smith-craft; Bezalel-of-Exodus 31 craftsmanship |
| **Semjâzâ** | enchantments + root-cuttings | **Primal herbalism / Occult enchantment** | Solomonic herb-lore (Wis 7:20); the woman of Prov 31's "vineyard" |
| **Armârôs** | resolving (countering) enchantments | Arcane counterspell | Discernment of spirits charism (1 Cor 12:10) |
| **Barâqîjâl** | astrology | **Occult divination** | Star-of-Bethlehem reading (Mt 2:2); cf. Aquinas Q.95 a.5 — natural-causation astronomy is licit |
| **Kôkabêl** | constellations | Occult star-omen | Same as above |
| **Êzêqêêl** | clouds | Primal weather-omen | Elijah's "small cloud" (1 Kgs 18:44) — petitioned, not technologized |
| **Araqiêl** | signs of the earth | Primal geomancy | (No licit parallel — Aquinas Q.95 a.3 lists this as paradigmatically demonic) |
| **Shamsiêl** | signs of the sun | Occult solar divination | Joshua's sun-stop (Josh 10:13) — petitioned miracle |
| **Sariel** | course of the moon | Occult lunar divination | (No licit parallel — calendar-keeping is licit; lunar prediction-of-fate is not) |

**The rule that emerges**: a discipline is licit when it (a) reads natural causation God built in, or (b) is a charism received in petition, and illicit when it (c) is technologized via Watcher-pact compact. **This is exactly Aquinas's q.96 a.2 causal-vs-significatory test**, derived independently from the Enochic pre-text — converging on the same line.

## What the canonical authors actually said

**Tolkien (Letter 131, Letter 155)** — Letter 131 to Milton Waldman (~1951) collapses *magic* and *the Machine* into one category: "all use of *external* plans or devices (apparatus) instead of development of the inherent inner powers or talents — or even the use of these talents with the corrupted motive of domination; bulldozing the real world, or coercing other wills." Elvish "magic" is in scare-quotes — it is "**Art, delivered from many of its human limitations**… its object is Art not Power, sub-creation not domination and tyrannous re-forming of Creation." Sauron is "the Lord of magic and machines" — a single ontological role. Letter 155 to Naomi Mitchison (draft Sept 1954) adds the magia/goeteia distinction *and explicitly rejects the older Christian moral coding*: "Neither is, in this tale, good or bad (per se), but only by motive or purpose or use." The hinge: "**The supremely bad motive is… domination of other 'free' wills.**" Magic is "**inherent power not possessed or attainable by Men as such**" — ontological, not procedural. "Both sides live mainly by 'ordinary' means." See [[2026-05-25-tolkien-lewis-primary-letter-131-waldman]], [[2026-05-25-tolkien-lewis-primary-letter-155-mitchison]], [[2026-05-24-magic-theology-problem-tolkien-magia-vs-elvish-art]].

**C.S. Lewis (Narnia, *That Hideous Strength*, *De Descriptione Temporum*)** — two-tier law: **Deep Magic** (binding law the Witch invokes legitimately) and **Deeper Magic** (sacrificial-redemptive law). The Witch is rule-exploitation; Aslan is sacrificial obedience. The Witch literally cannot understand self-sacrifice. In *That Hideous Strength*: magic = vessel/conduit, never autonomous; Merlin is licit only when possessed by Oyéresu; N.I.C.E.'s scientism is pact-magic with Macrobes. **The moral question is "by whose authority?"** Lewis's 1954 Cambridge inaugural *De Descriptione Temporum* gives the periodization that frames the whole problem: the Great Divide of the West "divides the present from… the age of Jane Austen and Scott" — "**Between Jane Austen and us, but not between her and Shakespeare, Chaucer, Alfred, Virgil, Homer, or the Pharaohs, comes the birth of the machines.**" The modern is post-Christian, *not pagan*: "**A post-Christian man is not a Pagan; you might as well think that a married woman recovers her virginity by divorce.**" This converges with Tolkien Letter 131: the modern technological imagination is the present-day form of the same disordered will-to-power that ancient sorcery represented. See [[2026-05-25-tolkien-lewis-primary-de-descriptione-temporum]], [[2026-05-24-magic-theology-problem-lewis-deep-magic-narnia]], [[2026-05-24-magic-theology-problem-that-hideous-strength-merlin]].

**Augustine + Aquinas** — Augustine: all magic is demonic (*De Doctrina Christiana*). Aquinas builds the systematic frame in *Summa Theologica* II-II, qq. 92–96:

- **Q.92 a.1 (definition of superstition)**: "Superstition is a vice contrary to religion by excess, not that it offers more to the divine worship than true religion, but because it offers divine worship either to whom it ought not, or in a manner it ought not." Superstition is *misdirected worship*, not "false belief."
- **Q.92 a.2 (three species)**: improper-mode worship (q.93), idolatry (q.94), and divinatory/magical practices (qq.95–96).
- **Q.95 a.1 (divination as usurpation)**: "If anyone presume to foreknow or foretell such like future things by any means whatever, except by divine revelation, he manifestly usurps what belongs to God."
- **Q.95 a.2**: "All divination results from the demons' operation" — either by explicit invocation, or because demons "thrust themselves into futile searchings of the future, in order to entangle men's minds with vain conceits."
- **Q.95 a.3**: three principal species — express demonic invocation (necromancy, geomancy, hydromancy, aeromancy, pyromancy), observation without express invocation (astrology, augury, omens, chiromancy), and active sortilege.
- **Q.95 a.5 (astrology)**: licit when forecasting effects with real natural causation (eclipses, weather); superstitious when used to predict "casual or fortuitous future events, or to know with certitude future human actions."
- **Q.96 a.1 (magic art)**: unlawful "because the means it employs for acquiring knowledge have not in themselves the power to cause science"; the signs are "empty signs" constituting "a kind of agreement or covenant made with the demons."
- **Q.96 a.2 (the causal-vs-significatory test)**: practices are superstitious when "they are employed for the purpose of producing those effects, not as causes but only as signs."
- **Q.96 a.4 (amulets)**: sacred words at the neck are licit only if they contain "nothing vain" (canonical Scripture only) and exclude "certain written characters, except the sign of the Cross."

The **Aquinas test** for any magical practice: does the means have natural causal power (licit), is the effect by divine grace (licit), or is the means a sign/token without natural causal power (superstition; "works" only by tacit or express demonic compact)? **Christianity is starker than Judaism/Islam on this**; the biblical worldview accepts magic produces real effects but routes them demonically. See [[2026-05-25-aquinas-summa-q92-superstition-general]], [[2026-05-25-aquinas-summa-q95-q96-divination-magic]], [[2026-05-24-magic-theology-problem-augustine-aquinas-miracle-vs-magic]].

**Acts 8 / Simon Magus** — magic = **transactional purchase**; miracle = grace-gift. "Simony" preserves the boundary. Apocryphal Peter-vs-Simon = real-power-collapses-before-prayer trope. See [[2026-05-24-magic-theology-problem-simon-magus-acts-8]].

**1 Cor 12** — nine charisms (word of wisdom, knowledge, faith, healing, miracles, prophecy, discernment of spirits, tongues, interpretation): gift not earned, communal not personal, Spirit-manifested not self-manifested, petition not technique. **The positive list to map PF2e Divine onto.** See [[2026-05-24-magic-theology-problem-charismata-1-corinthians-12]].

## Recommended default

**Hybrid #1 + #2 (Charism-Lewisian)**:

- **Divine-tradition classes** are charismata (sent, anointed) — pure approach #1.
- **Arcane / Occult / Primal** are reskinned as Lewisian conduits where the player chooses the source: angelic Watchers (the un-fallen Watchers, faithful to YHWH — gameable per the Enochic tradition that distinguishes obedient Watchers from fallen ones) vs fallen Watchers (paktos with the fallen powers).
- This keeps **every PF2e class playable**, gives every casting class real moral stakes, and avoids both legalism (#1 alone cuts too much) and outright demonization (#3 alone darkens the table).
- **Audience considerations**:
  - Reformed audience → tilt toward #3 (all non-divine = demonic); cessationism makes the magic-theology problem hardest. See [[denominational-lens-decision]].
  - Catholic / Orthodox / charismatic audience → #1 + #2 hybrid is natural fit.
  - Mere-Christianity audience → straight #2 Lewisian is the default ([[denominational-lens-decision]]).
  - Strict-historical audience (Testament-style) → #4 cuts most classes but maximal theological cleanness.

## Decision support

| If you want... | Pick |
|---|---|
| Most classes playable | #2 Lewisian or #5 Concession (all classes survive with fluff swaps) |
| Most theologically defensible | #1 Charism (rigorously biblical) or #3 All-demonic (rigorously Augustinian-Reformed) |
| Most fun at the table | #2 Lewisian/Tolkienian — gives players agency and moral weight without mechanical castration |
| Cleanest mechanical mapping | #1 Charism (1 Cor 12 → Divine spell schools) or #3 All-demonic (binary) |
| Best for a Reformed/cessationist audience | #3 All-demonic or #4 Strict-historicist |
| Best for a Catholic/Orthodox/charismatic audience | #1 Charism + #2 Lewisian hybrid |
