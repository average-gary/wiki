# PF2e Biblical Reskin

## Driving Case

Re-skin **Pathfinder 2e** lore to fit a **Christian Biblical worldview** as a campaign setting (and ultimately a content pack for the [[pf2e-worldbuilding-tool]]). The PF2e Remaster (2023–2024) removed alignment, typed energy as holy/unholy/spirit/void/vitality, and recast deities through a sanctification framework — these changes happen to make a monotheistic-canon reskin *more mechanically natural* than it was under the OGL editions. This topic surveys how to do that well: what's been tried, what works, what doesn't.

The output should be useful to:
- A GM running a Christian-fantasy PF2e campaign at the table
- A worldbuilder authoring a setting bible
- A content-pack author shipping the reskin as data for the worldbuilding tool ([[pf2e-licensing-posture]] applies)

## Sub-questions (research paths)

`--plan --deep` round on 2026-05-24, 6 paths × 8 agents:

1. **prior-christian-ttrpg-history** — DragonRaid, Heroes Unlimited Christian splats, Bibleman, Roleplaying Tips Christian campaign threads, indie projects (Dust to Dust, Testament d20, Soulforge), homebrew traditions on r/DnDBehindTheScreen / r/Pathfinder2e. What's been tried over 40 years; what worked, what flopped.
2. **remaster-fit-for-monotheism** — How the PF2e Remaster's structural changes (alignment removed, sanctified/anointed framework, holy/unholy/spirit/void/vitality damage, schools removed) make a Biblical reskin more or less mechanically natural. Where you still have to bend rules.
3. **theology-frameworks** — Mapping the Biblical cosmology (Heaven/Hell, the Fall, hierarchies of angels per Pseudo-Dionysius, demons, Nephilim, Sheol/Hades, the New Jerusalem) onto PF2e planes/creatures.
4. **magic-theology-problem** — The biggest design problem: how to handle "magic" when much of the Biblical text condemns sorcery (Deut 18:10–12, Gal 5:20, Rev 21:8). Options: charism model, Lewisian re-mythologizing, all-magic-is-demonic, strict-historicist. Pros/cons of each.
5. **class-ancestry-reskin** — How to reskin PF2e classes (Cleric, Champion, Druid, Sorcerer, Witch, Wizard, etc.) and ancestries (Aasimar→Nephilim, Tiefling, Dhampir, Orcs) to fit a Biblical lens.
6. **denominational-scope** — Which denominational lens (Catholic, Protestant — including Reformed / Evangelical / Pentecostal subdivisions, Eastern Orthodox, non-sectarian "mere Christianity" Lewis-style) frames the canon. Affects what counts as a "saint," which deuterocanonical books supply lore (Tobit's Raphael, 1 Enoch's Watchers, Wisdom of Solomon, 2 Maccabees), how angelology is structured, what "magic" means, and what the eschaton looks like. Strategy patterns: pick-one-and-state-it; multi-lens / "varies by region in setting"; ecumenical / mere-Christianity default.
