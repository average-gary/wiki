# Stacker News — Braidpool community thread

Captures the demand-side / cultural barrier that protocol design alone can't solve.

## Key quotes / claims
- Miners are *"blue collar mercenaries who just plug the machines in and make money"* — ideological decentralization arguments are an "uphill battle" against centralized pool UX/payout-smoothing.
- Implicit historical concession on original P2Pool: *"P2Pool couldn't scale and it's not being used anymore... when more miners joined, difficulty required for shares grew, making it more difficult for small miners."*
- Acknowledges p2poolv2/Braidpool is *"still in early development and not ready to use yet"* — confirms adoption gap.

## Why ingest
Lower-tier source than the technical critiques but useful for the **demand-side** framing: even with perfect SV2 + p2poolv2 integration, the miner-decision-function is not "decentralization > variance smoothing." Any go-to-market plan must contend with this.
