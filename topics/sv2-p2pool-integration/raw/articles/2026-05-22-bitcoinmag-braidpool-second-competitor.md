# Bitcoin Magazine — Braidpool: A Second Competitor In Decentralizing Mining

Frames the demand-side / variance / bootstrap critique that SV2 + p2poolv2 do not solve at the protocol layer.

## Bootstrap problem
> Any actual Braidpool must quickly grow to a sizable enough portion of the network to smooth out volatility... or those miners stay with a pool not achieving that growth will simply wind up losing themselves money.

There is no "Braidpool company" to subsidize early variance. Same problem applies to p2poolv2.

## UX burden
Every miner must run a full node. Operational barrier vs. plug-and-play centralized pools.

## Ocean's stagnation as ceiling signal
Even Ocean (the well-funded, mainstream-press-covered competitor) shows "no continued growth" despite first-mover credibility. Suggests a structural demand-side ceiling for decentralized pools as a category.

## Self-sabotage risk
Contentious tx-filtering decisions actively repel hashrate (Ocean's own historical frictions cited).

## Implication
Stratum V2's job declaration is necessary but not sufficient: it gives miners *the option* to select txs, but doesn't change the variance math that keeps them at centralized pools. p2poolv2 inherits this demand-side problem.
