# BOLT #12 — Offer Encoding

## What it is

An **offer** is a reusable, static payment request. Flow: `offer` →
`invoice_request` → `invoice` → payment.

> "An offer can be much longer-lived than a particular invoice_request: readers will
> request an invoice (or multiple) based on the offer." *(BOLT #12)*

Offers carry **no `payment_hash`** — they are reusable templates, not single-use
invoices. Interactive invoice fetch enables recurring payments (subscriptions,
donations, **recurring pool payouts**) and receiver-privacy via blinded paths.

## Why it matters to follow-up thesis #3

BOLT12 is the **payout-delivery rail** the comparator (OCEAN) uses: a miner
publishes **one** offer and the pool pays it every payout cycle with **no new
invoice** and **no on-chain footprint per payout**. See ingested OCEAN docs:
[[../articles/2026-07-23-ocean-bolt12-lightning-payouts|OCEAN BOLT12 payouts]].

**Layer caveat (mechanistic):** BOLT #12 governs message *encoding/negotiation* only.
The actual balance movement on receipt is HTLC settlement per BOLT #2/#3 — an
incoming HTLC settles the payer's (remote) balance onto the receiver's **local**
side. So *receiving* a BOLT12 payout **consumes the miner's pre-existing inbound**
liquidity and converts it to outbound; it does **not** create inbound. See
[[../../wiki/concepts/inbound-vs-outbound-liquidity|inbound vs outbound liquidity]].

and donations" not possible with BOLT11's one-time-use hashlocks
