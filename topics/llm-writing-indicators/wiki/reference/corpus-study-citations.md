# Reference: corpus-study & key-paper citations

Citable primary sources for the claims in this wiki, grouped by what they establish.

## Vocabulary shift (the lexical tells are real)

- **Kobak, González-Márquez, Horvát & Lause (2025).** "Delving into LLM-assisted writing in biomedical publications through excess vocabulary." *Science Advances* 11(27). arXiv:2406.07016. — 15.3M PubMed abstracts; delves ×28; ≥13.5% LLM-processed. [[../../raw/papers/2026-07-13-kobak-excess-vocabulary-pubmed|ingest]]
- **Liang et al. (2024).** "Monitoring AI-Modified Content at Scale… AI Conference Peer Reviews." *ICML 2024.* arXiv:2403.07183. — meticulous ×34.7; 6.5–16.9% modified. [[../../raw/papers/2026-07-13-liang-peer-reviews-ai-modified|ingest]]
- **Liang et al. (2024).** "Mapping the Increasing Use of LLMs in Scientific Papers." *COLM 2024.* arXiv:2404.01268. — 950k papers; CS ~17.5%. [[../../raw/papers/2026-07-13-liang-scientific-papers-mapping|ingest]]
- **Juzek & Ward (2025).** "Why Does ChatGPT 'Delve' So Much?" *COLING 2025.* arXiv:2412.11385. — RLHF cause; Nigerian-English theory not supported. [[../../raw/papers/2026-07-13-juzek-ward-why-delve|ingest]]

## Tells leaking into human language

- **Yakura, Brinkmann et al. (2024/25).** "Empirical evidence of LLM's influence on human spoken communication." Max Planck. arXiv:2409.01754. — 740k hours; delve/meticulous/realm rising in speech. [[../../raw/papers/2026-07-13-yakura-llm-influence-spoken|ingest]]

## Human & automated detection

- **Jakesch, Hancock & Naaman (2023).** "Human heuristics for AI-generated language are flawed." *PNAS* 120(11). — humans ~50%; invalid cues. [[../../raw/papers/2026-07-13-jakesch-human-heuristics-flawed|ingest]]
- **Mitchell et al. (2023).** "DetectGPT: … Probability Curvature." *ICML 2023.* arXiv:2301.11305. [[../../raw/papers/2026-07-13-mitchell-detectgpt|ingest]]
- **Hans et al. (2024).** "Spotting LLMs With Binoculars." *ICML 2024.* arXiv:2401.12070. [[../../raw/papers/2026-07-13-hans-binoculars|ingest]]
- **Kirchenbauer et al. (2023).** "A Watermark for Large Language Models." *ICML 2023 (Outstanding Paper).* arXiv:2301.10226. [[../../raw/papers/2026-07-13-kirchenbauer-watermark|ingest]]

## Detection limits & fairness

- **Liang, Yuksekgonul, Mao, Wu & Zou (2023).** "GPT detectors are biased against non-native English writers." *Patterns* (Cell Press). arXiv:2304.02819. — 61.22% FP on non-native essays. [[../../raw/papers/2026-07-13-liang-detectors-biased-nonnative|ingest]]
- **Krishna et al. (2023).** "Paraphrasing Evades Detectors… (DIPPER)." *NeurIPS 2023.* arXiv:2303.13408. — 70.3%→4.6%. [[../../raw/papers/2026-07-13-krishna-dipper-paraphrase-evasion|ingest]]
- **Sadasivan et al. (2023).** "Can AI-Generated Text be Reliably Detected?" arXiv:2303.11156. — AUROC→0.5 ceiling. [[../../raw/papers/2026-07-13-sadasivan-can-ai-text-be-detected|ingest]]
- **Shaib et al. (2025).** "Measuring AI 'Slop' in Text." arXiv:2509.19163. — structural taxonomy; GPT-5 0.14 precision. [[../../raw/papers/2026-07-13-shaib-measuring-ai-slop|ingest]]
