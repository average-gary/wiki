# "Not X but Y" antithesis

A signature rhetorical construction: the negative-parallelism frame that sets up a dismissed alternative only to pivot to the "real" answer.

## Forms

- "It's not just X, it's Y."
- "It is not about X; it's about Y."
- "not only X, but also Y."
- Wikipedia's real example: "is not a mirror but a portal."

The construction feels insightful and quotable, which is exactly why RLHF-tuned models over-produce it — it pattern-matches to the punchy, TED-talk register that human raters reward.

## Why it's a tell

Humans use antithesis, but sparingly and with earned contrast. LLMs deploy it as a default "profound-sounding" template, often where the X and Y are not actually opposed. When a piece hits this construction repeatedly — especially in openers and closers — it clusters with [[rule-of-three|rule of three]] and [[structural-formulas|structural formulas]] into a recognizable "LinkedIn-thought-leader" cadence.

## Confidence

Medium. It is distinctive but also a genuine human device and increasingly imitated. Use only as part of a cluster.
