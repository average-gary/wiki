# Formulaic phrases

Beyond single words, LLMs reach reflexively for a small set of multi-word connectives and hedges. These are stickier tells than individual words because the exact string recurs.

## Editorializing openers

- "It's important to note (that)…"
- "It's worth noting…"
- "It is essential to understand…"
- "Importantly," / "Notably,"

These announce significance without adding content. A human editor cuts them; an LLM emits them as scaffolding.

## Canned transitions and framings

- "That being said," / "At its core," / "To put it simply,"
- "This underscores the importance of…"
- "A key takeaway is…" / "From a broader perspective,"
- Over-use of "Moreover," "Furthermore," "Consequently," "Nevertheless," "Additionally" (especially sentence-initial "Additionally,").

## Vague attribution / weasel phrases

Statements sourced to no one in particular:
- "Industry reports suggest…" / "Observers have cited…" / "Experts argue…"
- "Some critics argue…" / "Several sources/publications…"

Per [[../../raw/papers/2026-07-13-liang-peer-reviews-ai-modified|Liang et al.]], LLM-heavy text specifically *lacks* concrete citations — vague attribution is the filler that replaces them.

## Hedging qualifiers

"Generally speaking," "typically," "tends to," "arguably," "to some extent," "broadly speaking." A little hedging is normal human caution; a paragraph where every claim is softened is a register tell (see [[sycophancy-and-positivity-register|sycophancy & positivity]]).

## Why it matters

The exact-string recurrence makes these greppable. A document with three or more of these phrases, none carrying real information, alongside [[lexical-overuse-words|vocabulary overuse]], is a strong cluster.
