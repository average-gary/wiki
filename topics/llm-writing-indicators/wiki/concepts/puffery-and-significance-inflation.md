# Puffery & significance inflation

LLMs habitually inflate the importance of whatever they describe. Everything "stands as a testament," "plays a vital role," or "marks a pivotal moment." This editorializing register is one of the most recognizable tells and a top category in the [[../../raw/articles/2026-07-13-wikipedia-signs-of-ai-writing|Wikipedia AISIGNS catalog]].

## Significance-inflation phrases

- "stands/serves as a…" / "is a testament/reminder to/of…"
- "plays a vital / significant / crucial / pivotal / key role"
- "underscores/highlights its importance"
- "represents/marks a shift" / "key turning point" / "pivotal moment in the evolution"
- "reflects broader…" / "setting the stage for…" / "leaving an indelible mark"

## Promotional puffery

- "rich cultural heritage," "vibrant," "rich history," "natural beauty"
- "nestled in the heart of" / "nestled within the breathtaking region"
- "groundbreaking," "renowned," "diverse array," "enduring legacy"

## The copula-avoidance correlate

A measured tell: a **~10% decline in plain "is/are"** in post-2023 text, as models replace simple copulas with inflated verbs — "serves as," "marks," "represents," "features" (for "has"), "stands as," "offers," "maintains." When a text never just says something *is* X but always *serves as* or *stands as* X, that is significance inflation at the grammatical level.

## Superficial-analysis tails

Hollow present-participle endings that sound analytical but add nothing: "…highlighting their historical significance," "…underscoring the ongoing relevance," "…reflecting broader trends." The sentence gestures at insight it never delivers.

## Why it happens

RLHF rewards writing that sounds impressive and complete; inflation is the path of least resistance to that reward. See [[why-llms-write-this-way|why LLMs write this way]] and the [[sycophancy-and-positivity-register|positivity register]].

## Why it matters

Puffery is a high-confidence tell *in aggregate* because human expert writing is usually more restrained and specific. But note it also flags genuine promotional human writing (marketing, travel brochures, obituaries), so weight it with concreteness: does the text back the significance claim with a specific fact, or just assert it?
