# Liang et al. — LLM use across scientific papers

**Quality: 4/5.** Authoritative preprint from the same Stanford group; huge corpus; companion to the peer-reviews paper.

## Key findings

- Corpus: **950,965 papers**, Jan 2020–Feb 2024, across **arXiv, bioRxiv, and Nature-portfolio journals**. Same distributional-estimation method.
- **Field ranking of LLM-modified content:** **Computer Science highest at up to ~17.5%** of sentences; **Mathematics and Nature-portfolio lowest at ~6.3%**. Steady month-over-month rise after Nov 2022.
- **Correlates of higher usage:** authors who post preprints frequently; papers in **crowded / fast-moving research areas**; **shorter papers**.

## Why it matters for a reviewer

Establishes the phenomenon is field-general, not biomedical-only, and quantifies which disciplines are most saturated — useful as a prior when judging how likely any given technical piece is LLM-touched.
