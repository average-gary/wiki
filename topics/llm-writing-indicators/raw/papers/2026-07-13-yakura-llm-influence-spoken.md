# Yakura et al. — LLM influence on human spoken communication

**Quality: 5/5.** Max Planck primary study; econometric causal inference (synthetic control).

## Findings

- Corpus: **740,249 hours** of discourse — **360,445 YouTube academic talks + 771,591 podcast episodes** — before vs after ChatGPT's **Dec 2022** release.
- Core finding: "a measurable and abrupt increase in the use of words preferentially generated by ChatGPT" leaking into **spontaneous spoken** conversation within 18 months.
- GPT words tracked: **delve, comprehend, boast, swift, meticulous.**
- Thesis: "machines, originally trained on human data and subsequently exhibiting their own cultural traits, can, in turn, measurably reshape human culture." Warns of erosion of linguistic/cultural diversity.
- Companion reporting (ZME Science) quantified **annual increases of 25–50%** for GPT words in academic talks; steepest in science/education, **flat in Religion & Spirituality and Sports** — adoption is domain-dependent.

## Why it matters for a reviewer

The tell-words are now bleeding into human *speech* and writing, so their standalone diagnostic value is *declining* through 2025–26. A word like "delve" in a 2026 document is weaker evidence than it was in 2023 — humans now use it too.
