# Em-dash discourse — Rolling Stone + TechCrunch

**Quality: 5/5.** Two named-outlet sources bracketing the em-dash story.

## Rolling Stone — "ChatGPT Hyphen" (Miles Klee, 11 Apr 2025)

- Documents the **early-2025 origin** of the "em-dash = ChatGPT" meme. A viral LinkedIn post: "If you're leaving in these dashes, pause and check" — asserted with no evidence.
- A LuxeGen podcast host coined **"ChatGPT hyphen,"** citing a fashion rebrand flagged as AI for two em dashes.
- Generational/literacy angle: many humans don't know how to type an em dash ("I didn't even know there was a shortcut"), feeding the tell's plausibility.
- Debunk: ChatGPT itself says em dashes are "not a reliable sign"; OpenAI notes "some early AI-generated content (especially before 2023) used em dashes more frequently than the average human writer."
- Historical defense: Emily Dickinson and Nietzsche used em dashes expressively; Bryan Garner called it "perhaps the most underused punctuation mark in American writing."
- The *real* tells per RS: "the flat quality, the formulaic sentences, the absence of original ideas" — not punctuation.

## TechCrunch — "OpenAI says it's fixed ChatGPT's em dash problem" (Sarah Perez, 14 Nov 2025)

- **Sam Altman (X, Nov 2025):** "Small-but-happy win" — "If you tell ChatGPT not to use em-dashes in your custom instructions, it finally does what it's supposed to do!"
- The problem "stumped OpenAI for some time" — users previously **could not** stop the em dashes even when explicitly asking.
- The em dash became a telltale across "school papers, emails, comments, customer service chats, LinkedIn posts, online forums, ad copy."
- **Key nuance:** the fix works via **custom instructions / personalization**, NOT a default change or model-weights update. (Corroborated by Digital Trends, 15 Nov 2025, same quote.)

## Why it matters for a reviewer

The canonical case of a legitimate human habit read as an AI signature — **high false-positive risk**. Timeline: heavy em-dash use was genuine model default through most of 2025 (so its *presence* in mid-2025 text was mild evidence), but after ~Nov 2025 both presence and absence prove little (suppressible on demand; long a human habit). Treat em-dash density as a weak, contested, time-sensitive signal.
