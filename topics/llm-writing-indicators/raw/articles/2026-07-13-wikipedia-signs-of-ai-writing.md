# Wikipedia — Signs of AI writing (the master checklist)

**Quality: 5/5.** THE gold-mine catalog. Community-vetted, grounded in real Wikipedia examples, continuously updated. Self-described as "a list of writing and formatting conventions typical of AI chatbots such as ChatGPT, with real examples." Repeatedly cautions these are observable patterns, **not definitive proof** — it's the *clustering* of tells that signals AI.

## (A) High-density AI vocabulary words

Additionally (esp. sentence-start), align with, boasts (="has"), bolster/bolstered, crucial, delve, emphasizing, enduring, enhance, fostering, garner, highlight (as verb), interplay, intricate/intricacies, key, landscape (abstract), meticulous/meticulously, pivotal, robust, showcase, tapestry (abstract), testament, underscore (as verb), valuable, vibrant.

## (B) Puffery / promotional language

"boasts a", "vibrant", "rich", "profound", "enhancing", "showcasing", "exemplifies", "commitment to", "natural beauty", "nestled", "in the heart of", "Nestled in the heart of", "groundbreaking", "renowned", "diverse array". Real full-phrase examples: "rich cultural heritage", "stands as a vibrant town", "rich history, spiritual heritage, and natural beauty", "nestled within the breathtaking region", "enduring legacy", "pivotal moment in the evolution", "indelible mark".

## (C) Undue emphasis on significance / legacy / broader trends

"stands/serves as", "is a testament/reminder", "a vital/significant/crucial/pivotal/key role/moment", "underscores/highlights its importance", "reflects broader", "symbolizing its ongoing/enduring/lasting", "contributing to the", "setting the stage for", "marking/shaping the", "represents/marks a shift", "key turning point", "evolving landscape", "focal point", "indelible mark", "deeply rooted".

## (D) Editorializing phrases

"It's important to note", "It's worth noting", "It is essential to understand", "Importantly", "Notably".

## (E) Superficial analyses (hollow present-participle tails)

"highlighting", "underscoring", "emphasizing", "reflecting broader trends" (e.g. "highlighting their historical and pedagogical significance"). Sound analytical, lack substantive support.

## (F) Vague attributions / weasel words

"Industry reports suggest", "Observers have cited", "Experts argue", "Some critics argue", "several sources/publications", plus "such as" before over-long exhaustive lists.

## (G) Negative parallelism / "Not just X but Y"

"not only … but also", "It is not just …, it's …", "not just X, but Y". Real example: "is not a mirror but a portal".

## (H) Rule of three

Three items/adjectives strung together. Real examples: "tiles, metals, and plastics" / "drywall, plywood, and other construction materials" / "electrical outlets, switches, and plumbing fixtures".

## (I) Section-summary / heading formulas

AI-favored headings: "Challenges and Legacy", "Future Prospects", "Future Outlook", "Future Directions", "Challenges and Future Directions". Sentence template: "Despite its … faces several challenges …" / "Despite these challenges".

## (J) Avoidance of plain "is/are" (inflated copulas)

"serves as a", "marks the", "represents", "features" (for "has"), "maintains", "offers", "stands as". A measured **10%+ decline in "is/are"** post-2023 as models replace copulas.

## (K) Transition-word overuse

"Moreover", "Furthermore", "Consequently", "Nevertheless", "Additionally".

## (L) Formatting / markup red flags

Excessive boldface scattered through paragraphs; Title Case For All Headings (violates MoS); vertical lists with inline bold headers ("**Header**: description"); **skipped heading levels** (starting at level 3, skipping level 2); thematic-break horizontal rules before headings.

## (M) Markdown leakage (very high-confidence)

Chat markdown surviving into non-markdown contexts: `**bold**`, `*italic*`, `#` lists, emoji as bullets/heading prefixes; **broken reference/citation tokens**: `contentReference`, `oaicite`, `oai_citation`, `+1`, `attached_file`, `grok_card`, `turn0search0`, stray `:::` fences.

## (N) Typography tells

Em dashes — "LLM output uses them more often than nonprofessional human-written text," often "in a formulaic, pat way" and surrounded by spaces contrary to typographic norms. Curly/smart quotes ("…") instead of straight quotes — flagged as **weak** evidence (Word/macOS auto-convert).

## (O) Citation problems

Broken external links; invalid/mismatched DOI or ISBN; DOIs linking to unrelated articles; book citations without page numbers; `utm_source=` tracking params in refs; named references never used; **fabricated sources** (real cases in Russian and Hungarian); off-topic citations.

## (P) Chatbot conversational markers

"I hope this helps", "Of course!", "Certainly!", "You're absolutely right!", "Would you like…", "is there anything else", "let me know", "As of my last knowledge update", plus talk-page tells "I appreciate the feedback", "I want to clarify", "I have revised the article".

## Meta-heuristic (from WikiProject AI Cleanup parent page)

- **Temporal cutoff:** "Text that was present in an article before November 30, 2022 (the release of ChatGPT) is very unlikely to be AI-generated."
- Honest caveat: detection "is difficult in most cases since the generated text is often indistinguishable from human text."

## Why it matters for a reviewer

The practical "what to look for" inventory — exact phrases and markers by category, with real examples. Note the article's own warning: these are surface signs; fixing them shouldn't mask deeper problems (unsourced claims, fabricated citations), and the vocabulary set *drifts over time* so any tell list must be dated.
