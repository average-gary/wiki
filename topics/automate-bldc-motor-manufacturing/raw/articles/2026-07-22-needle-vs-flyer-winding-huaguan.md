# Needle Winding vs. Flyer Winding (Huaguan)

- **Needle winding** ("internal/pole winding"): a hollow needle/nozzle moves in synchronized XYZ + C-axis rotation to wrap wire around individual poles. Preferred for **concentrated windings on internal (inner-rotor) stators**. Achieves **65–75% slot fill** via orthocyclic (precise layered) winding. Wire gauge typically **0.01–0.45 mm**, up to **1.8 mm** heavy. Slower but precise; multi-station configs parallelize to cut cycle time.
- **Flyer winding**: a rotating arm orbits a stationary workpiece, "throwing" wire into slots via pulleys/hollow shaft. Much faster for external geometries but only **45–55% slot fill** (centrifugal forces + flyer-to-slot distance).
- **Ballooning effect**: above ~3,000 RPM flyer wire bows outward under centrifugal force; modern machines use high-speed servos synchronized with precise tensioning to compensate. Needle winding minimizes "unsupported wire length" by keeping the nozzle tip close to the winding point.
- **Application split**: needle → internal stators, concentrated windings, high-performance BLDC/EV auxiliary; flyer → external stators, armatures, transformer coils, power-tool armatures, mass-market consumer goods.
- Neither is objectively better — choice is driven by electromagnetic + economic constraints (speed vs. precision/density).

**Fill-factor ladder (consistent across sources):** flyer 45–55% < random round-wire baseline ~55% < needle/orthocyclic 65–75% < hairpin ~70%+.
