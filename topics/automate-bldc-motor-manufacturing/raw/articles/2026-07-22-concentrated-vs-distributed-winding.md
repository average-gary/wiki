# Concentrated vs. Distributed Winding (GR Winding)

- **Concentrated (coil per tooth)**: trapezoidal back-EMF, lower copper, more torque ripple/harmonics, localized heating, **simpler and easier to automate** → mass-production appliances/portables.
- **Distributed (coils across multiple slots)**: sinusoidal back-EMF, higher copper, smoother torque, lower harmonics, better heat distribution, more complex layout → EVs, industrial drives, robotics.
- **Fractional-slot concentrated windings (FSCW)** blend both for next-gen EVs.
- Takeaway: the winding topology chosen for the electromagnetic design *dictates* the machine type (needle/segmented-concentrated vs flyer/hairpin/insertion-distributed) and therefore how automatable the winding step is.
