# Automation ROI & Volume Thresholds

**ROI / payback (ynypm — figures illustrative, vendor blog):**
- **Median ROI 18–36 months** (cited across 120+ deployments 2021–2024).
- **Volume/payback crossover**: automotive powertrain **>150,000 units/yr → 18–24 mo**; energy equipment 200–800/yr → 22–28 mo; aerospace 50–300/yr → 30–36 mo.
- Break-even assumes **>85% uptime**; each shortfall adds 6–12 mo. **Digital twin / virtual commissioning cuts payback 20–35%** (4–6 wk vs 12–18 wk commissioning), cuts unplanned downtime 22–38%.
- 68% of delayed-ROI cases trace to pre-deployment planning (NC-programming misalignment, material-flow/AGV conflicts, legacy MES/SCADA handshake gaps).

**Flexible-vs-dedicated framework (Fastems):**
- **Mass production (100Ks–millions/yr, very low variability) → dedicated transfer-line automation**; **variable batch (1 to tens-of-thousands/yr, medium-high variability) → flexible/reconfigurable automation**; sporadic one-offs → case-by-case.
- Threshold principle: high enough per-workpiece volume → dedicated lines; otherwise high-mix-medium-volume → flexible automation.

**Manual-vs-automated takt (LinkedIn, illustrative):** manual ~45 s/part vs automated SPM ~18 s/part (~150% throughput gain); payback typically 9–18 mo depending on shift pattern/mix/utilization; "stability/repeatability matter more than peak speed."
