# Hairpin Winding Defects & Fill Factor (Patsnap)

- **Slot fill by method**: hairpin/bar-wound **0.60–0.80**, random round wire 0.35–0.45, Litz 0.35–0.40. Hairpin ≈ near-theoretical packing of rectangles vs air gaps between random circles.
- **Core hairpin tradeoff (why automation adds defects)**: AC losses from skin + proximity effects escalate in the **200–600 Hz** range in traction motors; PWM harmonics force non-uniform current in rectangular conductors, hurting high-speed efficiency.
- **Welding is the primary failure point**: a 60-slot, 4-layer stator needs **120+ weld joints**; any single porosity/cold weld → open circuit or high-resistance joint that fails the whole motor.
- **Defect modes**: insulation abrasion during bending / slot-insertion / end-winding twisting; inter-turn shorts. Detection via laser profilometry, resistance measurement, HV impulse, tan-δ, surge testing, PD analysis.
