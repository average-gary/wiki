# Impregnation / Varnishing Methods (Lamnow)

- **Dripping/trickle**: windings preheated 100–115°C and rotated; resin dripped at 60–70°C, drawn in by capillary action, then cured. Best for small motors and **BLDC** — low resin use, fast, automatable on a rotate-and-drip station.
- **VPI (vacuum pressure impregnation)**: stator sealed in chamber, vacuum removes air/moisture, then **200–700 kPa** pressure drives resin into all gaps — most thorough, eliminates air pockets; high-voltage/critical motors.
- **Pond/dip (immersion)**: static immersion, optionally 0.3–0.5 MPa pressure; small/medium BLDC, EV and PMSM.
- **Roll (roll-through)**: stator submerged (≥200 mm coverage), 3–5 rotations; medium/large induction.
- **Pouring & spray**: gravity pour or surface spray for medium/large machines with exposed windings.
- **Purpose (all methods)**: fill air gaps → raise thermal conductivity; bond turns (resist vibration/loosening); seal against moisture/corrosive gas; add dielectric strength.
- Trade-off ladder: trickle (fast/cheap/low-resin, small & BLDC) → dip → VPI (highest quality, slowest, most capital-intensive).
