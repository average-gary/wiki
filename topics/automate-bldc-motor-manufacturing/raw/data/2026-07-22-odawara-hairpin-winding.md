# Hairpin Winding (Odawara Automation)

**Full ordered hairpin process:**
1. Core supply
2. Insulation paper insertion (formed to U/S/B shape per slot)
3. Coil forming of rectangular conductors (up to **6.5 × 4.5 mm**)
4. Enamel removal (mechanical or laser)
5. Coils aligned to winding pattern and inserted simultaneously
6. Coil twist (proprietary; minimizes end-turn height)
7. Coil-end welding (laser or TIG)
8. Bus-bar welding
9. Electrical test

- **Machine envelope**: stator OD **160–300 mm**, ID **100–250 mm**, height **35–200 mm**, **2–10 layers**.
- Flat hairpins pack more copper per slot → higher slot fill, better thermal dissipation vs round wire.
- Available fully automated or semi-automated modular/scalable lines.

**Companion (Odawara BLDC systems page):** Needle vs segment winding tradeoff — Needle: shorter winding time, lower cost, simpler termination + final assembly, but lower fill factor, slot-opening-critical. Segment: higher fill factor / more power in compact footprint, but longer winding, more complicated interpole/termination/final assembly.
