# High-Speed Rotor Magnet Retention (Mainrich)

- **Sintered NdFeB is mechanically asymmetric + brittle**: ~1000 MPa compressive but only **75–80 MPa tensile**, 250–290 MPa flexural — why magnets chip and why handling/insertion tooling must avoid tensile/peel loads.
- **Structural epoxies**: ~**17–20 MPa shear** at elevated temp (to ~20 MPa at 180°C, continuous service 200–220°C). Adhesive is strong in shear, weak in radial peel/tension → bonding alone only suits **tip speeds below ~100 m/s**.
- **Retention hierarchy by surface velocity**: bonded <100 m/s; metal sleeves (Inconel 718, Ti-6Al-4V, stainless) 150–250 m/s; **CFRP sleeves up to ~360 m/s**. Hoop stress scales with speed² and radius².
- **Grade/temperature**: N42SH/N45SH (~150°C), N42UH/N45UH (~180°C), N35EH/N42EH (~200°C).
- **Failure mode**: differing CTE between sleeve, magnet, rotor steel means cold bench tests can pass while service (max speed + max temp) fails.
