# Positioning and differentiation

His Words can win against a saturated faith-blocker market only if it is honest about three claims:

1. **The interruption rhythm is uncombined.** No competitor ships duration-of-use interrupts.
2. **The autonomy-supportive frame is uncombined.** No competitor combines mid-session prompts with grace-aligned, dismissable, ally-framed UX.
3. **The "no AI" credibility moat is uncombined.** No competitor ships scripture-only with zero AI-generated prayer / chat content.

Each individual claim is partially occupied. The *combination* is unoccupied. If His Words ships all three together, it is genuinely categorically distinct — not from one competitor, but from the union of all of them.

## Differentiator 1: interruption rhythm vs. verse gate

See [[wiki/concepts/interruption-rhythm|interruption rhythm]] and [[wiki/concepts/verse-gate-pattern|verse-gate pattern]] for full treatments.

| Competitor | Mechanism | Reset | Mid-session interrupt? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Psalmo | Verse on launch | Daily | No |
| Prayer Lock | Pray on launch | Per-session | No |
| Bible Mode | Devotional on launch | Per-session | No |
| BibleScroll | YouVersion read | Daily | No |
| Bible Focus | Coin economy | Earned | No |
| FaithLock × 6 | Pray-to-unlock | Per-session | No |
| One Sec (secular) | 6s pause | Per-launch | No |
| Opal (secular) | Limit + reminder | Scheduled | No |
| Holy Focus | Bible during prayer time | Schedule | No |

Every reviewed app — secular and faith — uses gate-on-launch or schedule-block. None recur **inside an active session**. This is the categorical gap.

## Differentiator 2: autonomy-supportive interrupt vs. coercive gate

The HCI literature ([[../raw/papers/2026-06-23-psych-locknType-vs-lukoff-blocking-vs-nudge|Lukoff 2022]], [[../raw/papers/2026-06-23-psych-haliburton-design-frictions-chi2024|Haliburton 2024]]) is clear: dismissable, mid-session, ally-framed prompts sustain adherence; hard blocks produce reactance and uninstalls.

The faith-blocker market is split:

- **Psalmo, Prayer Lock, FaithLock variants**: gate, must read/pray to unlock, no dismissal. Coercive.
- **Bible Mode (Strict Mode)**: prevents skipping. Coercive.
- **Bible Focus**: must earn coins. Coercive AND transactional.
- **One Sec, Opal (secular)**: dismissable. Autonomy-supportive but generic content.

His Words combines mid-session timing with [[wiki/concepts/mandatory-reflection-window|6s mandatory then dismissable]] structure — autonomy-supportive Scripture content. This combination is genuinely novel.

## Differentiator 3: scripture only, no AI generation

Per [[../raw/articles/2026-06-23-competitors-prayer-lock|Prayer Lock]], [[../raw/articles/2026-06-23-competitors-bible-mode|Bible Mode]], [[../raw/articles/2026-06-23-competitors-faithlock-variants|FaithLock variants]], and [[../raw/articles/2026-06-23-competitors-hallow-and-adjacents|Bible Chat / Creed]]: every meaningful competitor ships some form of AI-generated content — AI prayers, AI chat companions, AI mood-matched devotionals.

His Words ships **canonical scripture text only**. No LLM-generated prayers. No AI Bible chatbot. The verse the user reads at minute 5 is exactly what the translators committed to print, no model interpolation.

This is positioning as **credibility moat** for the segment of the market — Reformed, confessional, theologically conservative — that distrusts LLM-generated religious content. It is also marketing differentiator for the broader Christian audience that values authenticity over personalization.

See [[wiki/decisions/2026-06-24-no-ai-generated-content|decision: no AI-generated content]] for the full rationale.

## Where the contrarian objections land

Three steelman objections are addressed seriously in [[wiki/topics/contrarian-objections-and-responses|contrarian-objections-and-responses]]. Brief preview:

- **Crouch / Newport / Reinke** ([[../raw/articles/2026-06-23-contrarian-phone-is-the-problem|phone-is-the-problem]]): the phone itself is the problem; adding scripture to phone time baptizes the device. **Honest response**: His Words is a harm-reduction tool for a population that is going to use phones regardless. It is not a substitute for [[wiki/concepts/family-covenant-mode|covenant-style boundaries]], phone-free spaces, or paper-Bible discipline; it is a layer for the time the user is on the phone anyway.
- **Haidt** ([[../raw/articles/2026-06-23-contrarian-haidt-phone-free-schools|phone-free schools]]): teen interventions need *phone-free* spaces, not better phones. **Honest response**: His Words is adult-target by default. It does not pretend to substitute for parental boundary-setting per Haidt's prescription.
- **YouVersion already won** ([[../raw/articles/2026-06-23-contrarian-youversion-already-won|here]]): YouVersion is free, ubiquitous, has reading plans. **Honest response**: YouVersion is the *Bible-reading* app and has won that category. His Words is not a Bible-reading app; it is a *digital wellness app that uses scripture as the wellness content*. Hallow's $52M raise + #1 App Store ranking ([[../raw/articles/2026-06-23-market-hallow-funding-and-growth|here]]) proves a paid premium category exists *next to* free YouVersion. They are not substitutes.

## What positioning copy actually says

Three voices to test:

- **"Transform scrolling into moments with God."** (current brief positioning, pro-social, App Store-friendly per [[../raw/articles/2026-06-23-ios-app-store-approval-precedent|approval precedent]]).
- **"Your scrolling, with scripture between."** (less moral, more descriptive — appeals to non-evangelical Christians).
- **"A pause for the Word, every five minutes."** (most concrete; foregrounds the [[wiki/concepts/interruption-rhythm|rhythm]] explicitly).

Avoid: "block addictive apps" (surveillance framing — Apple-approval risk per [[../raw/articles/2026-06-23-ios-app-store-approval-precedent|here]]); "fight phone addiction" (Apple liability flag); "lock distractions" (FaithLock-saturated phrase).

## What "won" looks like at 12 / 24 months

12-month signal of category ownership:

- Top-3 ranking in App Store search for "Bible verse" + "screen time" combined queries.
- 50k+ MAU with 40%+ 30-day retention (vs. ~30% One Sec benchmark).
- ~500-1,000 paying subscribers at $50-70/yr.
- Recognition by Christian press as "the one that doesn't use AI."

24-month signal:

- Hallow or YouVersion considers shipping a copy feature (the strongest possible signal that the niche is real).
- Church-partnership distribution running (10+ congregations actively recommending).
- Competitor saturation has commoditized the verse-gate pattern; His Words is the only credible mid-session interrupt with autonomy-supportive frame.
