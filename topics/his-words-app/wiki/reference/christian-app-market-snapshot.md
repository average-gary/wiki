## Five distinct monetization shapes

1. **Donor-funded utility** (YouVersion). Requires patron-class capital ($10M+/yr ongoing). Not a startup path.
2. **Premium subscription** (Hallow, Glorify, Pray.com). $50-100/yr ARPU. Requires venture capital for marketing burn.
3. **Freemium + commerce** (Bible Gateway). Subscription is loss-leader for book sales. Requires publishing partnership.
4. **Per-resource e-commerce** (Olive Tree). Best for power-users; capped LTV.
5. **Church-partnership distribution** (Life.Church / YouVersion roots). Distribution through pulpits — low CAC, low brand control.

His Words [[wiki/topics/monetization-and-pricing|monetization plan]] is shape 2 with shape 5 as the distribution channel — explicitly avoiding shape 2's typical celebrity-burn pattern.

## 8-figure ARR path

Per [[../raw/articles/2026-06-23-market-sizing-and-monetization-patterns|sizing]]:

- **$10M ARR target**: 143k paid subs at $70 ARPU. At 3% free→paid conversion = ~4.8M total installs. Hallow hit similar in ~5 years with $52M of capital.
- **$50M ARR stretch**: 715k paid subs (current Hallow scale). ~24M installs at 3% conversion. Requires Super-Bowl-scale brand spend or church-partnership distribution at scale.

**Capital efficiency lever**: if His Words avoids celebrity-endorsement burn, it could hit $10M ARR on **$5-10M raised** vs. Hallow's $52M.

## Recent failures / sunset risk

- **Gospel Communications International** (Bible Gateway's predecessor) — failed on donation-only model, sold to Zondervan 2008.
- **Pray.com Series B trajectory** — uncertain. No publicized Series B/C since 2018 despite continued partnerships.
- **Echo Prayer, Sermon.ly** (mentioned in original brief) — not verifiable in research; likely sunset.

General pattern: faith-app sunsets are under-reported.

## Investor appetite signals (2024-2026)

- **a16z** (via Glorify $40M) — top-tier secular validation.
- **TPG Growth + Greylock + Spark + Science Inc.** (Pray.com) — broad VC participation.
- **Sovereign's Capital, Crossway Ventures, Faith Driven Investor** — explicitly faith-aligned funds; portfolios largely opaque.
- **Hobby Lobby's Green family** — patron-class faith capital, not VC.
- **Pray.com CEO publicly cites VC bias against faith-tech** — meaning faith-aligned funds are the path of least resistance for early rounds.

## Watchlist (could disrupt the category overnight)

- **YouVersion** could ship a blocker feature. They have not — but they could. With 1B installs and Hobby Lobby money, they would commoditize the category in a quarter.
- **Hallow** could ship "Scripture interrupt" as an add-on to their audio meditation. With $52M raised + #1 App Store presence, they could outspend a small operator on awareness alone.
- **Bible Chat / Creed** could pivot from AI-companion to AI-companion-with-blocker. Less likely; their model is engagement, not friction.

The fact that none of these incumbents has shipped a blocker yet is the strategic opening. Whether it is opportunity (incumbents are blind) or warning (incumbents have considered and rejected the mechanism for theological / UX reasons) is the bet.
