# Covenant Eyes — Christian Accountability Incumbent

## Company Snapshot

- Founded by Ron DeHaas; ~25 years in market.
- "Over 1.7 million people we've helped"; 71K+ app reviews at 4.5 stars.
- 2008 revenue ~$4M from ~56K subscriptions (Wikipedia / older filings); much larger today.
- Marketing claim: "75% of Christian men view pornography" — used as TAM hook.

## Product Surface (rebranded as "Victory by Covenant Eyes")

**Core mechanic — Screen Accountability:**
- Continuously captures screenshots of device screens.
- Screenshots are "blurred, distorted, and encrypted with AES 256-bit security, then deleted after 30 days."
- AI flags suspicious content; an **ally** (accountability partner) receives reports.
- Also includes domain blocking with custom allow/block lists, real-time notifications, and educational courses.

**Platforms:** iOS 15.1+, macOS 11+, Android 11+, Windows 10+. Unlimited devices per user; up to 10 individual users per family plan.

## Pricing (2026)

- Monthly: **$18/month**
- Yearly: **$198/year** (saves 10%)
- Lifetime: **$950 one-time** (saves 50% over 10 years)
- 30-day free trial.

Pricing is single-tier — no individual/family split, no à la carte feature gating.

## Distribution Channels

1. **DTC** — primary channel via web, app stores, podcast/sermon ads.
2. **Free programs as funnels:** Strive (21-day men's program), Arise (21-day women's program). Both are free hooks that upsell to paid.
3. **Church program ("Five Stones"):**
   - Targets "5 key areas" — men, women, couples, families, leadership.
   - Distributes via free downloadable brochures + book-a-meeting sales motion (Karen Potter listed as church-team contact).
   - Pastors can request "The Healing Church" book free.
   - **Pricing for congregations is not public** — gated behind sales calls. This is enterprise/B2C2C motion: pastor evangelizes, congregation members buy individually.

## The 2022 Controversy (Wired / Slate / Tech Policy Press)

What was reported:
- Multiple outlets in Feb–March 2022 reported that Covenant Eyes (and Accountable2You) were being used by churches and elder boards to **surveil pastors and staff** — not just parishioners voluntarily.
- The software's screenshots captured **legal but private** behavior: searches about LGBTQ identity, mental health questions, dating-site visits, even reading articles critical of the church. These were forwarded to "accountability partners" who in some cases were the pastor's employer.
- **Google removed both apps from Play Store** in early 2022, citing "accessibility functionalities for surveillance purposes in a manner prohibited by Google." Covenant Eyes was later reinstated after compliance changes.
- Specific incidents (per the reporting): pastors disciplined or forced to resign over searches that were not crimes and not necessarily even sins by the church's own doctrine.

Key design line the controversy exposes:
- **"Accountability" tools are surveillance tools** when the watcher is also the employer or has power over the watched.
- The blurred/distorted screenshot is not a privacy guarantee — the AI flag plus the social-pressure interpretation by the ally is what causes harm.
- 30-day retention is still 30 days of forwarded reports.

## Design Lessons for His Words

1. **Symmetry matters.** Covenant Eyes' model is asymmetric: one party is monitored, one party views. His Words should consider whether any "family mode" creates a power asymmetry that can be abused (parent–teen is one thing; elder board–pastor is another).
2. **No screenshots.** Screenshot capture is the feature that drove the 2022 controversy and the Play Store removal. It's also the feature that makes the product "real" to evangelical buyers. Tradeoff is sharp: don't capture screens, don't compete on this dimension.
3. **Pricing ceiling exists.** Covenant Eyes at $18/mo is the high-end anchor. Ever Accountable at $14.99 and Accountable2You at $11 are the competitive floor.
4. **Church distribution works but is sales-heavy.** Five Stones is gated behind book-a-meeting; this is high-touch enterprise sales. Not viable for a v1 indie launch.
5. **Reputational risk is real and ongoing.** Any product positioned as "accountability" inherits the Covenant Eyes shadow. Brand carefully.
