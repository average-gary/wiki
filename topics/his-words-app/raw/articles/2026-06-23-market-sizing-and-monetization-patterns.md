# Market sizing & monetization patterns

## US Christian smartphone TAM (2024)

From Pew RLS 2023-24 and 2024 Gallup:
- US population: ~340M.
- Identify as Christian: **~69%** = **235M people**.
- US smartphone penetration: ~85% of adults; ~80% across all ages including kids.
- **US Christian smartphone TAM ≈ 188M people** (235M × 80%).

**Note**: Hallow's Catholic-only positioning addresses a 75M-person sub-TAM and still hit #1 on the App Store. A non-denominational/Protestant position addresses 150M+.

## Monetization patterns — five distinct models

1. **Donor-funded utility** (YouVersion). Requires patron-class capital ($10M+/yr ongoing). Not a startup path.
2. **Premium subscription** (Hallow, Glorify, Pray.com). $50-100/yr ARPU. Requires venture capital for marketing burn.
3. **Freemium + commerce** (Bible Gateway). Subscription is loss-leader for book sales. Requires publishing partnership.
4. **Per-resource e-commerce** (Olive Tree). Best for power-users but capped LTV.
5. **Church-partnership distribution** (Life.Church / YouVersion roots). Distribution through pulpits — low CAC, low brand control.

## ARPU benchmarks
- Hallow: ~$70/yr.
- Calm/Headspace (secular comp): ~$70/yr.
- Pray.com: not disclosed but inferred ~$50-70.
- **The "Christian premium" appears to be flat at $70/yr** — same as secular wellness, not a discount.

## Conversion benchmarks
- Industry standard subscription apps: 2-5% free→paid conversion.
- Hallow inferred: ~36% if 730K paid / 2M downloads, but 2M is 2-year-old number — actual conversion likely 5-10% on current installs.

## What an "8-figure" path looks like for His Words

**Conservative target: $10M ARR.**
- At $70/yr ARPU = **143K paid subscribers**.
- At 3% free→paid conversion = **~4.8M total installs**.
- Hallow hit similar in ~5 years with $52M of capital.

**Stretch: $50M ARR.**
- 715K paid subscribers (current Hallow scale).
- ~24M installs at 3% conversion.
- Requires Super-Bowl-scale brand spend or church-partnership distribution.

### Capital efficiency lever
- If "His Words" can avoid the celebrity-endorsement burn (Hallow's biggest expense category by inference), it could hit $10M ARR on $5-10M raised vs. Hallow's $52M.
- Distribution alternatives: church partnerships (free), Christian podcaster ad-buys (cheaper than Wahlberg), organic social via Catholic/Protestant influencer micro-network.

## Recent failures / sunset risk
- **Gospel Communications International** (Bible Gateway's predecessor) — failed on donation-only model, sold to Zondervan 2008.
- **Echo Prayer, Sermon.ly** (mentioned in brief) — not verifiable in this research session; needs follow-up.
- **General pattern**: faith-app sunsets are under-reported; Christianity Today rarely covers product deaths.

## Investor appetite signals (2024-2026)
- **a16z** (via Glorify $40M) — top-tier secular validation.
- **TPG Growth + Greylock + Spark + Science Inc.** (Pray.com) — broad VC participation.
- **Sovereign's Capital, Crossway Ventures, Faith Driven Investor** — explicitly faith-aligned funds; portfolios largely opaque from public web.
- **Hobby Lobby's Green family** — patron-class faith capital, not VC.
- Pray.com CEO publicly cites VC bias against faith-tech as ongoing challenge — meaning faith-aligned funds are the path of least resistance for "His Words" early rounds.
