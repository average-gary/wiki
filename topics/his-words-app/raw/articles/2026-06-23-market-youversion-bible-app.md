# YouVersion — the elephant in the Christian-app room

## Ownership & history
- Founded **2007** as a website by **Bobby Gruenewald** under **Life.Church** (Edmond, Oklahoma; multi-site evangelical megachurch).
- First mobile app **2008** — among the first 200 apps in the Apple App Store.
- Sponsored since at least **2012** by **David Green** (founder of Hobby Lobby; estimated net worth ~$13B). Green's funding effectively makes YouVersion a donor-subsidized utility.

## Install milestones (last published)
| Year | Installs | Source |
|---|---|---|
| April 2017 | 268M+ | Wikipedia |
| November 2021 | 500M | Wikipedia |
| 2025 | **~1 billion** installs | Wikipedia |

This is one of the most-installed apps of any category, period.

## Content scale
- Bible content in **2,243 languages** (interface in 65+ languages).
- 3,000+ Bible versions; 800+ devotional reading plans.
- Audio Bibles in many languages.

## Revenue / monetization model
- **Donor-funded.** No paid subscription, no advertising, no in-app purchases.
- Effectively a non-profit ministry of Life.Church + Hobby Lobby family wealth.
- This is critical for competitor strategy: **YouVersion does not need to monetize**. Any for-profit Bible app cannot compete on price (free) or scale (1B installs, 2,200+ languages).

## Why no head-on competitor has emerged
1. Donor-subsidized free competitor with 17-year head start and category lock-in.
2. 800+ reading plans = enormous content moat.
3. The Bible itself is public domain (KJV, ASV, WEB) but **modern translations (NIV, ESV, NLT, CSB) are licensed**. YouVersion has secured licenses for 3,000+ versions — effectively unreplicable for a startup.
4. Habit / streak mechanics are deeply entrenched.

## Strategic takeaway for "His Words"
- **Do NOT position as a Bible-reading app.** YouVersion has won that battle.
- Adjacent verticals (digital wellness, prayer, scripture-meditation, Lectio Divina, Sabbath, screen-time replacement) are open.
- Hallow's success ($52M raised, #1 App Store) proves a paid premium category exists *next to* free YouVersion — they are not substitutes.
- The opportunity: be the "Calm/Headspace" of Christian habit formation, not the "Kindle" of Bible.

## Translation licensing as moat
- NIV → Zondervan/HarperCollins (gatekeeper for the most popular modern English translation).
- ESV → Crossway (gatekeeper for Reformed/evangelical premium translation).
- Any new app needs translation licensing deals. Public-domain-only positioning is viable but limits demographic reach.
