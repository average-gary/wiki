## Mechanism

**Earn-to-unlock economy.** Per description:

> "BibleFocus rewards time spent praying, meditating, studying scripture, and attending church — unlocking apps that would otherwise drain your day. The app locks distracting applications by default; only faith-building activities earn coins to unlock screen time."

Activities that mint coins:
- Guided prayer timers.
- Meditation timers.
- Bible study quizzes (4 difficulty levels).
- Scripture scanning (likely OCR — scan a paper Bible or printed devotional).
- Church check-ins (geofenced or self-reported).
- Daily verse reflections.

Coins → minutes of screen time on locked apps. "Light therapy meditation modes" suggests Calm/Headspace cosmetic borrowing.

## Notes

- **Coin economy is theologically contested.** Reformed / confessional Protestants frame prayer and church attendance as *worship*, not *transaction* — making them the price you pay for Instagram time inverts the relationship. His Words could differentiate by **never framing scripture as currency** — scripture is the content, not the transaction.
- **Church check-in is a fascinating idea but legally fraught** — false attendance claims, denominational disputes, geofence accuracy. Worth observing whether Rewired sustains it.
- **Scripture scanning is a real innovation** — His Words could absorb the "physical Bible bridge" idea (e.g., camera-scan a verse to dismiss the interrupt — proves you opened a real Bible).
- Rewired LLC operator pattern looks like a solo dev or 2-3 person shop. Slowing update cadence is a signal of either profitability (no need to ship) or decline.
