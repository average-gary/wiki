## Mechanism

**Daily-blocker with YouVersion handoff.** Per description:

> "BibleScroll blocks your most distracting apps, every day, until you spend time reading the Bible. The app connected directly with the YouVersion Bible app so you can read scripture in your favorite digital way."

Daily friction model — apps unlock once per day after a YouVersion reading session completes. Mechanism for verifying "reading completion" is opaque (likely time-based: N minutes elapsed in YouVersion).

## Pricing

- **Subscription-required after 7-day free trial.** Quote: "Subscription purchase is required to continue to use BibleScroll after our 7-day free trial."
- Specific tier amounts not in iTunes metadata.
- Free trial duration explicit; uncommon among iOS apps this category typically just paywall on launch.

iOS. Android status not stated; Screen Detox Inc not visible on Google Play search.

## Verse source

- **Defers to YouVersion entirely.** Does not bundle its own Bible. User reads in YouVersion (3,500+ translations, 2,300+ languages, audio) — best-in-class Bible reader experience.
- This is a **strategic delegation**: solves translation licensing forever, but creates a dependency on YouVersion app being installed.

## Notable features

- YouVersion deep linking / app-to-app handoff.
- Daily reset cadence (vs. recurring interrupt).
- Minimal feature set ("Read the Bible, before you scroll" — single value prop).

## Notes

- **YouVersion integration is both strength and weakness.** Strength: free best-in-class Bible. Weakness: requires YouVersion installed (most Christians have it, but adds setup friction); no offline behavior if YouVersion data not synced.
- **Daily cadence is shallow.** Same as Psalmo / Bible Mode — one read per day unlocks unlimited subsequent access. His Words' recurring N-minute model is materially harder.
- **Screen Detox Inc** suggests broader screen-time portfolio (likely secular blockers too) — operator playbook is "release a Christian fork of an existing blocker mechanism."
- Verification mechanism (how do they prove the user actually read?) is unclear; likely time elapsed in YouVersion. This is gameable — user can leave YouVersion open in background. His Words could differentiate by *displaying scripture inside its own UI* with active-engagement check (scroll through full passage, no skip).
