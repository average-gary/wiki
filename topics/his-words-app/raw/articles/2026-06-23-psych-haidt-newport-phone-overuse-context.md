# Macro-context: Haidt's *Anxious Generation* and Newport's *Digital Minimalism*

## Citations
- Haidt, J. (2024). *The Anxious Generation: How the Great Rewiring of Childhood Is Causing an Epidemic of Mental Illness.* Penguin Press.
- Newport, C. (2019). *Digital Minimalism: Choosing a Focused Life in a Noisy World.* Portfolio.
- Twenge, J. M., & Haidt, J. (2020-2024). Various papers in *Journal of Adolescence*, *Journal of Social and Personal Relationships*, *PNAS*, on smartphone use and adolescent mental health.
- Companion site: https://www.afterbabel.com/ — Haidt + Rausch ongoing analyses.

## Core claims relevant to His Words

### Haidt — *The Anxious Generation* (2024)
- **The Great Rewiring (2010-2015)**: smartphone + front-facing camera + algorithmic feeds + always-on-notifications produced a step-change in adolescent (especially girl) mental-health metrics. Anxiety, depression, self-harm, suicide all show inflection points 2012-2014.
- **Population-level effect.** Not a fringe-clinical issue. ~95% of US teens have a smartphone; ~40-60% of waking hours are phone-mediated.
- **Four norm proposals**: (1) no smartphones before high school, (2) no social media before 16, (3) phone-free schools, (4) more independence + free play.
- **Mechanism claims**: phenomenological replacement of embodied life; chronic interruption preventing flow/sleep; social-comparison + algorithmic amplification.

### Newport — *Digital Minimalism* (2019)
- **Digital declutter**: 30-day removal of all optional digital tech, then deliberate reintroduction with explicit purpose-fit checks.
- Argues against "moderation" — it doesn't work because the apps are *engineered* to defeat moderate use. Argues for either deliberate minimalism or wholesale removal.
- **Solitude deprivation**: humans need silent unmediated time; smartphones systematically prevent it.
- **High-quality leisure** (active, embodied, demanding) substitutes for the dopamine pull of low-quality digital leisure.

### Twenge & Haidt papers (peer-reviewed companions)
- Multiple papers showing dose-response between hours of social media use and depression/anxiety symptoms in teens, especially girls.
- Effect sizes are small per-individual but population-impactful given the scale.

## Implications for His Words' design

The Haidt/Newport thesis is **the audience-level justification** for an app like His Words — there is a real, large, non-clinical population that wants to reduce phone use without going fully phone-free. They are:
1. **Goal-committed already** (which is critical for implementation intentions to work — see Gollwitzer).
2. **Skeptical of more apps as the solution** (Newport's critique). The intervention must be *minimal and substitutive*, not additive.
3. **Reactant against moralizing or external control** (especially adolescents). Faith framing only works for the user's *own* faith — coerced spiritual content backfires.

### Tension with His Words specifically
- **Newport would argue** that adding *another* app to "fix" your phone problem is the wrong move; the right move is removing the problem apps. His Words sidesteps this by being *non-additive in attention surface* — it shows a verse only when the user is already in the problem app.
- **Haidt would endorse** the substitution model (a verse moment is a meaningful pause), but would push for the more radical "no social before 16" norm in parallel. His Words is best framed as a tool for adults and self-selecting teens, not as a substitute for boundaries.

## Design recommendations transferable
1. **Position as a substitution, not an addition.** The pitch is "your existing scrolling, with moments of meaning inserted" — not "yet another wellness app."
2. **Don't moralize.** Haidt's data and Newport's prescription both depend on user agency. Coercive or guilt-driven framing produces the reactance documented in Lukoff 2022.
3. **Match scale of intervention to scale of problem.** A pause every N minutes is a small, sustainable intervention for a chronic, large-surface problem. Hard blocks are scale-mismatched.
4. **Pair with off-app behavior.** Encourage users to use the redeemed minutes for offline activity (Newport's "high-quality leisure").
5. **Adult-target by default.** For minors, the more important intervention is parental boundary-setting, per Haidt; His Words should not pretend to substitute for that.

## Evidence rating
**Moderate-to-strong (context, not direct intervention evidence).** These are trade-nonfiction works backed by peer-reviewed companion research. They establish the *problem space* and audience case for a soft-interruption faith app, not the specific design parameters (which come from the CHI/PNAS papers).
