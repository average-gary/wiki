## Mechanism

**Multi-modal blocker.** Per description, combines:
- **Screen time blocker** (apps locked until devotional/verse complete).
- **Quiet Time Mode** (scheduled scripture-focused blocks).
- **Rotating Daily Prayer Widgets** (ambient, not blocking).
- **Bible Chat** (AI companion separate from blocker).
- **Strict Mode for devotionals** — release notes 2026-06-03: "prevents rushing through content." Likely time-gated reading enforcement (cannot tap "done" until N seconds elapsed on a passage).

Less mechanically pure than Psalmo or Prayer Lock — it's a kitchen-sink "faith dashboard" with blocking as one of many features.

## Pricing

- Free download. Subscription details not in iTunes metadata, but description's premium-feel widget customization + Bible Chat AI strongly imply a subscription tier (typical $4-8/mo for this category).
- Friday Labs LLC is the developer — venture-backed pattern (LLC suggests bootstrapped or pre-seed).

## Platform

iOS. Android status unconfirmed.

## Verse source

- **Multiple translations**: explicitly KJV and ESV called out. ESV requires a Crossway license, suggesting Friday Labs has paid for translation rights — material differentiation from Psalmo's public-domain-only stance.
- "Curated verses and devotionals" suggests editorial team or content licensing partnership.

## Notable features

- **Strict Mode** (2026-06 launch) — blocks rushing through devotionals. This is a meaningful UX innovation if executed well; matches the *intent* of His Words' recurring interrupt.
- **Bible Chat AI** — competing directly with Creed, Bible Chat (Book Vitals), and AI-prayer features in Prayer Lock.
- Lock-screen + home-screen widgets.
- Mindful screen time tracking dashboard.

## User reception

- **10,486 ratings, 4.93 stars** as of 2026-06-23 — second-tier scale (vs. Prayer Lock's 30k) but very high quality signal.
- Released 2025-05-02, current version 2.2.8 — ~13 months in market, steady development.
- Recent improvements: saved verses dashboard, Strict Mode, bug fixes.

## Notes

- **Strict Mode is a direct competitor to His Words' core thesis** — both bet that *time spent on scripture* matters more than *acknowledgment of scripture*. His Words goes further by making it *recurring*.
- ESV licensing is a real moat — Crossway is selective about app licensees. His Words must either pay for ESV/NIV or commit to public-domain translations + signal that explicitly to users who care.
- **Kitchen-sink positioning is a weakness** — blocker, AI chat, widgets, devotionals, screen-time tracker. His Words can win by being *focused*: one mechanism, executed perfectly, no AI, no chat.
- Friday Labs is a real dev shop (LLC, multiple updates per month). Treat as a credible competitor.
