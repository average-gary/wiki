## Hallow: Prayer & Meditation

- **367,794 ratings, 4.89 stars** — by far the largest faith-app on iOS after YouVersion Bible.
- Developer: Hallow, Inc. (Chicago, well-funded — raised $50M+ Series B in 2024 per public reporting).
- Mechanism: **audio-guided meditation, prayer, and Catholic Bible readings.** 10,000+ sessions. Subscription-driven (~$70-100/yr).
- **No app-blocking / screen-time-interrupt feature visible** in current App Store description (v13.5.0, 2026-06-08).
- Pricing: free tier with limited content + Hallow Plus subscription.
- Heavily Catholic in content but explicitly markets to all Christians.

## Why Hallow does not (yet) compete on the blocker axis

Hallow's product strategy is **content depth, not friction injection.** They are building the Christian Calm/Headspace — guided audio, celebrity narrators (Mark Wahlberg, Jonathan Roumie of *The Chosen*), liturgical calendar integration. Adding an iOS Screen Time API integration is plausible for them but not strategically core; they monetize on listening time, not on intercepting other apps.

**Risk to His Words**: if Hallow ships even a half-baked "scripture interrupt" feature, they have brand + distribution to commoditize the category overnight. Watch for it.

## Adjacent: Creed (Lemon Tree Labs)

- **30,321 ratings, 4.88 stars.**
- AI Bible chat companion with personalized prayers, devotionals, meditation experiences.
- "Private safe space for sharing spiritual concerns" — explicit therapy-adjacent positioning.
- Not a blocker. Competes with Bible Mode's Bible Chat and similar AI-companion apps.

## Adjacent: Dwell Audio Bible

- **83,215 ratings, 4.89 stars.**
- 20+ professional narrations, 14 translations, devotionals, kids content.
- Subscription-based audio Bible. Not a blocker.

## Adjacent: Bible Chat (Book Vitals)

- **343,754 ratings, 4.92 stars.**
- AI Bible companion + reading plans + prayer + community. Massive scale.
- Not a blocker, but the AI-prayer feature competes with Prayer Lock / FaithLock variants.

## Notable absences from blocker category

- **YouVersion (Bible by Life.Church)**: 13.4M ratings, the elephant in the room. Has reading plans, Verse of the Day, and "Streaks" — but **no app-blocker feature**. They could ship one tomorrow and dominate.
- **Hallow**: see above. Could ship one tomorrow.
- **Bible Chat / Creed**: AI-first, not friction-first.

## Notes

- The blocker category is **only attacked by indies**. No major faith-app brand has shipped one. This is either:
  - **Opportunity**: incumbents are blind to friction-injection because their KPI is engagement-with-our-app.
  - **Warning**: incumbents have considered and rejected the mechanism for theological / UX / business reasons (e.g., they think it cheapens scripture, or they don't want to be associated with restriction).
- **Hallow's silence on screen-time is the most strategically important data point**. If they thought it was a winner, they'd ship it. Their bet is *positive engagement with prayer audio*, not *negative friction on Instagram*.
- His Words must believe the *negative friction* thesis is correct AND defensible against a YouVersion / Hallow late entry. The defensibility comes from execution depth on the recurring interrupt model — something the indie pack hasn't built yet.
