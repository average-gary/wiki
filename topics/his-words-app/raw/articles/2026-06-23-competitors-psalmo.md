## Mechanism

**Verse-overlay before access.** On iOS, Psalmo uses Apple's Screen Time API to intercept launches of selected apps (Instagram, TikTok, X, YouTube, Snapchat, etc.) and displays today's Bible verse. **Once read, the app unlocks for the remainder of the day, resetting at midnight.** On Android, it uses the Accessibility service to detect app launches and trigger the same overlay.

This is closer to a *one-time daily verse gate* than a recurring N-minute interrupt — once the verse is acknowledged for the day, the friction is gone.

## Pricing

- **Free tier**: app pausing, daily verse, home-screen widgets, 3 themes.
- **Premium tier** (annual subscription with free trial): remaining 12+ themes, custom photo backgrounds, lock-screen widgets, all verse categories, AI prayer features.
- No one-time / lifetime purchase. No price disclosed publicly on landing page (subscription gated at install).

## Platform

Both iOS and Android. iOS gets full feature parity (lock-screen widgets require iOS 16+); Android lacks lock-screen widget support due to platform limitations.

## Verse source

- King James Version (KJV) and World English Bible (WEB). Both **public domain** translations.
- 31,000+ offline verses bundled with app (no network required).
- 50+ verse categories: anxiety, grief, gratitude, hope, etc.
- No NIV / ESV / NLT / CSB — likely cost / licensing dodge.

## Notable features

- 15+ aesthetic themes (premium).
- Home-screen + lock-screen widgets with auto-refreshing verses.
- AI-powered prayer and reflection generator.
- Marketing claim: "no ads, tracking, or manipulation tactics" (privacy positioning).
- Built by Latvian studio **Artmvstd SIA**.

## User reception

## Notes

- **No accountability or family features.** Solo discipline only — no partner pairing, no parent dashboard.
- **No KJV/NIV/ESV bypass for memorization context** — categories are topical, not chronological/canonical.
- Brand new entrant (1.0 launch this month) so pricing/features may shift rapidly.
- Founder identity opaque — only studio brand "Artmvstd" credited.
- Differentiation gap: Psalmo's once-per-day model means a heavy scroller still gets unlimited Instagram time after a single 5-second verse acknowledgment. His Words' recurring N-minute interrupt is materially different.
