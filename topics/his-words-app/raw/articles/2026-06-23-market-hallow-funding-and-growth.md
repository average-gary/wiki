# Hallow — Catholic prayer app market profile

## Founders & founding
- Founded **December 2018** by Alex Jones (CEO), Erich Kerekes, and Alessandro DiSanto.
- HQ Chicago.

## Funding rounds (disclosed)
| Date | Round | Amount | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| April 2021 | Series A (reported) | **$12M** | Crain's Chicago Business |
| November 2021 | Series B (reported) | **$40M** | Fortune |
| **Total disclosed** | | **~$52M** | America Magazine |

Specific lead investors and post-money valuation **not disclosed** in public Wikipedia entry. Crunchbase/PitchBook would clarify but are paywalled. No confirmed Series C as of Wikipedia revision through mid-2025.

## User & growth metrics
- **Feb 2022**: 2M+ downloads (last published).
- **Feb 2024**: Hit **#1 on the Apple App Store overall** — beating ChatGPT and Google. This is a watershed moment for a religious app.
- 2024 downloads almost certainly multiples higher than 2M but Hallow no longer publishes a number.

## Pricing (current as of May 2025)
- **Annual subscription: $69.99/yr** (also referred to as "Hallow Plus").
- Monthly tier available.
- No public lifetime tier confirmed in this source (other reports suggest a higher-tier "Pro" exists; needs verification).

## Estimated revenue
- **~$51.4M/year** estimated annual income per Giles Fraser analysis in *UnHerd* (Dec 2024). At $69.99 ARPU, this implies roughly **730K paying subscribers** if estimate is accurate.
- Confidence: medium — single-source estimate, not an audited number.

## Celebrity endorsements (paid talent / partnerships)
- **Jonathan Roumie** (lead actor in *The Chosen*) — featured prayer narrator and ad face.
- **Mark Wahlberg** — partnership announced **April 2022**. Wahlberg has been the highest-profile face of the brand and personally invested.
- **Liam Neeson** — November 2023 partnership, later called a "mistake" by CEO Alex Jones (Dec 2024).
- **Russell Brand** — partnership terminated April 2025 after rape charges.

## Super Bowl marketing
- Hallow ran Super Bowl LVIII ads in Feb 2024 (with Wahlberg + Roumie). Estimated cost: $7M+ for a 30-sec slot in 2024, plus production. Direct cost not in this source — to verify with Forbes/AdAge coverage.

## Implications for "His Words"
- Catholic-only positioning has not capped TAM enough to prevent #1 ranking.
- ARPU at ~$70/yr is the de-facto "Christian premium" benchmark.
- Celebrity endorsements clearly drive download spikes but carry reputational risk (3 of 4 cited celebs caused PR issues).
- A Protestant/non-denominational equivalent has whitespace given Hallow's explicitly Catholic liturgy.
