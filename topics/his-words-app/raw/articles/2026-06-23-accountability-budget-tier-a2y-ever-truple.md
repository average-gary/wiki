# The Budget Tier — Accountable2You, Ever Accountable, Truple

## Comparative Pricing (2026)

| App | Personal Monthly | Personal Annual | Family Tier | Free Trial |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Covenant Eyes (anchor) | $18 | $198 | included (10 users) | 30-day |
| **Accountable2You** | $11 | $121 ($10.08/mo) | $16/mo or $176/yr | not specified |
| **Ever Accountable** | $14.99 | $129 ($10.75/mo) | unlimited devices/partners on personal | 14-day + 3-mo guarantee |
| **Truple** | $16 | $160 ($13.33/mo) | multi-device household | 30-day money-back |

All three undercut Covenant Eyes and target the same Christian-purity-accountability buyer.

## Accountable2You

- **Personal: $11/mo** — unlimited partners, 6 active installs.
- **Family: $16/mo** — adds parent/child accounts, time-monitoring alerts, up to 20 installs.
- Annual: 1 month free.
- Devices: Windows, Mac, Android, iOS, Chromebook, **Linux** (the only major one with Linux).
- Monitors browsing history "even in Incognito mode," app usage, device usage.
- Customizable trigger words; instant alerts via text/email.
- Christian positioning is implicit (church/school pricing tiers, "Defending Purity Book," not heavy religious branding on landing page).
- **Was also removed from Google Play Store in 2022** alongside Covenant Eyes for accessibility-API surveillance use. Status post-2022 unclear from current site.

## Ever Accountable

- **Monthly: $14.99 / Annual: $129** — single tier, simpler pricing.
- 14-day free trial, 3-month money-back guarantee.
- One reporting user per subscription, **unlimited** devices and accountability partners.
- Devices: Android, iOS, Windows, Mac, ChromeOS, Kindle.
- **Differentiation hook: ISO certification in both information security AND privacy** — claims to be "the only accountability app" with this dual certification.
- Default is **monitor-only, not block.** Optional filtering layer (free on Android/iOS) activates safe search and blocks porn/malware/spyware sites.
- Screenshot accountability + uninstall alerts (parents/partners notified if user tries to remove the app).
- Internet accountability: full app content on Android, browser-only on iOS (Apple API limit).

## Truple

- **Monthly: $16 / Annual: $160 ($13.33/mo).**
- 30-day money-back guarantee.
- **Distinguishing mechanic: random screenshot capture at 30s–5min intervals** (configurable). Captures everything on screen regardless of app.
- High-risk content flagged with red borders; all screenshots remain accessible to the partner.
- Marketed primarily to **parents of children/teens** rather than men's accountability, though it spans both.
- Concerns flagged: pornography exposure, excessive screen time, cyberbullying.
- Multi-device household pricing.

## Patterns Across the Budget Tier

1. **Price floor is ~$11/mo personal, ~$16/mo family.** Anything cheaper risks looking unserious; anything more is competing with Covenant Eyes on brand.
2. **Three monetization shapes:**
   - Single tier (Ever Accountable, Truple) — simplicity wins.
   - Personal/Family split (Accountable2You) — adds upsell ladder.
   - Lifetime + monthly + annual ladder (Covenant Eyes) — anchors high.
3. **Differentiation hooks they actually use:**
   - ISO/security certs (Ever Accountable).
   - Linux support (Accountable2You).
   - Screenshot frequency control (Truple).
   - Lifetime price (Covenant Eyes).
4. **Family tier is consistently a small premium** (~50% over personal) — not a separate product.
5. **All struggle with iOS limitations.** None can do deep monitoring on iPhone without a Bark-style hardware play. iOS gets browser-only monitoring at best. Apple's Screen Time API + Family Controls framework is the ceiling.

## Distribution

- **All three rely on DTC + church partnerships + word-of-mouth.** None have Bark's school-district flywheel; none have Covenant Eyes' 25-year incumbent brand.
- Accountable2You has school/church pricing tiers but they're not heavily promoted.
- Affiliate programs exist (typical 20–30% rev share) but are not headline differentiators.
- SEO competition is brutal — "accountability software," "porn blocker," "Christian accountability app" are all crowded keyword sets.

## Design Lessons for His Words

1. **Don't enter as a 5th budget-tier accountability app.** The space is saturated; differentiation is exhausted (Linux, ISO certs, screenshot frequency).
2. **If a family/accountability mode ships, it shouldn't try to compete on monitoring depth.** None of these apps make great products on iOS. The category is inherently constrained by Apple's APIs.
3. **The ~$15/mo price band is taken.** A His Words family mode priced as an add-on at $3–5/mo could disrupt; pricing it standalone at $15 puts it head-to-head with Ever Accountable.
4. **Watch the privacy story.** Ever Accountable explicitly leans into ISO certs because the rest of the category has trust deficits. Any His Words family mode should pre-empt this with a privacy-first design narrative.

## Status / Open Questions
