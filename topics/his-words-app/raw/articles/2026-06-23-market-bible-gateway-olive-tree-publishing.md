# Bible Gateway + Olive Tree — the publishing-house monetization model

## Bible Gateway
- Founded 1993 by Nick Hengeveld at Calvin College.
- **Acquired by Zondervan (HarperCollins subsidiary) in late 2008** from Gospel Communications International (donation-driven ministry that ran out of money — note: instructive failure mode).
- 232 Bible versions in 74 languages (as of Nov 2021).
- Alexa #744 globally — substantial traffic.
- Leadership: Joseph Park (President from 2009), Rachel Barach (GM from 2010).

### Monetization stack
- **Bible Gateway Plus** freemium membership (premium study tools).
- Bookstore: 500K+ Christian resources for sale.
- Affiliate relationship with Christianbook.com.
- Bible Gateway Deals discount program.
- **Implicit**: drives sales of Zondervan's NIV translation — vertically integrated.

## Olive Tree Bible Software
- Founded 1984 (project) / 2000 (company) by Drew Haninger. HQ Spokane, WA.
- **Acquired by HarperCollins Christian Publishing in May 2014.**
- **Spun out: September 11, 2020** — sold by HarperCollins to Gospel Technologies LLC (owned by Steven Cummings, former Olive Tree VP of Operations).
- 1,000+ Bible resources; works with Zondervan, Thomas Nelson, Baker Publishing.

### Monetization
- E-book / digital resource sales (per-resource purchase, not subscription-first).
- Multi-platform: iOS, Android, Windows, Mac.

## The translation-licensing moat
This is the single most strategic point for "His Words":

| Translation | Owner | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **NIV** (most popular modern English) | Zondervan / HarperCollins | Gates Bible Gateway, Bible.com, anyone using NIV |
| **NLT** | Tyndale House | Independent Christian publisher |
| **ESV** | Crossway | Gates Reformed/evangelical premium positioning |
| **CSB** | Lifeway / B&H | SBC-aligned |
| **NASB** | Lockman Foundation | Academic/literal segment |
| **KJV / WEB / ASV** | Public domain | Free for any app to use |

### Licensing economics
- Zondervan (NIV) typically charges per-installation, per-use, or revenue-share royalties (specific terms not public).
- Reportedly low- to mid-single-digit % of subscription revenue, OR fixed annual fees.
- For an app with $50M revenue at Hallow scale, NIV licensing alone could be $1-3M/yr (estimate).

## Why HarperCollins divested Olive Tree (2020)
- Possibly: redundant with Bible Gateway and Zondervan's direct e-book channel.
- Possibly: app distribution unprofitable at scale required.
- Possibly: 30%-cut iOS/Android economics squeezed margins below acceptability.
- Confidence: medium — actual rationale not public.

## Strategic takeaway for "His Words"
- **Decide translation strategy early.** Public-domain-only (KJV/WEB) avoids licensing but limits TAM.
- **NIV deal is the single biggest BD lever** — without it, half of US Protestants will not consider the app.
- **Avoid the Gospel Communications failure mode** — Bible Gateway's predecessor went insolvent on donation funding alone. Pure-donation model only works at YouVersion's scale or with a Hobby-Lobby-class patron.
