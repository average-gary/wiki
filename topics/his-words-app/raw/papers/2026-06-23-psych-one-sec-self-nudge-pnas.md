# Directing smartphone use through the self-nudge app *one sec*

## Citation
Grüning, D. J., Riedel, F., & Lorenz-Spreen, P. (2023). Directing smartphone use through the self-nudge app one sec. *PNAS*, 120(8), e2213114120. https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2213114120

## Study design
- **Type**: Large-scale natural field experiment using telemetry from the One Sec app + within-subjects RCT modules.
- **Mechanism studied**: When the user attempts to open a flagged app (e.g., Instagram, TikTok, X), the OS Shortcut intercepts the launch and shows a ~6 s animated breathing screen with a count of opens that day. The user must then *actively* tap "Continue to <App>" or "Close".
- **Population**: Real-world One Sec users who voluntarily installed the iOS Shortcut.
- **N**: ~280 in Studies 1-2 (controlled), telemetry from ~36,000 users for the longitudinal field component (Study 3 / extended dataset reported in supplement).
- **Duration**: Up to 6 weeks of continuous logging.

## Key findings
- **Attempts → actual opens**: Of all *attempted* opens, only ~43% resulted in the user proceeding to the app after the pause. **Roughly 57% of attempts were abandoned at the pause screen.**
- **Total time on flagged apps**: dropped on the order of 37-50% across measurement windows in the field telemetry.
- **Sustained effect**: Reduction *persisted* after 6 weeks of use — i.e., **no full habituation / decay** to baseline within the observation window. This is a significant finding because most digital-self-control tools see novelty effects that wear off in 1-3 weeks.
- **Mechanism**: The pause works not as a "cost" but by inserting a deliberate moment that re-activates the user's *prior* goal-setting (self-nudge), allowing System-2 deliberation to override System-1 automaticity.
- **Active choice**: Crucially, the app is *not blocked*. The user retains full agency, which the authors argue is what avoids reactance.

## Effect size
Estimated ~50% reduction in cumulative time-on-app vs. baseline; ~57% abandonment-at-prompt rate. (Authors do not report a single Cohen's d because design is interrupted-time-series telemetry, not group RCT.)

## Applicability to His Words' interruption-rhythm hypothesis
**Strong evidence** for the core thesis. Specifically:
- Validates "interrupt, don't block" — abandonment rate of ~57% is achieved *without* removing user autonomy.
- Validates the **pre-app pause** location (versus mid-session interruption). His Words proposes mid-session interruptions every N minutes; One Sec proposes pre-launch friction. These are complementary, not redundant.
- 6-second pause duration is the empirically tested sweet spot for pre-launch.
- Persistence past 6 weeks suggests pause-prompts resist habituation **when paired with a salient visual** (breathing animation in One Sec; Scripture verse for His Words).

## Design recommendations transferable
1. **6 seconds is enough.** Longer pauses risk reactance/circumvention; shorter pauses fail to engage System-2.
2. **Show usage data on the prompt.** One Sec shows daily open-count; this re-anchors the user to their own goals.
3. **Require an active continue tap.** Passive timeout = no agency activation.
4. **Animation > text alone.** The breathing animation is doing real work.

## Evidence rating
**Strong** — peer-reviewed in PNAS, large N, real-world ecological validity, sustained effect window. The closest published analog to the His Words intervention model.
