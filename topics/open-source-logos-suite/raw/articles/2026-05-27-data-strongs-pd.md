# Strong's Concordance (Public Domain)

## Key findings
- Published 1890 by James Strong; PD across all jurisdictions.
- ~8,674 Hebrew/Aramaic root entries (H-numbers); ~5,624 Greek (G-numbers).
- The Strong's number is the **canonical biblical-search ID** — every major Bible app keys lookups by H#### / G####.
- Bound originally to KJV concordance, but the H/G numbers themselves work across translations.
- Digital text on Wikisource, OpenScriptures, Blue Letter Bible, e-sword modules.
- Definitions are short and dated — pair with BDB (Hebrew) and Thayer (Greek) for richer entries; pair with TBESH/TBESG (STEPBible) for modern brief glosses.

## Notable quotes / specifics
- Wikipedia: "Editions and reprints of Strong's Concordance are now in the public domain."
- Strong's numbers were extended later (e.g., Goodrick-Kohlenberger numbers for NIV); G/K is **not** public domain — beware.

## Source notes
- Hard requirement for any user-facing lexicon UX: Strong's numbers are how laypeople do "what does the Greek word here mean?"
- Free; ship without attribution legally required (though crediting James Strong is good form).
- Watch out for "Enhanced Strong's" or "Updated Strong's" — those are frequently copyrighted derivatives.
