The American Bible Society's scripture.api.bible service, the largest aggregated Bible API. Claims "nearly 2,500 text and audio Bible versions in 1,600+ languages." Free tier exists for non-commercial use. Critical caveat: "some Bibles require a Unique License agreement with the IP holder" — i.e. the public free key gets you a wide selection of public-domain and openly-licensed translations (KJV, BSB, WEB, ASV, etc.) but not ESV, NIV, NASB, CSB, or NLT. Those copyrighted majors are listed in the catalog under unique-license gating; obtaining them requires direct negotiation with the IP holder (Crossway, Biblica, Lockman, Holman, Tyndale respectively). NIV and KJV are explicitly named on landing pages as supported, suggesting Biblica (NIV) does grant unique licenses to API.Bible partners on a case-by-case basis. ABS does not publish a self-serve path to copyrighted modern Bibles.
