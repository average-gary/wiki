Pulpit AI is sermon-ops SaaS for pastors: upload a sermon recording or transcript, get back video clips with captions, group/discussion guides, daily devotionals, blog posts, newsletters, and a "sermon assistant" that drafts openers, metaphors, and applications. The corpus is implicitly the pastor's own sermon content (closed-corpus repurposing), but the homepage makes no explicit claim about retrieval scope, training data isolation, citation, or hallucination prevention. There is no technical disclosure of model, RAG architecture, or grounding strategy. The product is positioned as content marketing/multiplication, not theological assistance — a different niche from Logos AI but illustrative of how Christian-AI startups can succeed without publishing any architecture.
