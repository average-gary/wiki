# Logos pricing tiers — subscription shift (logos.com/compare)

## Key findings

- **Three subscription tiers, all with 30-day free trial**:
  - **Premium**: $9.99/month or $99.99/year ($8.33/month equivalent). "Great for small group leaders & in-depth study."
  - **Pro**: $14.99/month or $149.99/year ($12.50/month equivalent). "Perfect for pastors & sermon prep."
  - **Max**: $19.99/month or $199.99/year ($16.67/month equivalent). "Ideal for language study & research."

- **Feature gating** (only what the compare page makes explicit):
  - Premium: instant verse details, curated library search, group discussion question generator.
  - Pro: + sermon planning and prep tools, + Greek and Hebrew tools.
  - Max: + advanced original language tools, + grammar and syntax analysis, + academic writing support.

- **Library count not disclosed per tier** on compare page. This is intentional — Logos has decoupled "tools access" (subscription) from "library ownership" (still purchased separately as books or via legacy base packages).

- **Strategic implication**: Subscription pricing maxes out at ~$200/year. Compare to legacy base packages where Diamond was ~$3,000 and Portfolio was ~$5,000+ as one-time purchases. This is a deliberate pivot from selling 50–80,000-resource libraries up-front to selling tool access + AI as recurring revenue. The library now functions as a moat-via-prior-purchase: existing Logos users have $1,000s sunk into proprietary-format books they cannot easily port out.

- **Lock-in mechanism**: Legacy customers' purchased libraries remain accessible to them. New customers buy subscriptions but can also still purchase individual books / bundles outside the subscription. The subscription includes access to a "curated library" that is presumably a Faithlife-licensed reading set, separate from the buy-to-own catalog.

## Source notes

Primary source — Logos's own /compare page accessed 2026-05-27. Highest-confidence pricing data. The compare page deliberately omits the legacy package tiers (Starter, Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, Diamond, Portfolio); to find those a researcher must dig into base package marketing or third-party sales aggregators. The named subscription tiers (Premium/Pro/Max) are the current consumer-facing product. For a clone project: this is the modern pricing target to undercut. A self-hosted open-source equivalent at $0 + book BYO would be ~10–20× cheaper TCO over five years even before counting purchased books.
