# Logos Bible Software (Wikipedia)

## Key findings

- **Origin**: Founded 1992 by Bob Pritchett, Kiernon Reiniger, and Dale Pritchett; Bob and Kiernon were ex-Microsoft. The trio "quit their jobs to develop Christian software."
- **Acquisition root**: In 1995, Logos acquired data from the **CDWordLibrary** project at Dallas Theological Seminary and built the Logos Library System (LLS) on top. This is the seed of the proprietary library format.
- **Rebrand**: Renamed to **Faithlife Corporation** in October 2014 to broaden its identity beyond a single product. By October 2022 the company refocused on Bible study + digital discipleship, exiting church management.
- **Ownership**: Currently a portfolio company of **Cove Hill Partners** (private equity). CEO is Bill McCarthy.
- **Tech stack**: C# and C++ (desktop app), with iOS (since November 2009 with Logos 4) and Android (since 2012, expanded August 2012) clients.
- **Platforms**: Windows, macOS, iOS, iPadOS, Android. No Linux client.
- **Library scale**: "Over 120,000 Christian ebooks" via partnerships with **more than 500 publishers** — described as the largest catalog among comparable Bible software products. (Logos's own marketing claims 250,000+ resources & courses; this gap likely reflects courses, journals, and tagged datasets vs strict ebook count.)
- **Core feature set listed**: digital library application with eBook functionality, resource linking, note-taking, linguistic analysis for original languages, split-screen viewing, highlighting, reading plans, **Bible Word Study**, **Passage Guide**, and resource databases.
- **Variants/SKUs**:
  - **Verbum** — Catholic edition with Catholic databases and Deuterocanonical content. Same engine, different curation + UI tone.
  - **Noet** (2014–2020, discontinued) — humanities/classics/philosophy variant. Notable as a discontinued attempt at horizontal expansion.
- **Imprints / brand stack**: Word by Word (blog, 2004), What in the Word? (video/podcast), Logos Live (interview show), **Lexham Press** (in-house imprint, sold to Baker Publishing Group on 2025-09-23).
- **Headquarters**: Bellingham, Washington. ~320 employees as of 2016 (probably larger now).
- **Reception**: Praised for resource quantity and unique tools; criticized for **high cost** and **lack of speed** compared to competitors (recurrent complaint in user communities — important moat-vulnerability signal).
