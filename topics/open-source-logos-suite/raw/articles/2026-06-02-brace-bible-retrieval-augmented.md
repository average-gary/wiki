BRACE describes itself as "a bespoke commentary generator" that "aims to provide insights & answers solely based on the Biblical text," using the RSVCE translation. The README references "Corrective Retrieval-Augmented Generation" and the maintainer notes self-hosting "permissively-licensed AI model(s)" on personal hardware (i.e., not OpenAI/Anthropic APIs). Cited verses come directly from RSVCE. The maintainer explicitly warns "the underlying AI can misinterpret text" and asks users to verify references — an honest hallucination disclaimer rather than a prevention guarantee. Python `app.py` based, hosted at brace.cdcl.ml. License not stated in the visible README. Activity is modest (~40 commits, 3 stars). Useful as a reference architecture: small, single-developer, locally hostable, citation-leaning, but no formal "refuse on no-source" mode.
