# Andersen-Forbes Hebrew database

The Andersen-Forbes phrase-marker analysis is a complete syntactic annotation of the Hebrew Bible by Francis I. Andersen and A. Dean Forbes, distributed via Logos as a paid resource and not openly licensed. The site notes ongoing work toward "an error-correction/enhancement release followed by a release realizing full representation," but no open-data release. For christ-is-lord this means Andersen-Forbes is unavailable as a source; the open Hebrew syntactic option is the Clear-Bible / Groves Center MACULA Hebrew tree (built on the Westminster Hebrew Syntax now released CC BY 4.0). Documentation of the Andersen-Forbes phrase-marker scheme is still useful as a comparison point — its phrase-marker idea (every constituent gets an explicit label and grammatical-relation tag) is conceptually compatible with the lowfat `class`/`role` attributes and with the kinds of canonical queries Logos users expect. christ-is-lord cannot replicate Andersen-Forbes-specific queries verbatim but can express most equivalents over MACULA's lowfat trees.
