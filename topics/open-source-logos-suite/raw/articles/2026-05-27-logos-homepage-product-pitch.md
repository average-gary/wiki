# Logos.com homepage — current product framing

## Key findings

- **Tagline / positioning**: "The #1 Bible app for transformative study, preaching, and teaching." Logos pitches itself as a *platform*, not a product — combining "powerful research tools with curated biblical content."
- **Headline tools shown on the homepage** (these are the features Logos itself thinks are the differentiators worth marketing):
  - **AI-powered research assistant** — 24/7 answers to biblical inquiries. This is a recent addition, likely 2024-2025, and signals Faithlife's strategic bet that AI is now part of the moat (or part of the threat — they may be racing to own the chat-with-Bible UX before a generic LLM wrapper does).
  - **Passage Guide** — "Explore themes, concepts, and historical events across Scripture." Aggregates commentary, cross-references, illustrations, sermons, and media for any verse/passage.
  - **Greek/Hebrew word study tools** — deep linguistic analysis.
  - **Sermon Builder** — pre-made and customizable templates for sermon prep.
  - **Search functionality** — passages, figures, theological concepts.
  - **Counseling resource access** — biblical counseling content integrated into library context.
- **Library claim**: **250,000+ Christian books and courses**, "fully searchable, curated for [users'] needs." This is the marketing number and is larger than the Wikipedia "120,000 ebooks" — discrepancy likely covers Mobile Ed courses, journal articles, sermon archives, and tagged datasets. Sample resources called out: Matthew Henry's Commentary, ESV Study Bible, topical devotionals.
- **Platforms**: iOS, Android, web ("anywhere access"). Desktop apps still exist but are less prominently marketed at the consumer landing — strategic shift toward mobile-first / web-first.
- **User base claim**: ~**6 million Scripture students globally**. Whether this is registered accounts, MAU, or lifetime is unclear (vendor metric).
- **Pricing as marketed on homepage**: 30-day free trial, "as little as 27 cents a day" (≈ $98.55/year, lining up with the $99.99/yr Premium tier from the compare page).

## Pricing (from /compare)

Logos has shifted from a perpetual-license-with-base-package model to a **subscription model** circa 2024-2025. Three tiers:

| Tier | Monthly | Annual | Persona |
|------|---------|--------|---------|
| **Premium** | $9.99/mo | $99.99/yr ($8.33/mo equiv) | "Small group leaders & in-depth study" |
| **Pro** | $14.99/mo | $149.99/yr ($12.50/mo equiv) | "Pastors & sermon prep" |
| **Max** | $19.99/mo | $199.99/yr ($16.67/mo equiv) | "Language study & research" |

All tiers include 30-day free trial. Tier feature gating:
- **Premium**: per-verse details, curated library search, group discussion question generator.
- **Pro**: adds sermon planning/prep, Greek and Hebrew tools.
- **Max**: adds advanced original-language tools, grammar/syntax analysis, academic writing support (likely where syntactic search & full Cascadia integration live).

**Important**: This subscription model is a *break* from the historical Logos pricing (perpetual base packages: Bronze ~$300, Silver ~$650, Gold ~$1,550, Platinum ~$2,150, Diamond ~$3,000, Portfolio ~$5,000+). The classic tier names are no longer prominent on the live page but the legacy library-purchase model still exists alongside the subscription. Users who bought legacy packages keep their books.

## Source notes

Primary source — Logos's own marketing copy. Use for: feature names, current SKU lineup, current pricing, user-base claims. Distrust for: actual library size (vendor figure), genuine differentiation vs competitors (puffery), 6M user count (definition unspecified). The shift to a 3-tier subscription (Premium/Pro/Max) versus the historical Bronze→Portfolio ladder is the single most important piece of competitive intelligence here for a clone project — Logos is monetizing the *tools* now, not just the *books*.
