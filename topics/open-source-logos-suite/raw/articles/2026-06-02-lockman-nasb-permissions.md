Lockman Foundation's permissions page, which is the public-facing terms surface for NASB use; Lockman does not publish a public NASB API. Standard quote allowance: up to 1,000 verses without written permission, but with two hard limits — quoted material cannot constitute a complete book of the Bible, and cannot exceed 50% of the total work. Storage limit: "may not" store more than 1,000 verses in an electronic retrieval system. Unique 2024-2026 addition: an explicit AI-usage clause permitting AI systems to quote up to 1,000 verses in a single response (covering the full response if needed) with required NASB-edition tag and a lockman.org link for responses over 10 verses. API-style access exists only via direct Lockman Foundation negotiation; one developer reports that Lockman "gave one to me, for my own API," and one was granted to American Bible Society — i.e. discretionary case-by-case licensing, not a self-serve developer portal.
