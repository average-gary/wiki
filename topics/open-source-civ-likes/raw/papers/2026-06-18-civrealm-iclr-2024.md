# CivRealm: A Learning and Reasoning Odyssey in Civilization for Decision-Making Agents

Peer-reviewed at ICLR 2024 by the Beijing Institute for General Artificial
Intelligence (BIGAI) and collaborators. Establishes Freeciv-web (and the
underlying GPL Freeciv C server) as the canonical open-source 4X testbed for
modern decision-making AI research.

## Key claims

- **Open-source civ-likes are research-grade**: The paper builds directly on
  Freeciv-web/Freeciv, citing its imperfect-information, general-sum,
  variable-player-count properties as uniquely valuable vs. Atari/StarCraft.
- **Dual interface**: Provides Gymnasium-style tensor API for RL agents AND a
  language interface for LLM agents — means a single open-source civ-like can
  serve two distinct AI research communities.
- **Diplomacy and negotiation are first-class**: framing emphasizes social /
  language reasoning, not just optimal play.
- **Open challenge**: Both RL agents and LLM agents reach reasonable
  performance on mini-games but **struggle on full games** — the full
  civilization game remains unsolved at ICLR 2024 publication.
- **Stack**: GPL-3.0; depends on freeciv-web, FCIV-NET, freeciv-bot.

## Why this matters

Bridges open-source civ-game world to top-tier ML research. Any wiki article
on Freeciv's role outside hobby gaming must cite this. Validates that
Freeciv's age (1996+) and coverage are not handicaps but rather *advantages*
for reproducibility.

## Notable counterpoint

A pre-LLM-era result by Voss et al. (2019) reported that knowledge-based RL
won full Freeciv games against the built-in AI. The methodological frames
differ — CivRealm targets self-play / multi-agent, Voss targets win-vs-fixed-AI
— and the contrast is itself worth flagging in the wiki.
