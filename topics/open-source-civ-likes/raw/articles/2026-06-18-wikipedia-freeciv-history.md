# Wikipedia — Freeciv

Encyclopedia summary covering Freeciv's history, balance issues, and
fork landscape.

## Structural issues documented

- **City smallpox balance failure**: experienced players found that
  sprawling many small cities was strictly optimal, making developing
  individual cities pointless. Required a structural rebalance in **v2.0
  (2005)** — i.e. **5+ years of broken balance** before fix.
- **Multiplayer wasn't an original design target**; community team-play
  feature had to be retro-fitted via fork c. 2002.
- **Original play.freeciv.org server was shut down in March 2018** and only
  later "revived by volunteers" — multi-month outage of the canonical
  multiplayer host.

## Fork sprawl (named)

Four parallel implementations with overlapping scope:
- **Freeciv** (canonical, GTK/SDL2/Qt, GPL-2.0+) — Freeciv 3.2.4 released
  March 2026, **upstream still shipping**
- **Freeciv21** (Qt fork, Longturn-focused, GPL-3.0+)
- **Freeciv-web** (browser fork, AGPL-3.0+, with PBEM)
- **Fciv.net / freecivx.net** (3D experiments)

## Game scope

- Up to **126 players**
- **33 languages**
- Hex / iso / overhead displays
- Longturn community = day-long-turn variant, **20–30 players per match**

## Why this matters

Establishes the fork taxonomy and that **upstream Freeciv is not abandoned**
— important framing context, since a casual reader might assume Freeciv21
superseded it. Also documents the smallpox / multiplayer / server-outage
issues that motivated forks.
