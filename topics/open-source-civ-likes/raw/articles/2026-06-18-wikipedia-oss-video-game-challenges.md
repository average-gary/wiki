# Wikipedia — Open-source video game (challenges section)

General-purpose framing source for the macro critique of OSS games.

## Structural critiques

- OSS games *"often take longer to mature, are less common and often lack
  the production value of commercial titles."*
- **Art bottleneck** named explicitly: *"music and art development is not
  built up from the work of others in the same way that coding would be."*
  — i.e. open-source's compounding advantage doesn't apply to assets.
- **Community retention failure**: cites Adam Geitgey's 2004 analysis that
  gamers *"move on to new games relatively quickly and so do not give back
  to the project"* — undermines the OSS feedback loop for games specifically.
- **"Reinventing the wheel"** called out as a *"notable problem"* — exactly
  the Freeciv / Freeciv21 / Fciv.net / freecivx.net pattern.

## Why this matters

General-purpose framing source for the macro critique (art bottleneck,
churn, fragmentation) that explains why specific projects in the
civ-like genre keep hitting the same walls.
